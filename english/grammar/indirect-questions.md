# Preguntas Indirectas (Indirect Questions)

## Que son

Las preguntas indirectas son preguntas "envueltas" en frases mas educadas o formales. En espanol apenas cambia la estructura, pero en ingles si: **el orden cambia a sujeto + verbo** (como una afirmacion).

| Pregunta directa | Pregunta indirecta |
|-------------------|--------------------|
| Where is the bank? | Could you tell me **where the bank is**? |
| What time does it start? | Do you know **what time it starts**? |
| Are you coming? | I was wondering **if you are coming**. |

> **La regla clave:** En la pregunta indirecta, el orden es **sujeto + verbo** (NO verbo + sujeto). No hay inversion.

---

## Estructuras de introduccion

| Formal/Educado | Ejemplo |
|----------------|---------|
| Could you tell me... | Could you tell me where the station is? |
| Do you know... | Do you know what time it opens? |
| Can you tell me... | Can you tell me how to get there? |
| I'd like to know... | I'd like to know when the meeting starts. |
| I was wondering... | I was wondering if you could help me. |
| Would you mind telling me... | Would you mind telling me where you live? |
| Do you have any idea... | Do you have any idea how much it costs? |

---

## Con WH- words (who, what, where, when, why, how)

La palabra WH- se mantiene, pero el orden cambia:

| Directa (verbo + sujeto) | Indirecta (sujeto + verbo) |
|---------------------------|----------------------------|
| Where **does** she **live**? | Do you know where she **lives**? |
| What **did** he **say**? | Can you tell me what he **said**? |
| When **will** they **arrive**? | Do you know when they **will arrive**? |
| Why **was** she **angry**? | I'd like to know why she **was** angry. |
| How much **does** it **cost**? | Could you tell me how much it **costs**? |
| Who **did** you **call**? | I was wondering who you **called**. |

### Ejemplos

1. **Do you know where I can buy tickets?** (¿Sabes donde puedo comprar entradas?)
2. **Could you tell me what time the train leaves?** (¿Podrias decirme a que hora sale el tren?)
3. **I'd like to know why the flight was cancelled.** (Me gustaria saber por que se cancelo el vuelo.)
4. **Do you have any idea how long it takes?** (¿Tienes idea de cuanto tarda?)
5. **Can you tell me who is in charge?** (¿Puedes decirme quien esta a cargo?)

---

## Sin WH- word (preguntas de si/no)

Cuando la pregunta original es de si o no, usamos **if** o **whether**:

| Directa | Indirecta |
|---------|-----------|
| Is she coming? | Do you know **if** she is coming? |
| Did he pass the exam? | I was wondering **whether** he passed the exam. |
| Can you drive? | Could you tell me **if** you can drive? |
| Will it rain? | Do you know **if** it will rain? |
| Has she arrived? | I'd like to know **whether** she has arrived. |

### Ejemplos

1. **Do you know if the shop is open?** (¿Sabes si la tienda esta abierta?)
2. **Could you tell me whether this seat is taken?** (¿Podrias decirme si este asiento esta ocupado?)
3. **I was wondering if you could lend me your phone.** (Me preguntaba si podrias prestarme tu telefono.)
4. **Do you have any idea if they serve food here?** (¿Tienes idea de si sirven comida aqui?)
5. **I'd like to know whether the price includes breakfast.** (Me gustaria saber si el precio incluye desayuno.)

---

## Embedded questions (preguntas dentro de afirmaciones)

A veces la pregunta indirecta no es realmente una pregunta, sino una afirmacion:

| Tipo | Ejemplo |
|------|---------|
| Afirmacion | I don't know **where she lives**. |
| Afirmacion | I can't remember **what his name is**. |
| Afirmacion | Nobody knows **when it will end**. |
| Afirmacion | I have no idea **how old she is**. |
| Afirmacion | It depends on **whether you agree**. |

> **Nota:** Estas NO llevan signo de interrogacion porque no son preguntas.

---

## Errores comunes de hispanohablantes

| Incorrecto | Correcto | Por que |
|------------|----------|---------|
| Do you know where **is** the bank? | Do you know where the bank **is**? | No inversion en indirectas |
| Can you tell me what **does** it mean? | Can you tell me what it **means**? | Sin auxiliar DO/DOES/DID |
| I wonder where **did** he go. | I wonder where he **went**. | Sin inversion ni auxiliar |
| Could you tell me **that** she is here? | Could you tell me **if** she is here? | If/whether para si/no, no that |
| I don't know **what is** his name. | I don't know **what his name is**. | Sujeto antes del verbo |

> **Consejo practico:** Si puedes convertir la pregunta indirecta en una afirmacion (quitando "Do you know..."), el orden debe sonar natural como afirmacion: "The bank is on Main Street" → "Do you know where the bank is?" ✓
