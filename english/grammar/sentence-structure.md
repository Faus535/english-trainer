# Estructura de Oraciones en Ingles

## 1. Orden SVO (Sujeto - Verbo - Objeto)

La regla MAS importante del ingles. A diferencia del espanol, el ingles tiene un orden de palabras muy rigido.

En espanol podemos decir "El libro lo compro Maria" o "Maria compro el libro". En ingles, el orden casi siempre es **Subject + Verb + Object**.

| Sujeto (S) | Verbo (V) | Objeto (O) |
|-------------|-----------|------------|
| I | eat | breakfast |
| She | reads | books |
| They | play | soccer |
| The dog | chased | the cat |
| My mother | cooks | dinner |

### Ejemplos

1. **I love chocolate.** (Yo amo el chocolate.)
2. **She speaks three languages.** (Ella habla tres idiomas.)
3. **They bought a new car.** (Ellos compraron un coche nuevo.)
4. **The teacher explained the lesson.** (El profesor explico la leccion.)
5. **We visited our grandparents.** (Visitamos a nuestros abuelos.)
6. **He drinks coffee every morning.** (El toma cafe cada manana.)
7. **My sister studies medicine.** (Mi hermana estudia medicina.)
8. **The children broke the window.** (Los ninos rompieron la ventana.)
9. **She wrote a beautiful poem.** (Ella escribio un hermoso poema.)
10. **We need more time.** (Necesitamos mas tiempo.)

> **Error comun de hispanohablantes:** Decir "Is very important the education" en lugar de "Education is very important." En ingles el sujeto SIEMPRE va antes del verbo en oraciones afirmativas.

## 2. Preguntas: Auxiliar + Sujeto + Verbo

Para formar preguntas en ingles, se invierte el orden del auxiliar y el sujeto. Esto es muy diferente al espanol, donde simplemente cambiamos la entonacion.

### Estructura general

**Auxiliar + Sujeto + Verbo principal + Complemento?**

### Con DO/DOES/DID (presente y pasado simple)

| Pregunta | Traduccion |
|----------|------------|
| **Do** you like pizza? | Te gusta la pizza? |
| **Does** she work here? | Ella trabaja aqui? |
| **Did** they come yesterday? | Vinieron ayer? |
| **Do** we need anything? | Necesitamos algo? |
| **Does** he speak Spanish? | El habla espanol? |

### Con BE (ser/estar)

| Pregunta | Traduccion |
|----------|------------|
| **Is** she a doctor? | Es ella doctora? |
| **Are** you tired? | Estas cansado? |
| **Was** he at home? | Estaba el en casa? |
| **Were** they happy? | Estaban contentos? |
| **Is** it raining? | Esta lloviendo? |

### Con HAVE (tiempos perfectos)

| Pregunta | Traduccion |
|----------|------------|
| **Have** you finished? | Has terminado? |
| **Has** she been to Paris? | Ha estado ella en Paris? |
| **Have** they eaten yet? | Han comido ya? |
| **Had** you seen it before? | Lo habias visto antes? |

### Con modales

| Pregunta | Traduccion |
|----------|------------|
| **Can** you help me? | Puedes ayudarme? |
| **Will** they come? | Vendran? |
| **Should** I call her? | Deberia llamarla? |
| **Would** you like some tea? | Te gustaria un poco de te? |
| **Must** we leave now? | Debemos irnos ahora? |

### Preguntas con WH- (question words)

La palabra interrogativa va ANTES del auxiliar:

**WH- + Auxiliar + Sujeto + Verbo?**

1. **What do you want?** (Que quieres?)
2. **Where does she live?** (Donde vive ella?)
3. **When did they arrive?** (Cuando llegaron?)
4. **Why are you crying?** (Por que estas llorando?)
5. **How does this work?** (Como funciona esto?)
6. **Who did you call?** (A quien llamaste?)
7. **Which one do you prefer?** (Cual prefieres?)
8. **How many books do you have?** (Cuantos libros tienes?)
9. **How much does it cost?** (Cuanto cuesta?)
10. **How often do you exercise?** (Con que frecuencia haces ejercicio?)

> **Excepcion importante:** Cuando WHO o WHAT son el sujeto, NO se usa auxiliar:
> - **Who called you?** (Quien te llamo?) - "Who" es el sujeto
> - **What happened?** (Que paso?) - "What" es el sujeto

## 3. Oraciones Negativas: Sujeto + Auxiliar + NOT + Verbo

### Estructura

**Sujeto + Auxiliar + not + Verbo principal + Complemento**

### Con DO/DOES/DID

| Afirmativa | Negativa |
|------------|----------|
| I like fish. | I **do not (don't)** like fish. |
| She works here. | She **does not (doesn't)** work here. |
| They came yesterday. | They **did not (didn't)** come yesterday. |
| We need help. | We **do not (don't)** need help. |
| He plays guitar. | He **does not (doesn't)** play guitar. |

### Con BE

| Afirmativa | Negativa |
|------------|----------|
| I am happy. | I **am not (I'm not)** happy. |
| She is a teacher. | She **is not (isn't)** a teacher. |
| They are ready. | They **are not (aren't)** ready. |
| He was tired. | He **was not (wasn't)** tired. |
| We were late. | We **were not (weren't)** late. |

### Con HAVE

| Afirmativa | Negativa |
|------------|----------|
| I have finished. | I **have not (haven't)** finished. |
| She has seen it. | She **has not (hasn't)** seen it. |
| They had left. | They **had not (hadn't)** left. |

### Con modales

| Afirmativa | Negativa |
|------------|----------|
| I can swim. | I **cannot (can't)** swim. |
| She will come. | She **will not (won't)** come. |
| You should go. | You **should not (shouldn't)** go. |
| He must leave. | He **must not (mustn't)** leave. |

## 4. Orden de los Adjetivos

Cuando usamos varios adjetivos antes de un sustantivo, siguen un orden especifico. En espanol, los adjetivos suelen ir despues del sustantivo, pero en ingles van ANTES y en este orden:

### OSASCOMP

| Orden | Categoria | Ejemplo |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| 1 | **O**pinion (opinion) | beautiful, ugly, nice, horrible |
| 2 | **S**ize (tamano) | big, small, tiny, huge |
| 3 | **A**ge (edad) | old, young, new, ancient |
| 4 | **S**hape (forma) | round, square, flat, long |
| 5 | **C**olor (color) | red, blue, green, dark |
| 6 | **O**rigin (origen) | Spanish, American, Chinese |
| 7 | **M**aterial (material) | wooden, metal, cotton, leather |
| 8 | **P**urpose (proposito) | sleeping (bag), running (shoes) |

### Ejemplos

1. **A beautiful small old round red Italian leather handbag.**
   (Un hermoso bolso pequeno viejo redondo rojo italiano de cuero.)

2. **A nice big new house.** (Una bonita casa grande nueva.)

3. **An ugly old square brown wooden table.** (Una fea mesa vieja cuadrada marron de madera.)

4. **A lovely little young black French cat.** (Un adorable gatito joven negro frances.)

5. **Two beautiful large ancient round Greek marble columns.** (Dos hermosas columnas grandes antiguas redondas griegas de marmol.)

> **Consejo practico:** Rara vez usamos mas de 2-3 adjetivos seguidos. Si necesitas mas, considera reestructurar la oracion.

## 5. Posicion de los Adverbios

La posicion del adverbio depende de su tipo.

### Adverbios de frecuencia: ANTES del verbo principal, DESPUES de BE

| Correcto | Incorrecto |
|----------|------------|
| I **always** wake up early. | I wake up **always** early. |
| She **never** eats meat. | She eats **never** meat. |
| They **usually** go by bus. | They go **usually** by bus. |
| He **is always** late. | He **always is** late. |
| We **are often** busy. | We **often are** busy. |

**Lista de adverbios de frecuencia (de mayor a menor):**
always (100%) > usually (80%) > often (70%) > sometimes (50%) > occasionally (30%) > rarely/seldom (10%) > never (0%)

### Adverbios de modo (HOW): generalmente al FINAL

1. **She sings beautifully.** (Ella canta hermosamente.)
2. **He drives carefully.** (El conduce cuidadosamente.)
3. **They worked hard.** (Trabajaron duro.)
4. **She spoke quietly.** (Ella hablo en voz baja.)
5. **The children played happily.** (Los ninos jugaron felizmente.)

### Adverbios de lugar (WHERE): al FINAL, antes de los de tiempo

1. **I'll meet you there.** (Te vere alli.)
2. **She went upstairs.** (Ella subio arriba.)
3. **They live nearby.** (Ellos viven cerca.)

### Adverbios de tiempo (WHEN): al FINAL o al PRINCIPIO

1. **I saw her yesterday.** (La vi ayer.)
2. **Yesterday, I saw her.** (Ayer, la vi.)
3. **We'll finish tomorrow.** (Terminaremos manana.)
4. **She's arriving soon.** (Ella llega pronto.)
5. **I've already eaten.** (Ya he comido.) - "already" va antes del participio

### Orden cuando hay varios adverbios: MODO + LUGAR + TIEMPO

1. **She sang beautifully at the concert last night.**
   (Ella canto hermosamente en el concierto anoche.)

2. **He worked hard in the office all day.**
   (El trabajo duro en la oficina todo el dia.)

3. **They played happily in the park yesterday.**
   (Jugaron felizmente en el parque ayer.)

## 6. There is / There are

Se usa para indicar la existencia o presencia de algo. Equivale al espanol "hay".

### Estructura

| Forma | Singular | Plural |
|-------|----------|--------|
| Afirmativa | **There is** (There's) a book. | **There are** some books. |
| Negativa | **There is not** (There isn't) a book. | **There are not** (There aren't) any books. |
| Pregunta | **Is there** a book? | **Are there** any books? |

### Ejemplos

1. **There is a park near my house.** (Hay un parque cerca de mi casa.)
2. **There are three bedrooms in the apartment.** (Hay tres habitaciones en el apartamento.)
3. **There isn't any milk in the fridge.** (No hay leche en la nevera.)
4. **There aren't enough chairs for everyone.** (No hay suficientes sillas para todos.)
5. **Is there a pharmacy nearby?** (Hay una farmacia cerca?)
6. **Are there any questions?** (Hay alguna pregunta?)
7. **There was an accident on the highway.** (Hubo un accidente en la autopista.)
8. **There were many people at the party.** (Habia muchas personas en la fiesta.)
9. **There will be a meeting tomorrow.** (Habra una reunion manana.)
10. **There have been several complaints.** (Ha habido varias quejas.)

> **Error comun de hispanohablantes:** Decir "Have many people" en lugar de "There are many people." En ingles SIEMPRE necesitas "there" para expresar existencia.

## 7. IT como Sujeto Impersonal (Dummy Subject)

En ingles, toda oracion necesita un sujeto. Cuando no hay un sujeto logico, usamos **"it"** como sujeto vacio. En espanol muchas veces no usamos sujeto explicito.

### Para el clima

1. **It's raining.** (Esta lloviendo.)
2. **It's snowing.** (Esta nevando.)
3. **It's cold today.** (Hace frio hoy.)
4. **It's hot and humid.** (Hace calor y humedad.)
5. **It was sunny yesterday.** (Ayer estuvo soleado.)

### Para la hora y la fecha

1. **It's 3 o'clock.** (Son las 3.)
2. **It's Monday.** (Es lunes.)
3. **It's January 15th.** (Es 15 de enero.)
4. **It's early.** (Es temprano.)
5. **It's getting late.** (Se esta haciendo tarde.)

### Para distancias y tiempo

1. **It's 5 miles from here.** (Esta a 5 millas de aqui.)
2. **It takes two hours to get there.** (Se tarda dos horas en llegar.)
3. **It's a long way to the station.** (Hay un largo camino hasta la estacion.)

### Con adjetivos + infinitivo/gerundio

1. **It's important to study every day.** (Es importante estudiar cada dia.)
2. **It's difficult to learn a new language.** (Es dificil aprender un nuevo idioma.)
3. **It's nice to meet you.** (Es un placer conocerte.)
4. **It's no use crying.** (No sirve de nada llorar.)
5. **It's worth visiting that museum.** (Vale la pena visitar ese museo.)
6. **It seems that she is upset.** (Parece que ella esta molesta.)
7. **It appears that we were wrong.** (Parece que estabamos equivocados.)
8. **It doesn't matter.** (No importa.)

> **Error comun de hispanohablantes:** Omitir "it" y decir "Is important to study" en lugar de "It is important to study."

## 8. Patrones Comunes de Oraciones

### Patron 1: Sujeto + Verbo (intransitivo)

1. **The baby cried.** (El bebe lloro.)
2. **The sun rises.** (El sol sale.)
3. **She laughed.** (Ella rio.)
4. **Birds fly.** (Los pajaros vuelan.)
5. **He disappeared.** (El desaparecio.)

### Patron 2: Sujeto + Verbo + Objeto Directo

1. **I read the newspaper.** (Yo leo el periodico.)
2. **She cooked dinner.** (Ella preparo la cena.)
3. **They built a house.** (Ellos construyeron una casa.)
4. **We watched a movie.** (Vimos una pelicula.)
5. **He fixed the car.** (El arreglo el coche.)

### Patron 3: Sujeto + Verbo + Objeto Indirecto + Objeto Directo

1. **She gave me a present.** (Ella me dio un regalo.)
2. **He told us a story.** (El nos conto una historia.)
3. **I sent her a letter.** (Le envie una carta a ella.)
4. **They offered him the job.** (Le ofrecieron el trabajo.)
5. **She showed me her photos.** (Ella me mostro sus fotos.)

### Patron 4: Sujeto + Verbo + Complemento (linking verbs)

1. **She is a doctor.** (Ella es doctora.)
2. **The food smells delicious.** (La comida huele deliciosa.)
3. **He became famous.** (El se hizo famoso.)
4. **They seem happy.** (Ellos parecen felices.)
5. **The milk tastes sour.** (La leche sabe agria.)

### Patron 5: Sujeto + Verbo + Objeto + Complemento del objeto

1. **They elected him president.** (Lo eligieron presidente.)
2. **She painted the wall blue.** (Ella pinto la pared de azul.)
3. **The news made her sad.** (La noticia la puso triste.)
4. **I consider him a friend.** (Lo considero un amigo.)
5. **They named the baby Emma.** (Llamaron a la bebe Emma.)

### Patron 6: Oraciones con clausulas subordinadas

1. **I think that she is right.** (Creo que ella tiene razon.)
2. **He said that he would come.** (El dijo que vendria.)
3. **I know where she lives.** (Se donde vive ella.)
4. **She asked if I was coming.** (Ella pregunto si yo iba a venir.)
5. **We don't know when they will arrive.** (No sabemos cuando llegaran.)

### Patron 7: Oraciones pasivas (Objeto + be + participio pasado)

1. **The book was written by Shakespeare.** (El libro fue escrito por Shakespeare.)
2. **English is spoken worldwide.** (El ingles se habla en todo el mundo.)
3. **The house was built in 1990.** (La casa fue construida en 1990.)
4. **The email has been sent.** (El correo ha sido enviado.)
5. **The meeting will be held tomorrow.** (La reunion se celebrara manana.)

## Resumen de Errores Comunes de Hispanohablantes

| Error comun | Incorrecto | Correcto |
|-------------|-----------|----------|
| Omitir el sujeto | Is important | **It** is important |
| Orden incorrecto en preguntas | You like pizza? | **Do you** like pizza? |
| Adjetivo despues del sustantivo | A car red | A **red car** |
| Doble negacion | I don't have nothing | I don't have **anything** |
| Omitir auxiliar en negativa | I no like | I **don't** like |
| Omitir "there" | Have many people | **There are** many people |
| Orden de adverbios | I go always | I **always go** |
| Usar "have" en lugar de "there is" | Have a problem | **There is** a problem |
