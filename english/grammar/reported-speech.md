# Reported Speech (Estilo Indirecto)

## Que es el Reported Speech

Cuando contamos lo que alguien dijo sin usar sus palabras exactas. En espanol tambien lo hacemos, pero en ingles hay que **cambiar el tiempo verbal** hacia atras (backshift).

| Direct Speech (palabras exactas) | Reported Speech (lo que cuentas) |
|----------------------------------|----------------------------------|
| "I am tired." | She said she was tired. |
| "I will help you." | He said he would help me. |
| "I have finished." | She said she had finished. |

---

## Regla principal: Backshift (retroceso de tiempos)

Cuando el verbo introductor esta en pasado (said, told, asked), el tiempo verbal retrocede un paso:

| Direct Speech | Reported Speech |
|---------------|-----------------|
| Present Simple: "I work here." | Past Simple: She said she worked there. |
| Present Continuous: "I am working." | Past Continuous: She said she was working. |
| Past Simple: "I worked late." | Past Perfect: She said she had worked late. |
| Present Perfect: "I have seen it." | Past Perfect: She said she had seen it. |
| Will: "I will come." | Would: She said she would come. |
| Can: "I can swim." | Could: She said she could swim. |
| May: "I may leave." | Might: She said she might leave. |
| Must: "I must go." | Had to: She said she had to go. |

> **Nota:** Si el verbo introductor esta en presente (says, tells), NO hay backshift: He says he is tired.

---

## Cambios adicionales

Ademas del tiempo verbal, cambian los pronombres, adverbios de tiempo y lugar:

| Direct | Reported |
|--------|----------|
| I / me | he, she / him, her |
| my | his, her |
| we / us | they / them |
| here | there |
| now | then / at that moment |
| today | that day |
| yesterday | the day before / the previous day |
| tomorrow | the next day / the following day |
| this | that |
| these | those |
| ago | before / earlier |
| last week | the week before / the previous week |
| next week | the following week |

---

## Say vs Tell

| Say | Tell |
|-----|------|
| No necesita objeto directo | SIEMPRE lleva objeto directo (a quien) |
| She **said** (that) she was tired. | She **told me** (that) she was tired. |
| He **said** he would come. | He **told us** he would come. |

> **Error comun:** "She said me" es INCORRECTO. Usa "She told me" o "She said to me".

## Ejemplos completos

### Afirmaciones

1. **"I love this city." → He said he loved that city.** (Dijo que amaba esa ciudad.)
2. **"We are leaving tomorrow." → They said they were leaving the next day.** (Dijeron que se iban al dia siguiente.)
3. **"I have never been to Japan." → She said she had never been to Japan.** (Dijo que nunca habia estado en Japon.)
4. **"I will call you later." → He told me he would call me later.** (Me dijo que me llamaria mas tarde.)
5. **"I can't find my keys." → She said she couldn't find her keys.** (Dijo que no encontraba sus llaves.)

### Preguntas (Reported Questions)

Las preguntas reportadas NO usan signo de interrogacion ni inversion:

| Directa | Reportada |
|---------|-----------|
| "Where do you live?" | She asked me where I lived. |
| "Are you coming?" | He asked if I was coming. |
| "What time does it start?" | She asked what time it started. |
| "Have you finished?" | He asked if I had finished. |
| "Can you help me?" | She asked if I could help her. |

**Regla:** Con WH- (where, what, when, why, how) → usa la misma palabra WH-.
Sin WH- (yes/no questions) → usa **if** o **whether**.

1. **"Do you speak English?" → She asked me if I spoke English.** (Me pregunto si hablaba ingles.)
2. **"Where is the station?" → He asked where the station was.** (Pregunto donde estaba la estacion.)
3. **"Why did you leave?" → She asked me why I had left.** (Me pregunto por que me habia ido.)
4. **"Will it rain tomorrow?" → He asked whether it would rain the next day.** (Pregunto si lloveria al dia siguiente.)
5. **"How much does it cost?" → She asked how much it cost.** (Pregunto cuanto costaba.)

### Ordenes y peticiones (Reported Commands)

Se usan con **tell + objeto + to + infinitivo** (afirmativo) o **tell + objeto + not to + infinitivo** (negativo):

| Directa | Reportada |
|---------|-----------|
| "Close the door." | He told me to close the door. |
| "Don't touch that!" | She told me not to touch that. |
| "Please sit down." | He asked me to sit down. |
| "Don't be late." | She told us not to be late. |

1. **"Stop talking!" → The teacher told us to stop talking.** (El profesor nos dijo que dejaramos de hablar.)
2. **"Please help me." → She asked me to help her.** (Me pidio que la ayudara.)
3. **"Don't forget your keys." → He reminded me not to forget my keys.** (Me recordo que no olvidara las llaves.)

## Errores comunes de hispanohablantes

| Incorrecto | Correcto | Por que |
|------------|----------|---------|
| She said me that... | She **told** me that... | Con objeto directo → told |
| He asked did I like it. | He asked **if I liked** it. | No inversion en reported questions |
| She asked where is the bank. | She asked where the bank **was**. | Orden sujeto + verbo, no pregunta |
| He told that he was tired. | He **said** that he was tired. | Tell necesita objeto: told ME |
| She said she can come. | She said she **could** come. | Backshift: can → could |
