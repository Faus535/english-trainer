# Oraciones de Relativo (Relative Clauses)

## Que son

Las relative clauses anaden informacion extra sobre un sustantivo usando pronombres relativos: **who, which, that, where, when, whose**.

| Oracion simple | Con relative clause |
|----------------|---------------------|
| I have a friend. She speaks 5 languages. | I have a friend **who speaks 5 languages**. |
| This is the book. I told you about it. | This is the book **that I told you about**. |

---

## Pronombres relativos

| Pronombre | Se usa para | Ejemplo |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| who | Personas | The woman **who** called you is here. |
| which | Cosas y animales | The car **which** I bought is blue. |
| that | Personas y cosas (informal) | The man **that** lives next door is nice. |
| where | Lugares | The restaurant **where** we ate was great. |
| when | Tiempo | I remember the day **when** we met. |
| whose | Posesion (de quien) | The girl **whose** bag was stolen called the police. |
| whom | Personas (objeto, formal) | The person **whom** you met is my boss. |

---

## Defining vs Non-defining

### Defining (especificativa) — SIN comas

Identifica de QUE persona/cosa estamos hablando. Sin ella, la frase pierde sentido o cambia de significado.

1. **The man who lives next door is a doctor.** (El hombre que vive al lado es doctor.)
2. **The book that I lent you is very old.** (El libro que te preste es muy viejo.)
3. **People who exercise regularly live longer.** (La gente que hace ejercicio regularmente vive mas.)
4. **The restaurant where we had dinner was expensive.** (El restaurante donde cenamos era caro.)
5. **I need a phone that has a good camera.** (Necesito un telefono que tenga buena camara.)

> **Nota:** En defining clauses puedes usar **that** en lugar de who/which. Tambien puedes omitir el pronombre si es objeto: "The book (that) I read" = "The book I read".

### Non-defining (explicativa) — CON comas

Anade informacion extra que se podria quitar sin cambiar el significado basico. Siempre va entre comas.

1. **My sister, who lives in London, is a lawyer.** (Mi hermana, que vive en Londres, es abogada.)
2. **The Eiffel Tower, which was built in 1889, is in Paris.** (La Torre Eiffel, que fue construida en 1889, esta en Paris.)
3. **My boss, who is very strict, gave me a raise.** (Mi jefe, que es muy estricto, me dio un aumento.)
4. **Madrid, where I was born, is the capital of Spain.** (Madrid, donde naci, es la capital de Espana.)
5. **Her new car, which she bought last week, broke down.** (Su coche nuevo, que compro la semana pasada, se averia.)

> **Importante:** En non-defining clauses NO puedes usar "that". Solo who/which/where/when/whose.

---

## Cuando omitir el pronombre relativo

Solo se puede omitir en **defining clauses** cuando el pronombre es **objeto** (no sujeto):

| Se puede omitir | No se puede omitir |
|-----------------|---------------------|
| The film (that) **I saw** was good. (pronombre = objeto) | The man **who called** is here. (pronombre = sujeto) |
| The person (whom) **you met** is my friend. | The dog **that bit** me was big. |
| The hotel (that) **we stayed at** was nice. | The girl **whose bag** was stolen cried. |

**Truco:** Si despues del pronombre hay un sujeto diferente (I, you, we, they...), el pronombre es objeto y se puede omitir. Si despues del pronombre viene un verbo, es sujeto y NO se puede omitir.

---

## Preposiciones en relative clauses

En ingles informal, la preposicion va al final. En formal, va antes del pronombre.

| Informal (normal) | Formal |
|--------------------|--------|
| The city **that** I come **from**. | The city **from which** I come. |
| The person **who** I was talking **to**. | The person **to whom** I was talking. |
| The chair **(that)** she was sitting **on**. | The chair **on which** she was sitting. |

---

## Ejemplos adicionales con whose, where, when

1. **The woman whose husband is a pilot travels a lot.** (La mujer cuyo marido es piloto viaja mucho.)
2. **I know a place where you can eat for five euros.** (Conozco un sitio donde puedes comer por cinco euros.)
3. **Do you remember the summer when we went to Italy?** (¿Recuerdas el verano cuando fuimos a Italia?)
4. **The student whose exam was perfect got a prize.** (El estudiante cuyo examen fue perfecto gano un premio.)
5. **This is the park where I run every morning.** (Este es el parque donde corro cada manana.)

## Errores comunes de hispanohablantes

| Incorrecto | Correcto | Por que |
|------------|----------|---------|
| The man **which** called me... | The man **who** called me... | Which es para cosas, who para personas |
| My brother, **that** is tall... | My brother, **who** is tall... | That no se usa en non-defining clauses |
| The house **who** I bought... | The house **that/which** I bought... | Who es para personas, no cosas |
| The city **where** I visited... | The city **that** I visited... | Where = "en la que", no "la que" |
| A person **whose** is kind... | A person **who** is kind... | Whose = posesion (cuyo), who = sujeto |
