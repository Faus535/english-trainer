# Guia Completa de Tiempos Verbales

Los tiempos verbales en ingles son mas simples de lo que parecen. Esta guia te da la estructura, cuando usarlos y ejemplos claros.

---

## Present Simple

**Nombre en espanol:** Presente Simple

### Estructura

| Forma | Estructura | Ejemplo |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| Afirmativa | Subject + verb (he/she/it adds -s) | I work. / She works. |
| Negativa | Subject + do/does + not + verb | I don't work. / She doesn't work. |
| Pregunta | Do/Does + subject + verb? | Do you work? / Does she work? |

### Cuando se usa

- **Habitos y rutinas:** I drink coffee every morning.
- **Hechos y verdades generales:** Water boils at 100 degrees Celsius.
- **Estados permanentes:** She lives in Madrid.
- **Horarios y programas:** The train leaves at 8 AM.

### Ejemplos

1. I usually wake up at 7 AM.
2. She speaks three languages fluently.
3. They don't eat meat.
4. Does he work on weekends?
5. The sun rises in the east.

### Signal words (palabras clave)

always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never, every day, every week, every month, every year, on Mondays, twice a week

## Present Continuous

**Nombre en espanol:** Presente Continuo / Presente Progresivo

### Estructura

| Forma | Estructura | Ejemplo |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| Afirmativa | Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing | I am working. / She is reading. |
| Negativa | Subject + am/is/are + not + verb-ing | I am not working. / She isn't reading. |
| Pregunta | Am/Is/Are + subject + verb-ing? | Are you working? / Is she reading? |

### Cuando se usa

- **Acciones que ocurren ahora mismo:** I'm writing an email right now.
- **Situaciones temporales:** He's staying with his parents this week.
- **Planes futuros confirmados:** We're meeting them tomorrow at noon.
- **Tendencias o cambios:** The climate is getting warmer.

### Ejemplos

1. I'm studying English at the moment.
2. She isn't listening to music right now.
3. Are they coming to the party tonight?
4. He is currently working on a new project.
5. We're having dinner with friends today.

### Signal words (palabras clave)

now, right now, at the moment, currently, today, this week, tonight, look!, listen!

## Past Simple

**Nombre en espanol:** Pasado Simple / Preterito Indefinido

### Estructura

| Forma | Estructura | Ejemplo |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| Afirmativa | Subject + verb-ed (regular) / 2nd form (irregular) | I worked. / She went. |
| Negativa | Subject + did + not + verb | I didn't work. / She didn't go. |
| Pregunta | Did + subject + verb? | Did you work? / Did she go? |

### Cuando se usa

- **Acciones terminadas en el pasado:** I visited Rome last summer.
- **Serie de acciones pasadas:** She woke up, had breakfast, and left the house.
- **Estados pasados:** He lived in London for five years (ya no vive alli).
- **Habitos pasados:** When I was young, I played football every day.

### Ejemplos

1. I traveled to Japan last year.
2. She didn't finish the report yesterday.
3. Did you see the movie last night?
4. They moved to a new city in 2020.
5. We had a great time when we were on vacation.

### Signal words (palabras clave)

yesterday, last week, last month, last year, ago, in 2020, in the 1990s, when I was young, the other day

## Past Continuous

**Nombre en espanol:** Pasado Continuo / Pasado Progresivo

### Estructura

| Forma | Estructura | Ejemplo |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| Afirmativa | Subject + was/were + verb-ing | I was working. / They were sleeping. |
| Negativa | Subject + was/were + not + verb-ing | I wasn't working. / They weren't sleeping. |
| Pregunta | Was/Were + subject + verb-ing? | Were you working? / Was she sleeping? |

### Cuando se usa

- **Accion en progreso en un momento del pasado:** At 9 PM, I was watching TV.
- **Acciones interrumpidas:** I was cooking when the phone rang.
- **Dos acciones simultaneas en el pasado:** While she was reading, he was cleaning.
- **Descripcion de ambiente o contexto:** The birds were singing and the sun was shining.

### Ejemplos

1. I was studying when you called me.
2. They were playing football all afternoon yesterday.
3. She wasn't paying attention during the meeting.
4. Were you sleeping when I arrived?
5. While we were walking in the park, it started to rain.

### Signal words (palabras clave)

while, when, at that time, at 8 o'clock, all day yesterday, all morning, as

## Present Perfect

**Nombre en espanol:** Presente Perfecto / Preterito Perfecto

### Estructura

| Forma | Estructura | Ejemplo |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| Afirmativa | Subject + have/has + past participle | I have worked. / She has gone. |
| Negativa | Subject + have/has + not + past participle | I haven't worked. / She hasn't gone. |
| Pregunta | Have/Has + subject + past participle? | Have you worked? / Has she gone? |

### Cuando se usa

- **Experiencias de vida (sin decir cuando):** I have been to Paris three times.
- **Acciones pasadas con resultado presente:** She has lost her keys (todavia no las encuentra).
- **Acciones recientes:** They have just arrived.
- **Periodos de tiempo no terminados:** I have read two books this month.
- **Con since/for para duracion:** We have lived here since 2015.

### Ejemplos

1. I have already finished my homework.
2. She hasn't visited her grandparents yet.
3. Have you ever tried sushi?
4. He has worked here for ten years.
5. We have just received the results.

### Signal words (palabras clave)

already, yet, just, ever, never, since, for, recently, lately, so far, up to now, this week/month/year, how many times

## Present Perfect Continuous

**Nombre en espanol:** Presente Perfecto Continuo / Presente Perfecto Progresivo

### Estructura

| Forma | Estructura | Ejemplo |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| Afirmativa | Subject + have/has + been + verb-ing | I have been working. / She has been reading. |
| Negativa | Subject + have/has + not + been + verb-ing | I haven't been working. / She hasn't been reading. |
| Pregunta | Have/Has + subject + been + verb-ing? | Have you been working? / Has she been reading? |

### Cuando se usa

- **Accion que empezo en el pasado y continua ahora:** I have been studying English for three years.
- **Accion reciente con resultado visible:** She's tired because she has been running.
- **Enfasis en la duracion de la actividad:** They have been waiting for two hours.
- **Acciones repetidas recientes:** He has been calling you all morning.

### Ejemplos

1. I have been learning Spanish since January.
2. She has been working here for five years.
3. How long have you been waiting?
4. It has been raining all day.
5. They haven't been sleeping well lately.

### Signal words (palabras clave)

for, since, all day, all morning, all week, how long, recently, lately

## Past Perfect

**Nombre en espanol:** Pasado Perfecto / Pluscuamperfecto

### Estructura

| Forma | Estructura | Ejemplo |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| Afirmativa | Subject + had + past participle | I had worked. / She had finished. |
| Negativa | Subject + had + not + past participle | I hadn't worked. / She hadn't finished. |
| Pregunta | Had + subject + past participle? | Had you worked? / Had she finished? |

### Cuando se usa

- **Accion anterior a otra accion pasada:** When I arrived, they had already left.
- **Causa de una situacion pasada:** She was happy because she had passed the exam.
- **Experiencia antes de un momento pasado:** I had never seen snow before I moved to Canada.
- **Third conditional (condicional 3):** If I had known, I would have helped.

### Ejemplos

1. By the time we got there, the concert had already started.
2. She had never driven a car before that day.
3. Had you finished your homework before dinner?
4. They had lived in three countries before they turned 30.
5. I didn't go to the movie because I had already seen it.

### Signal words (palabras clave)

before, after, already, by the time, when, until, as soon as, never ... before, by then

## Future Simple (will)

**Nombre en espanol:** Futuro Simple

### Estructura

| Forma | Estructura | Ejemplo |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| Afirmativa | Subject + will + verb | I will work. / She will help. |
| Negativa | Subject + will + not (won't) + verb | I won't work. / She won't help. |
| Pregunta | Will + subject + verb? | Will you work? / Will she help? |

### Cuando se usa

- **Predicciones (opiniones, creencias):** I think it will rain tomorrow.
- **Decisiones espontaneas:** I'm hungry. I'll make a sandwich.
- **Promesas:** I will always love you.
- **Ofertas y sugerencias:** I'll carry that bag for you.
- **Hechos futuros inevitables:** She will turn 30 next year.

### Ejemplos

1. I think they will win the game.
2. Don't worry, I'll help you with that.
3. She won't be at the meeting tomorrow.
4. Will you come to my birthday party?
5. It will probably snow next week.

### Signal words (palabras clave)

tomorrow, next week/month/year, I think, I believe, probably, perhaps, maybe, I promise, I'm sure

## Future with Going to

**Nombre en espanol:** Futuro con "going to" (Futuro de intencion)

### Estructura

| Forma | Estructura | Ejemplo |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| Afirmativa | Subject + am/is/are + going to + verb | I am going to work. / She is going to study. |
| Negativa | Subject + am/is/are + not + going to + verb | I'm not going to work. / She isn't going to study. |
| Pregunta | Am/Is/Are + subject + going to + verb? | Are you going to work? / Is she going to study? |

### Cuando se usa

- **Planes e intenciones decididas:** I'm going to start a new course next month.
- **Predicciones basadas en evidencia visible:** Look at those clouds! It's going to rain.
- **Intenciones firmes:** She's going to quit her job.

### Ejemplos

1. I'm going to visit my parents this weekend.
2. They're going to buy a new house next year.
3. She isn't going to accept that offer.
4. Are you going to take the exam?
5. Look at that car! It's going to crash!

### Signal words (palabras clave)

tomorrow, next week/month/year, this weekend, I'm planning to, I intend to, soon

## Future Continuous

**Nombre en espanol:** Futuro Continuo / Futuro Progresivo

### Estructura

| Forma | Estructura | Ejemplo |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| Afirmativa | Subject + will + be + verb-ing | I will be working. / She will be traveling. |
| Negativa | Subject + will + not + be + verb-ing | I won't be working. / She won't be traveling. |
| Pregunta | Will + subject + be + verb-ing? | Will you be working? / Will she be traveling? |

### Cuando se usa

- **Accion en progreso en un momento futuro:** This time tomorrow, I'll be flying to London.
- **Eventos futuros que ocurriran de forma natural:** She'll be starting university in September.
- **Preguntas educadas sobre planes:** Will you be using the car tonight?

### Ejemplos

1. This time tomorrow, I'll be sitting on the beach.
2. At 5 PM, she will be giving her presentation.
3. Will you be attending the conference next Monday?
4. They won't be working during the holidays.
5. Next week, we'll be celebrating our anniversary.

### Signal words (palabras clave)

this time tomorrow, at 5 PM, at this time next week, next Monday, in an hour, all day tomorrow

## Future Perfect

**Nombre en espanol:** Futuro Perfecto

### Estructura

| Forma | Estructura | Ejemplo |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| Afirmativa | Subject + will + have + past participle | I will have finished. / She will have left. |
| Negativa | Subject + will + not + have + past participle | I won't have finished. / She won't have left. |
| Pregunta | Will + subject + have + past participle? | Will you have finished? / Will she have left? |

### Cuando se usa

- **Accion que estara completada antes de un momento futuro:** By next Friday, I will have finished the project.
- **Logros acumulados en el futuro:** By 2030, they will have built 500 houses.
- **Suposiciones sobre el pasado desde el futuro:** She will have arrived by now (suposicion).

### Ejemplos

1. By tomorrow, I will have completed the report.
2. She will have graduated by next summer.
3. Will you have finished the book by Friday?
4. They won't have saved enough money by then.
5. By the time you arrive, we will have eaten dinner.

### Signal words (palabras clave)

by tomorrow, by next week/month/year, by the time, by then, before, by the end of

## Conditional (would)

**Nombre en espanol:** Condicional Simple

### Estructura

| Forma | Estructura | Ejemplo |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| Afirmativa | Subject + would + verb | I would travel. / She would help. |
| Negativa | Subject + would + not (wouldn't) + verb | I wouldn't travel. / She wouldn't help. |
| Pregunta | Would + subject + verb? | Would you travel? / Would she help? |

### Cuando se usa

- **Situaciones hipoteticas (second conditional):** If I had more money, I would travel the world.
- **Peticiones educadas:** Would you open the window, please?
- **Deseos y preferencias:** I would like a cup of coffee.
- **Habitos pasados (como "used to"):** When I was a child, I would play outside every day.
- **Consejos:** I would recommend the fish.

### Ejemplos

1. If I were you, I would accept the job offer.
2. Would you mind helping me with this?
3. She would love to visit Japan someday.
4. I wouldn't do that if I were you.
5. They would always go to the beach on Sundays when they were kids.

### Signal words (palabras clave)

if, would, could, might, I wish, in that case, hypothetically

## Errores Comunes de Hispanohablantes

### 1. Confundir Present Perfect con Past Simple

**Error:** "I have gone to Paris last year."
**Correcto:** "I went to Paris last year."

En espanol se usa el preterito perfecto ("he ido") con expresiones de tiempo pasado, pero en ingles, si se menciona un momento especifico del pasado (last year, yesterday, in 2020), se debe usar Past Simple.

**Regla:** Si hay una referencia temporal pasada y concreta, usa Past Simple.

### 2. Omitir el auxiliar en preguntas y negaciones

**Error:** "You like coffee?" / "I no like coffee."
**Correcto:** "Do you like coffee?" / "I don't like coffee."

En espanol la pregunta se forma solo con la entonacion, y la negacion se hace poniendo "no" antes del verbo. En ingles se necesita el auxiliar do/does/did.

### 3. Usar Present Simple en lugar de Present Continuous

**Error:** "I work right now." (cuando estas trabajando en este momento)
**Correcto:** "I'm working right now."

En espanol no hay distincion tan marcada entre "trabajo" y "estoy trabajando." En ingles, si la accion ocurre ahora mismo, se debe usar Present Continuous.

### 4. Usar doble negacion

**Error:** "I don't know nothing."
**Correcto:** "I don't know anything."

En espanol la doble negacion es correcta ("No se nada"), pero en ingles estandar solo se usa una negacion.

### 5. Confundir "will" y "going to"

**Error:** "Look at those clouds, it will rain!" (con evidencia visible)
**Correcto:** "Look at those clouds, it's going to rain!"

Cuando hay evidencia visible de lo que va a pasar, se usa "going to." "Will" se usa para predicciones basadas en opinion o creencia.

### 6. Olvidar la -s en tercera persona del Present Simple

**Error:** "She work every day."
**Correcto:** "She works every day."

En espanol los verbos ya cambian de forma segun la persona, asi que no se piensa en "anadir algo." En ingles, la unica conjugacion del presente es la -s/-es para he/she/it.

### 7. Traducir literalmente el Present Perfect Continuous

**Error:** "I am studying English since three years."
**Correcto:** "I have been studying English for three years."

En espanol se dice "Estudio ingles desde hace tres anos" (presente simple), pero en ingles se necesita Present Perfect Continuous + for/since para expresar duracion desde el pasado hasta ahora.

### 8. Confundir "for" y "since"

**Error:** "I have lived here since five years."
**Correcto:** "I have lived here for five years." / "I have lived here since 2021."

- **For** = duracion (for two hours, for three years, for a long time)
- **Since** = punto de inicio (since Monday, since 2015, since I was a child)

### 9. Usar Past Simple en lugar de Past Perfect para la accion anterior

**Error:** "When I arrived, they already left."
**Correcto:** "When I arrived, they had already left."

Cuando una accion ocurre antes de otra en el pasado, la primera accion va en Past Perfect.

### 10. Traducir "si" condicional de forma literal

**Error:** "If I would have money, I would travel."
**Correcto:** "If I had money, I would travel."

En la segunda condicional (situaciones hipoteticas presentes), la clausula con "if" lleva Past Simple, no "would." "Would" solo va en la clausula de resultado.
