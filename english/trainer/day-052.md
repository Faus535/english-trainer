# Dia 52 — Entonacion + Numeros (25 min)

## Objetivo del dia
Aprender los patrones basicos de entonacion inglesa y dominar numeros hablados.

## Bloque 1: Entonacion basica (10 min)

La entonacion es la "melodia" de las frases. En ingles, subir o bajar la voz cambia el significado.

### Dos patrones principales

**1. Entonacion descendente (falling) ↘ — afirmaciones y preguntas WH**

La voz BAJA al final. Se usa para:
- Afirmaciones: "I live in Madrid." ↘
- Preguntas con WH (what, where, when, why, who, how): "What's your name?" ↘
- Ordenes: "Close the door." ↘

**Practica** (baja la voz en la ultima palabra):

1. I'm from Spain. ↘
2. What's your name? ↘
3. Where do you live? ↘
4. She works in a hospital. ↘
5. How are you? ↘

**2. Entonacion ascendente (rising) ↗ — preguntas si/no y listas incompletas**

La voz SUBE al final. Se usa para:
- Preguntas de si/no: "Do you like pizza?" ↗
- Elementos de una lista (excepto el ultimo): "I bought apples ↗, bananas ↗, and oranges ↘."
- Cuando no has terminado de hablar: "Well..." ↗

**Practica** (sube la voz al final):

1. Do you like coffee? ↗
2. Are you coming? ↗
3. Is she your sister? ↗
4. Can I help you? ↗
5. Have you been to London? ↗

### Entonacion en listas

> I bought **apples** ↗, **bananas** ↗, and **oranges** ↘.

El ultimo elemento SIEMPRE baja. Los anteriores suben para indicar "aun no he terminado".

**Practica estas listas:**

1. I speak Spanish ↗, English ↗, and French ↘.
2. She likes running ↗, swimming ↗, and cycling ↘.
3. We need milk ↗, bread ↗, eggs ↗, and butter ↘.

### Ejercicio: ¿Sube o baja?

1. Where are you from? → ↘ (pregunta WH)
2. Do you have a car? → ↗ (pregunta si/no)
3. I love this song. → ↘ (afirmacion)
4. Can you hear me? → ↗ (pregunta si/no)
5. What happened? → ↘ (pregunta WH)

## Bloque 2: Numeros hablados (10 min)

Los numeros en ingles hablado tienen trampas. Aqui estan las principales.

### -teen vs -ty (la confusion clasica)

| Numero | Pronunciacion | Acento |
|--------|--------------|--------|
| 13 thirteen | /θɜːrˈtiːn/ | Acento en -TEEN |
| 30 thirty | /ˈθɜːr.ti/ | Acento en THIR- |
| 14 fourteen | /fɔːrˈtiːn/ | Acento en -TEEN |
| 40 forty | /ˈfɔːr.ti/ | Acento en FOR- |
| 15 fifteen | /fɪfˈtiːn/ | Acento en -TEEN |
| 50 fifty | /ˈfɪf.ti/ | Acento en FIF- |
| 16 sixteen | /sɪksˈtiːn/ | Acento en -TEEN |
| 60 sixty | /ˈsɪks.ti/ | Acento en SIX- |

**Regla:** -TEEN tiene acento al final. -TY tiene acento al principio.

Practica: "I'm thirTEEN" vs "I'm THIRty". Di ambos 5 veces.

### Fechas

| Escrito | Se dice |
|---------|---------|
| March 5th | March fifth /mɑːrtʃ fɪfθ/ |
| January 1st | January first |
| July 22nd | July twenty-second |
| December 31st | December thirty-first |

**Nota:** En ingles americano: "March fifth" o "March five". En ingles britanico: "the fifth of March".

### Dinero

| Escrito | Se dice |
|---------|---------|
| $3.50 | "three fifty" o "three dollars and fifty cents" |
| $10.99 | "ten ninety-nine" |
| €2.30 | "two thirty" o "two euros and thirty cents" |
| $0.99 | "ninety-nine cents" |

### Hora

| Escrito | Se dice |
|---------|---------|
| 2:30 | "two thirty" o "half past two" |
| 9:15 | "nine fifteen" o "a quarter past nine" |
| 7:45 | "seven forty-five" o "a quarter to eight" |
| 12:00 | "twelve o'clock" o "noon/midnight" |

### Ejercicio: Di estos en voz alta

1. 15 vs 50 → "fifTEEN" vs "FIFty"
2. $7.99 → "seven ninety-nine"
3. March 14th → "March fourteenth"
4. 3:45 → "three forty-five" o "a quarter to four"
5. 13 vs 30 → "thirTEEN" vs "THIRty"
