## Bloque 2: Used to / Would (10 min)

Dos formas de hablar del pasado habitual:

### "Used to" — estados Y habitos pasados
- **Estado pasado:** "I **used to** live in Barcelona." (ya no vivo alli)
- **Habito pasado:** "I **used to** walk to school." (ya no lo hago)
- **Negativa:** "I **didn't use to** like coffee." (ahora si me gusta)
- **Pregunta:** "**Did** you **use to** smoke?" (¿fumabas antes?)

### "Would" — SOLO habitos pasados (NO estados)
- **Habito pasado:** "We **would** play football every Sunday." ✓
- **Estado pasado:** "I **would** live in Barcelona." ✗ INCORRECTO — usa "used to"

**Regla clave:**
- ¿Es un **habito** repetido? → "used to" o "would"
- ¿Es un **estado** (vivir, ser, tener, gustar)? → SOLO "used to"

| Frase | Used to | Would | ¿Por que? |
|-------|---------|-------|-----------|
| "I ___ live in Madrid." | ✓ | ✗ | Estado (vivir) |
| "We ___ go fishing on Sundays." | ✓ | ✓ | Habito repetido |
| "She ___ be very shy." | ✓ | ✗ | Estado (ser) |
| "They ___ visit us every summer." | ✓ | ✓ | Habito repetido |
| "He ___ have a dog." | ✓ | ✗ | Estado (tener) |

**Ejercicio:** Completa con "used to" o "would" (o ambos si es posible):
1. When I was a child, I ___ play in the park every day.
2. My grandmother ___ live in a small village.
3. We ___ eat ice cream after school.
4. I ___ be afraid of the dark.
5. My parents ___ take us to the beach every summer.

> Respuestas: 1) used to / would 2) used to (estado) 3) used to / would 4) used to (estado) 5) used to / would
