# Dia 47 — /r/ vs /l/ (right vs light) (25 min)

## Objetivo del dia
Dominar la diferencia entre /r/ y /l/ inglesas y aprender conectores basicos.

## Bloque 1: /r/ vs /l/ (10 min)

La /r/ inglesa es MUY diferente de la /r/ espanola. No tiene NADA que ver.

### Como producir cada sonido

| Sonido | Posicion de la lengua | Descripcion |
|--------|----------------------|-------------|
| /r/ inglesa | La lengua se curva hacia atras, **NO toca nada** | Los labios se redondean ligeramente. La punta de la lengua flota en el centro de la boca. |
| /l/ inglesa | La punta de la lengua **toca** detras de los dientes superiores | El aire escapa por los lados de la lengua. |

**Error comun:** Los hispanohablantes usan la /r/ espanola (con vibracion, como en "perro"). La /r/ inglesa **NUNCA vibra** y la lengua **NUNCA toca** el paladar.

**Truco para /r/:** Di "ahhh" con la boca abierta. Ahora, sin cerrar la boca, curva la lengua lentamente hacia atras. Cuando escuches el sonido cambiar, esa es la /r/ inglesa.

### Pares minimos /r/ vs /l/

| /r/ | /l/ | Significado /r/ | Significado /l/ |
|-----|-----|-----------------|-----------------|
| right /raɪt/ | light /laɪt/ | derecho/correcto | luz/ligero |
| read /riːd/ | lead /liːd/ | leer | liderar |
| red /red/ | led /led/ | rojo | lidero (pasado) |
| rock /rɒk/ | lock /lɒk/ | roca | cerradura |
| rain /reɪn/ | lane /leɪn/ | lluvia | carril |
| race /reɪs/ | lace /leɪs/ | carrera | encaje |
| rice /raɪs/ | lice /laɪs/ | arroz | piojos |
| pray /preɪ/ | play /pleɪ/ | rezar | jugar |
| fry /fraɪ/ | fly /flaɪ/ | freir | volar |
| crowd /kraʊd/ | cloud /klaʊd/ | multitud | nube |
| crime /kraɪm/ | climb /klaɪm/ | crimen | escalar |

### Frases de practica

1. Turn **right** at the traffic **light**. → /r/ luego /l/
2. I **read** that she will **lead** the team. → /r/ luego /l/
3. The **rain** fell on the **lane**. → /r/ luego /l/
4. Let's **pray** before we **play**. → /r/ luego /l/
5. The **crowd** looked at the **cloud**. → /r/ luego /l/
6. She wore **red** and **led** the parade. → /r/ luego /l/

### Trabalenguas

> **Really leery Larry rarely relies on Lucy.**

Repite 5 veces, cada vez mas rapido.

## Bloque 2: Conectores basicos (10 min)

Los conectores unen ideas y hacen tu ingles mas fluido y sofisticado.

### Conectores de causa y contraste

| Conector | Significado | Ejemplo |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| because | porque | I stayed home **because** it was raining. |
| although | aunque | **Although** it rained, we went out. |
| however | sin embargo | I tried hard. **However**, I failed. |
| therefore | por lo tanto | He studied hard; **therefore**, he passed. |
| moreover | ademas | It's cheap. **Moreover**, it's high quality. |
| in addition | adicionalmente | She speaks French. **In addition**, she speaks German. |
| on the other hand | por otro lado | Pizza is delicious. **On the other hand**, it's unhealthy. |

### Ejercicio: Conecta las ideas

1. I was tired. I went to the gym. (although) → **Although I was tired, I went to the gym.**
2. She studied hard. She passed the exam. (therefore) → **She studied hard; therefore, she passed the exam.**
3. It was cold. We went for a walk. (however) → **It was cold. However, we went for a walk.**
4. I stayed home. It was raining. (because) → **I stayed home because it was raining.**
5. He's smart. He works hard. (moreover) → **He's smart. Moreover, he works hard.**
