# Dia 31 — /w/ vs /v/ (25 min)

## Objetivo del dia
Diferenciar /w/ y /v/, otro par problematico para hispanohablantes, y contrastar Present Perfect vs Past Simple.

## Bloque 1: /w/ vs /v/ (10 min)

Muchos hispanohablantes dicen "wine" y "vine" exactamente igual. Son sonidos diferentes.

### Como producir cada sonido

| Sonido | Posicion | Descripcion |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| /w/ | Labios MUY redondeados | Como prepararte para dar un beso. No hay contacto de dientes. Labios forman un circulo pequeno. |
| /v/ | Labiodental | Dientes superiores sobre labio inferior + friccion. |

**Truco:** Para /w/, di "uuu" y luego pasa a la vocal. "uuuine" = wine. Para /v/, muerde tu labio: "vvvine" = vine.

### Pares minimos /w/ vs /v/

| /w/ | /v/ | Significado /w/ | Significado /v/ |
|-----|-----|-----------------|-----------------|
| wine /waɪn/ | vine /vaɪn/ | vino | vid/enredadera |
| west /west/ | vest /vest/ | oeste | chaleco |
| wet /wet/ | vet /vet/ | mojado | veterinario |
| wail /weɪl/ | veil /veɪl/ | lamento | velo |
| wow /waʊ/ | vow /vaʊ/ | guau | juramento |
| worse /wɜːrs/ | verse /vɜːrs/ | peor | verso |
| wary /ˈwer.i/ | vary /ˈver.i/ | cauteloso | variar |
| wheel /wiːl/ | veal /viːl/ | rueda | ternera |

### Frases de practica

1. The **wine** grows on a **vine**. → /w/ luego /v/
2. My **vest** keeps me warm in the **west**. → /v/ luego /w/
3. The **wet vet** examined the dog. → /w/ luego /v/
4. She wore a **veil** with a **wail**. → /v/ luego /w/
5. He said "**wow**" after the **vow**. → /w/ luego /v/
6. This **verse** is **worse** than the last. → /v/ luego /w/

## Bloque 2: Present Perfect vs Past Simple (10 min)

Esta es una de las confusiones mas comunes. La clave es: **tiempo especifico o no**.

### La regla de oro

| Situacion | Tiempo | Ejemplo |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| **Present Perfect** | Sin tiempo especifico | I **have seen** that movie. (en algun momento de mi vida) |
| **Past Simple** | Con tiempo especifico | I **saw** that movie **yesterday**. (se cuando fue) |

### Palabras que fuerzan Past Simple
yesterday, last week, last year, in 2020, two days ago, when I was young

### Palabras que fuerzan Present Perfect
ever, never, already, yet, just, since, for, recently, so far

### Ejercicio: Elige el correcto

1. I ___ (have visited / visited) Paris in 2019. → **visited** (tiempo especifico: 2019)
2. I ___ (have visited / visited) Paris three times. → **have visited** (sin tiempo especifico)
3. She ___ (has left / left) five minutes ago. → **left** (ago = Past Simple)
4. She ___ (has just left / just left). → **has just left** (just = Present Perfect)
5. ___ you ever ___ (have...eaten / did...eat) frog legs? → **Have you ever eaten** (ever = PP)

### Pares de ejemplo

| Present Perfect | Past Simple |
|----------------|-------------|
| I have been to Japan. | I went to Japan last year. |
| She has written 3 books. | She wrote her first book in 2015. |
| We have already eaten. | We ate at 7 pm. |
| Have you ever tried sushi? | Did you try the sushi yesterday? |
