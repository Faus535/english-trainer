## Bloque 2: Estructura de la frase inglesa — SVO (10 min)

El ingles tiene una regla de oro: **Sujeto + Verbo + Objeto (SVO)**. El verbo NUNCA cambia de posicion.

**En español puedes decir:**
- "Pizza como yo" (OVS) — se entiende
- "Yo como pizza" (SVO) — se entiende
- "Como pizza" (VO) — se entiende

**En ingles SOLO funciona:**
- "I eat pizza" (SVO) — correcto
- ~~"Pizza eat I"~~ — INCORRECTO
- ~~"Eat I pizza"~~ — INCORRECTO

**Preguntas — usa auxiliares, NO muevas el verbo:**
- ~~"Eat you pizza?"~~ → **"Do you eat pizza?"**
- ~~"Like she coffee?"~~ → **"Does she like coffee?"**
- ~~"Went he home?"~~ → **"Did he go home?"**

**Negativas — igual, usa auxiliares:**
- ~~"I not eat pizza"~~ → **"I don't eat pizza"**
- ~~"She not likes coffee"~~ → **"She doesn't like coffee"**

**Estructura completa:**

| Tipo | Estructura | Ejemplo |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Afirmativo | S + V + O | I eat pizza. |
| Negativo | S + aux + not + V + O | I don't eat pizza. |
| Pregunta | Aux + S + V + O? | Do you eat pizza? |
| Wh-pregunta | Wh + aux + S + V? | What do you eat? |

**Practica — corrige estas frases:**
1. ~~"Always I drink coffee."~~ → **"I always drink coffee."**
2. ~~"Speaks she English?"~~ → **"Does she speak English?"**
3. ~~"Not I understand."~~ → **"I don't understand."**
4. ~~"Yesterday went I to the park."~~ → **"Yesterday I went to the park."**
5. ~~"Pizza likes he."~~ → **"He likes pizza."**
