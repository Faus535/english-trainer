# Dia 64 — Linking y Elision (25 min)

## Objetivo del dia
Aprender como los nativos conectan palabras (linking), eliminan sonidos (elision) y asimilan consonantes en el habla real. Iniciar Reported Speech.

## Bloque 1: Linking, Elision y Assimilation (10 min)

### Linking (enlace entre palabras)

Cuando una palabra termina en consonante y la siguiente empieza en vocal, se **enlazan**:

| Frase | Pronunciacion enlazada | IPA |
|---|---|---|
| turn off | tur-noff | /tɜːr.nɒf/ |
| an apple | a-napple | /ən.ˈæp.əl/ |
| pick it up | pi-ki-tup | /pɪk.ɪt.ʌp/ |
| come in | co-min | /kʌm.ɪn/ |

**Intrusive /r/:** Entre dos vocales, los britanicos insertan una /r/ que no existe en la escritura:
- "law and order" → /lɔː**r**.ənd.ˈɔːr.dər/
- "idea of" → /aɪˈdɪə.**r**.əv/

### Elision (desaparicion de sonidos)

Los sonidos /t/ y /d/ desaparecen entre consonantes:

| Frase | Se escribe | Se pronuncia | IPA |
|---|---|---|---|
| next day | nex**t** day | nex day | /neks.deɪ/ |
| last night | las**t** night | las night | /lɑːs.naɪt/ |
| just now | jus**t** now | jus now | /dʒʌs.naʊ/ |
| send me | sen**d** me | sen me | /sen.miː/ |
| old man | ol**d** man | ol man | /oʊl.mæn/ |

### Assimilation (cambio de sonidos)

Un sonido cambia para parecerse al que le sigue:

| Frase | Cambio | IPA |
|---|---|---|
| ten boys | /n/ → /m/ antes de /b/ | /tem.bɔɪz/ |
| good girl | /d/ → /ɡ/ antes de /ɡ/ | /ɡʊɡ.ɡɜːrl/ |
| in Paris | /n/ → /m/ antes de /p/ | /ɪm.ˈpær.ɪs/ |

**Ejercicio:** Lee estas frases en voz alta aplicando linking, elision y assimilation:
1. I went to an old house. (linking: an-old)
2. She must be at the last meeting. (elision: las meeting)
3. He ran past me. (elision: pas me)

## Bloque 2: Reported Speech — Estructura (10 min)

El Reported Speech (estilo indirecto) se usa para contar lo que alguien dijo.

**Estructura:** Sujeto + said (that) + oracion con **backshift** de tiempo verbal.

### Backshift de tiempos verbales

| Directo | Reportado |
|---|---|
| "I **am** tired." | She said she **was** tired. |
| "I **like** pizza." | He said he **liked** pizza. |
| "I **will** come." | She said she **would** come. |
| "I **can** swim." | He said he **could** swim. |
| "I **have** finished." | She said she **had** finished. |

### Cambios de tiempo y lugar

| Directo | Reportado |
|---|---|
| today | that day |
| tomorrow | the next day |
| yesterday | the day before |
| here | there |
| now | then |
| this | that |

**Ejercicio:** Transforma a reported speech:
1. "I am happy." → She said (that) ___
2. "We will travel tomorrow." → They said (that) ___
3. "I can speak French." → He said (that) ___
4. "I bought a car yesterday." → She said (that) ___
5. "I am working here now." → He said (that) ___

**Respuestas:**
1. She said (that) she was happy.
2. They said (that) they would travel the next day.
3. He said (that) he could speak French.
4. She said (that) she had bought a car the day before.
5. He said (that) he was working there then.
