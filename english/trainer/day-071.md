# Dia 71 — Stress y ritmo (25 min)

## Objetivo del dia
Entender que el ingles es un idioma stress-timed (a diferencia del espanol) y comenzar con las clausulas de relativo.

## Bloque 1: Stress-timed vs Syllable-timed (10 min)

El espanol es **syllable-timed**: cada silaba dura lo mismo. El ingles es **stress-timed**: las silabas acentuadas vienen a intervalos regulares y las no acentuadas se comprimen.

### Ejemplo clave

Estas dos frases tardan **el mismo tiempo** en decirse:

- "The **CAT** sat **ON** the **MAT**." (3 stressed, 6 total syllables)
- "The **BEAU**tiful cat was **SIT**ting on the **COM**fortable mat." (3 stressed, 14 total syllables)

Ambas tienen 3 acentos principales, asi que el ritmo es el mismo — las silabas extra se comprimen.

### Reglas de word stress

**Sustantivos de 2 silabas → acento en la 1ra:**
| Palabra | Stress | IPA |
|---|---|---|
| TAble | **TA**-ble | /ˈteɪ.bəl/ |
| DOCtor | **DOC**-tor | /ˈdɒk.tər/ |
| WATer | **WA**-ter | /ˈwɔː.tər/ |

**Verbos de 2 silabas → acento en la 2da:**
| Palabra | Stress | IPA |
|---|---|---|
| preSENT | pre-**SENT** | /prɪˈzent/ |
| reCORD | re-**CORD** | /rɪˈkɔːrd/ |
| proDUCE | pro-**DUCE** | /prəˈdjuːs/ |

**Ojo:** Misma palabra, diferente funcion:
- **RE**cord (sustantivo) = un disco/registro
- re**CORD** (verbo) = grabar

**Ejercicio — Marca las silabas acentuadas (mayusculas):**
1. I want to go to the shops. → I **WANT** to **GO** to the **SHOPS**.
2. She told me about the problem. → She **TOLD** me aBOUT the **PROB**lem.
3. The children are playing outside. → The **CHIL**dren are **PLAY**ing out**SIDE**.

## Bloque 2: Relative Clauses — Estructura (10 min)

### Defining Relative Clauses (especificativas)
Dan informacion **esencial** — sin ella, no sabes de que hablas. **Sin comas.**

- "The man **who called** is my father." (cual hombre? el que llamo)
- "The book **that I bought** is great." (cual libro? el que compre)

### Non-defining Relative Clauses (explicativas)
Dan informacion **extra** — puedes quitarla y la frase sigue teniendo sentido. **Con comas.**

- "My father, **who lives in Madrid**, called me." (ya sabemos quien es)
- "London, **which is the capital**, is very expensive." (info adicional)

### Pronombres relativos

| Pronombre | Uso | Ejemplo |
|---|---|---|
| who | personas | The girl **who** sings is my sister. |
| which | cosas | The car **which** is red is mine. |
| that | personas/cosas (solo defining) | The man **that** called / The book **that** I read |
| where | lugares | The city **where** I was born. |
| when | tiempos | The day **when** we met. |
| whose | posesion | The man **whose** car was stolen. |

**Ejercicio — Completa con who, which, that, where, when, whose:**
1. The teacher ___ helped me was very kind. (who/that)
2. The movie ___ we watched was boring. (which/that)
3. The restaurant ___ we had dinner was expensive. (where)
4. The woman ___ bag was stolen called the police. (whose)
5. That was the year ___ I graduated. (when)
