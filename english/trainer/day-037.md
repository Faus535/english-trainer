# Dia 37 — /θ/ vs /t/ completo (25 min)

## Objetivo del dia
Dominar la diferencia /θ/ vs /t/ con ejemplos de confusion real y aprender el futuro con going to.

## Bloque 1: /θ/ vs /t/ — confusion completa (10 min)

Si sustituyes /θ/ por /t/, creas frases absurdas o incomprensibles.

### Ejemplos de confusion

| Lo que quieres decir | Si dices /t/ | El nativo escucha |
|---------------------|-------------|-------------------|
| I **think** so. | I "tink" so. | ¿Tink? No existe. |
| **Three** books. | "Tree" books. | ¿Libros de arbol? |
| She **thought** well. | She "taught" well. | Ella ENSENO bien (diferente!) |
| Take a **bath**. | Take a "bat". | ¡Toma un murcielago! |
| **Both** of them. | "Boat" of them. | ¿Barco de ellos? |
| Open your **mouth**. | Open your "mout". | Suena extrano. |

### thought /θɔːt/ vs taught /tɔːt/

Esta pareja es peligrosa:
- **thought** = penso (pasado de think) → She **thought** about it.
- **taught** = enseno (pasado de teach) → She **taught** English.

### Frases de practica avanzada

1. **THREE TREES** — si dices /triː triːz/ suena como "tree trees". Di: /θriː triːz/.
2. She **THOUGHT** well. (penso) vs She **TAUGHT** well. (enseno)
3. I need a **BATH** (bano), not a **BAT** (murcielago)!
4. **BOTH** boats sailed away. → /θ/ al inicio, no "boat boats".
5. **THANK** the driver, don't fill the **TANK**!

### Ejercicio

Graba tu voz diciendo estas 5 frases. Escuchate. ¿Se distingue /θ/ de /t/?

## Bloque 2: Futuro con going to (10 min)

### Estructura

> **Subject + am/is/are + going to + verbo base**

### Cuando usar going to

| Uso | Ejemplo |
|-----|---------|
| Planes decididos | I**'m going to** study medicine. |
| Predicciones con evidencia | Look at those clouds — it**'s going to** rain! |
| Intenciones | She**'s going to** call the doctor tomorrow. |

### will vs going to — la diferencia clave

| will | going to |
|------|----------|
| Decision espontanea | Plan previo |
| "The phone's ringing — I**'ll** answer it." | "I**'m going to** call mom tonight." |
| Prediccion (opinion) | Prediccion (evidencia) |
| "I think it **will** rain." | "Look at those clouds — it**'s going to** rain." |

### Ejercicio: Elige will o going to

1. (Ves a alguien con maletas) Where ___ you ___? → **are** you **going to** go (evidencia)
2. (Te ofrecen cafe) Yes, I ___ have one, please. → **'ll** (espontaneo)
3. (Tu amigo planeo un viaje) He ___ visit London next month. → **'s going to** (plan)
4. (Opinion) I think Spain ___ win the World Cup. → **will** (opinion)
5. (Promesa) I ___ never forget you. → **will** (promesa)
