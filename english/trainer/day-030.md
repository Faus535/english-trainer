# Dia 30 — /b/ vs /v/ completo (25 min)

## Objetivo del dia
Consolidar la diferencia /b/ vs /v/ con frases completas y practicar el Present Perfect.

## Bloque 1: Pares minimos /b/ vs /v/ en contexto (10 min)

Recuerda: /b/ = labios juntos explotan. /v/ = dientes superiores sobre labio inferior + friccion.

### Frases de confusion

Lee cada frase en voz alta. Exagera /b/ y /v/:

1. It's **very** good with **berries**. → /v/ en very, /b/ en berries.
2. The **best vest** I own is blue. → /b/ en best, /v/ en vest.
3. He made a **bow** and a **vow**. → /b/ en bow, /v/ en vow.
4. Don't **ban** the **van**! → /b/ en ban, /v/ en van.
5. I'll **bet** on the **vet**. → /b/ en bet, /v/ en vet.
6. The **base** of the **vase** is cracked. → /b/ en base, /v/ en vase.
7. Every **boat** deserves a **vote**. → /b/ en boat, /v/ en vote.
8. That **bolt** carries 100 **volts**. → /b/ en bolt, /v/ en volt.

### Trabalenguas

> **Victor's very brave brother bought a beautiful velvet vest.**

Repitelo 5 veces aumentando la velocidad.

### Ejercicio: Elige /b/ o /v/

Completa con la palabra correcta:

1. She is a ___ (vet/bet) for animals. → **vet**
2. I want to ___ (vote/boat) in the election. → **vote**
3. That's the ___ (best/vest) option. → **best**
4. He drives a white ___ (ban/van). → **van**
5. The ___ (berry/very) is ___ (berry/very) sweet. → **berry**, **very**

## Bloque 2: Present Perfect — practica (10 min)

### Ejercicio 1: Experiencias de vida

Responde con oraciones completas:

1. Have you ever been to London? → I have never been to London. / Yes, I have been to London.
2. Have you ever eaten sushi? → ___
3. Have you ever seen a famous person? → ___
4. Have you ever flown in a plane? → ___
5. Have you ever read a book in English? → ___

### Ejercicio 2: Forma oraciones

Usa las palabras clave (already, yet, just, never, ever):

1. She / leave / already → **She has already left.**
2. I / not finish / yet → **I haven't finished yet.**
3. He / just / arrive → **He has just arrived.**
4. You / ever / travel / to Asia? → **Have you ever traveled to Asia?**
5. We / never / see / that movie → **We have never seen that movie.**
