# Unit 9: Dictation ‚ÄĒ British English (English with Lucy Style)

## Objetivo

Practicar dictation con contenido en ingles britanico, enfocandote en las caracteristicas de pronunciacion britanica que difieren del ingles americano. Trabajaras con 10 oraciones que destacan la R no rotica, la "broad A", la "T" glotal y otras marcas del acento britanico estandar (Received Pronunciation y estuary English).

## Contenido

### Caracteristicas del ingles britanico (RP / Standard British)

Antes de las oraciones, repasemos las diferencias clave:

| Caracteristica | Ingles britanico | Ingles americano | Ejemplo |
|---|---|---|---|
| R no rotica | No se pronuncia /r/ despues de vocal | Se pronuncia siempre | "car" ‚Üí /k…Ďňź/ (GB) vs /k…Ďňźr/ (US) |
| Broad A | /…Ďňź/ en "bath, grass, dance" | /√¶/ en las mismas | "bath" ‚Üí /b…Ďňźőł/ (GB) vs /b√¶őł/ (US) |
| T glotal | /t/ ‚Üí / Ē/ entre vocales | /t/ ‚Üí /…ĺ/ (flap) | "butter" ‚Üí /ňąb Ć Ē…ô/ (GB) vs /ňąb Ć…ĺ…ôr/ (US) |
| O abierta | /…í/ en "hot, lot, got" | /…Ďňź/ en las mismas | "hot" ‚Üí /h…ít/ (GB) vs /h…Ďňźt/ (US) |
| "U" larga | /juňź/ tras t, d, n | A menudo solo /uňź/ | "tune" ‚Üí /tjuňźn/ (GB) vs /tuňźn/ (US) |
| Linking R | R aparece entre vocales | No aplica | "idea of" ‚Üí /a…™ňąd…™…ôr …ôv/ (GB) |

#### Oracion 1: La R no rotica

**Formal (texto completo):**
"Her car was parked in the corner of the car park near the harbour."

**Natural (como suena en RP):**
"Her car was parked in the corner of the car park near the harbour."

**IPA britanico:**
/h…úňź k…Ďňź w…ôz p…Ďňźkt …™n √į…ô ňąk…Ēňźn…ôr …ôv √į…ô k…Ďňź p…Ďňźk n…™…ô √į…ô ňąh…Ďňźb…ô/

**IPA americano (comparacion):**
/h…úňźr k…Ďňźr w…ôz p…Ďňźrkt …™n √į…ô ňąk…Ēňźrn…ôr …ôv √į…ô k…Ďňźr p…Ďňźrk n…™r √į…ô ňąh…Ďňźrb…ôr/

**Fenomenos:** R no rotica en "her" /h…úňź/, "car" /k…Ďňź/, "corner" /ňąk…Ēňźn…ô/, "park" /p…Ďňźk/, "near" /n…™…ô/, "harbour" /ňąh…Ďňźb…ô/. Ninguna /r/ despues de vocal.

#### Oracion 2: Broad A

**Formal:**
"After the bath, she walked along the path to the grass and had a glass of water."

**Natural:**
"After the bath, she walked along the path t…ô the grass'n had a glass of water."

**IPA britanico:**
/ňą…Ďňźft…ô √į…ô b…Ďňźőł  Éiňź w…Ēňźkt …ôňąl…íŇč √į…ô p…Ďňźőł t…ô √į…ô …°r…Ďňźs …ôn h√¶d …ô …°l…Ďňźs …ôv ňąw…Ēňźt…ô/

**Fenomenos:** Broad A /…Ďňź/ en: "after" /ňą…Ďňźft…ô/, "bath" /b…Ďňźőł/, "path" /p…Ďňźőł/, "grass" /…°r…Ďňźs/, "glass" /…°l…Ďňźs/. En americano, todas estas tendrian /√¶/.

#### Oracion 3: T glotal

**Formal:**
"I've got a bottle of water and a bit of butter for the sandwiches."

**Natural:**
"I've go' a bo'le of wa'er and a bi' of bu'er for the sandwiches."

**IPA britanico (estuary/informal):**
/a…™v …°…í Ē …ô ňąb…í Ē…ôl …ôv ňąw…Ēňź Ē…ô …ôn …ô b…™ Ē …ôv ňąb Ć Ē…ô f…ô √į…ô ňąs√¶nw…™d í…™z/

**Fenomenos:** T glotal / Ē/ en: "got" /…°…í Ē/, "bottle" /ňąb…í Ē…ôl/, "water" /ňąw…Ēňź Ē…ô/, "bit" /b…™ Ē/, "butter" /ňąb Ć Ē…ô/. Este es un rasgo mas informal (estuary English) pero muy comun.

#### Oracion 4: O abierta + R no rotica

**Formal:**
"He thought it was important to sort out the problem before morning."

**Natural:**
"He thought it was important t…ô sort out the problem before morning."

**IPA britanico:**
/hiňź őł…Ēňźt …™t w…ôz …™mňąp…Ēňźt…ônt t…ô s…Ēňźt a ät √į…ô ňąpr…íbl…ôm b…™ňąf…Ēňź ňąm…Ēňźn…™Ňč/

**Fenomenos:** O abierta /…í/ en "problem" /ňąpr…íbl…ôm/. R no rotica en "important" /…™mňąp…Ēňźt…ônt/, "sort" /s…Ēňźt/, "before" /b…™ňąf…Ēňź/, "morning" /ňąm…Ēňźn…™Ňč/.

#### Oracion 5: Linking R

**Formal:**
"The idea of going to America is rather exciting, isn't it?"

**Natural:**
"The idea-r-of going to America's rather exciting, isn't it?"

**IPA britanico:**
/√į…™ a…™ňąd…™…ôr …ôv ňą…°…ô ä…™Ňč t ä …ôňąm…õr…™k…ôz ňąr…Ďňź√į…ôr …™kňąsa…™t…™Ňč ňą…™z…ônt …™t/

**Fenomenos:** **Linking R** ‚ÄĒ en "idea of", una /r/ aparece entre la vocal final de "idea" y la vocal inicial de "of": /a…™ňąd…™…ôr …ôv/. Tambien en "rather exciting": /ňąr…Ďňź√į…ôr …™k/. Esta /r/ no existe en la escritura pero se inserta para conectar vocales.

#### Oracion 6: Mezcla de rasgos

**Formal:**
"The teacher asked the class to answer the last question before half past four."

**Natural:**
"The teacher asked the class t…ô answer the last question before half past four."

**IPA britanico:**
/√į…ô ňątiňźt É…ôr …Ďňźskt √į…ô kl…Ďňźs t…ô ňą…Ďňźns…ô √į…ô l…Ďňźst ňąkw…õst É…ôn b…™ňąf…Ēňź h…Ďňźf p…Ďňźst f…Ēňź/

**Fenomenos:**
- Broad A: "asked" /…Ďňźskt/, "class" /kl…Ďňźs/, "answer" /ňą…Ďňźns…ô/, "last" /l…Ďňźst/, "half" /h…Ďňźf/, "past" /p…Ďňźst/
- R no rotica: "teacher" /ňątiňźt É…ô/, "answer" /ňą…Ďňźns…ô/, "before" /b…™ňąf…Ēňź/, "four" /f…Ēňź/

#### Oracion 7: Entonacion britanica

**Formal:**
"I'm not entirely sure, but I think the restaurant is somewhere near the station."

**Natural:**
"I'm not entirely sure, but I think the restaurant's somewhere near the station."

**IPA britanico:**
/a…™m n…ít …™nňąta…™…ôli  É…Ēňź b…ôt a…™ őł…™Ňčk √į…ô ňąr…õstr…ínts ňąs Ćmwe…ô n…™…ô √į…ô ňąste…™ É…ôn/

**Fenomenos:**
- "sure" / É…Ēňź/ (no / É är/)
- "restaurant" /ňąr…õstr…ínt/ (3 silabas en britanico, la "au" no se pronuncia)
- "somewhere" /ňąs Ćmwe…ô/ (R no rotica)
- "near" /n…™…ô/ (R no rotica)

#### Oracion 8: Vocabulario britanico

**Formal:**
"We queued for ages at the chemist's, then popped into the shop for a tin of biscuits."

**Natural:**
"We queued for ages at the chemist's, then popped into the shop for a tin of biscuits."

**IPA britanico:**
/wiňź kjuňźd f…ôr ňąe…™d í…™z …ôt √į…ô ňąk…õm…™sts √į…õn p…ípt ňą…™nt…ô √į…ô  É…íp f…ôr …ô t…™n …ôv ňąb…™sk…™ts/

**Fenomenos:**
- Vocabulario britanico: "queued" (hicimos cola), "chemist's" (farmacia), "popped into" (entrar rapidamente), "tin" (lata), "biscuits" (galletas)
- O abierta: "shop" / É…íp/, "popped" /p…ípt/

#### Oracion 9: U larga + Rasgos mixtos

**Formal:**
"On Tuesday, the students were given a new opportunity to study music at the university."

**Natural:**
"On Tuesday, the students were given a new opportunity t…ô study music at the university."

**IPA britanico:**
/…ín ňątjuňźzde…™ √į…ô ňąstjuňźd…ônts w…ô ňą…°…™v…ôn …ô njuňź ňĆ…íp…ôňątjuňźn…™ti t…ô ňąst Ćdi ňąmjuňźz…™k …ôt √į…ô ňĆjuňźn…™ňąv…úňźs…™ti/

**Fenomenos:**
- /j/ antes de /uňź/: "Tuesday" /ňątjuňźzde…™/, "students" /ňąstjuňźd…ônts/, "new" /njuňź/, "opportunity" /ňĆ…íp…ôňątjuňźn…™ti/, "music" /ňąmjuňźz…™k/, "university" /ňĆjuňźn…™ňąv…úňźs…™ti/
- En americano: "Tuesday" /ňątuňźzde…™/, "students" /ňąstuňźd…ônts/, "new" /nuňź/

**Formal:**

### Ejercicio 4: Lectura con acento britanico

Lee este texto intentando usar rasgos britanicos (broad A, R no rotica, linking R).

> "Last Saturday, after a long bath, I walked to the park near my father's house. The grass was rather lovely. I sat in the garden and had a glass of water. The weather was beautiful ‚ÄĒ not a cloud in the sky. I thought about going for a drive, but it was half past four and I was rather tired."

Palabras clave con broad A: last, bath, park, father's, grass, rather, garden, glass, half, past
Palabras con R no rotica: after, water, weather, four, rather, tired

## Repaso

1. **R no rotica**: en ingles britanico, la /r/ no se pronuncia despues de vocal ‚ÄĒ "car" = /k…Ďňź/, "teacher" = /ňątiňźt É…ô/
2. **Broad A**: "bath, glass, grass, last, path, ask" se dicen con /…Ďňź/ en britanico, /√¶/ en americano
3. **T glotal**: en habla informal britanica, /t/ entre vocales ‚Üí / Ē/ ‚ÄĒ "butter" = /ňąb Ć Ē…ô/
4. **Linking R**: cuando una palabra termina en vocal y la siguiente empieza con vocal, aparece una /r/ ‚ÄĒ "idea of" = /a…™ňąd…™…ôr …ôv/
5. **/juňź/** tras consonantes: "Tuesday" = /ňątjuňźzde…™/, "new" = /njuňź/ en britanico
