# Unit 5: Dictation — BBC 6 Minute English Style

## Objetivo

Practicar la comprension auditiva con material de tipo educativo britanico a velocidad moderada. Trabajaras con 10 oraciones basadas en el estilo de programas como BBC 6 Minute English y BBC Learning English, enfocandote en identificar formas reducidas, contracciones y vocabulary en contexto.

## Contenido

### Antes de empezar

La estrategia para dictation es:
1. **Primera escucha** (lee en voz alta la version "Natural"): capta la idea general.
2. **Segunda escucha**: intenta escribir las palabras exactas.
3. **Tercera escucha**: verifica y corrige.
4. **Compara** con la version completa.

Lee cada version "Natural" en voz alta a velocidad rapida. Luego intenta escribir la version "Formal" sin mirarla.

### Las 10 oraciones

---

#### Oracion 1: Medio ambiente

**Formal (texto completo):**
"Scientists have discovered that the oceans are warming faster than we previously thought."

**Natural (como suena):**
"Scientists've discovered that the oceans're warming faster than we previously thought."

**IPA natural:**
/ˈsaɪəntɪsts.əv dɪˈskʌvərd ðət ði ˈoʊʃənz.ər ˈwɔːrmɪŋ ˈfæstər ðən wiː ˈpriːviəsli θɔːt/

**Fenomenos presentes:**
- "have" → /əv/ (contraccion avanzada)
- "that" → /ðət/ (forma debil)
- "than" → /ðən/ (forma debil)

---

#### Oracion 2: Tecnologia

**Formal:**
"More and more people are using their phones to pay for things instead of cash."

**Natural:**
"More'n more people're using their phones tə pay for things instead of cash."

**IPA natural:**
/ˈmɔːr.ən mɔːr ˈpiːpəl.ər ˈjuːzɪŋ ðər foʊnz tə peɪ fər θɪŋz ɪnˈstɛd əv kæʃ/

**Fenomenos presentes:**
- "and" → /ən/ (forma debil)
- "are" → /ər/ (forma debil)
- "to" → /tə/ (forma debil)
- "for" → /fər/ (forma debil)
- "of" → /əv/ (forma debil)

---

#### Oracion 3: Salud

**Formal:**
"Research suggests that getting enough sleep is just as important as eating well and exercising."

**Natural:**
"Research suggests thət getting enough sleep's just as important as eating well'n exercising."

**IPA natural:**
/rɪˈsɜːrtʃ səˈdʒɛsts ðət ˈɡɛtɪŋ ɪˈnʌf sliːps dʒʌst əz ɪmˈpɔːrtənt əz ˈiːtɪŋ wɛl.ən ˈɛksərsaɪzɪŋ/

**Fenomenos presentes:**
- "that" → /ðət/ (schwa)
- "is" se une a "sleep" → "sleep's"
- "as" → /əz/ (forma debil)
- "and" → /ən/ (forma debil)

---

#### Oracion 4: Cultura

**Formal:**
"The number of people who are learning a second language has been increasing steadily."

**Natural:**
"The number of people who're learning a second language's been increasing steadily."

**IPA natural:**
/ðə ˈnʌmbər əv ˈpiːpəl huːr ˈlɜːrnɪŋ ə ˈsɛkənd ˈlæŋɡwɪdʒ.əz bɪn ɪnˈkriːsɪŋ ˈstɛdɪli/

**Fenomenos presentes:**
- "of" → /əv/ (forma debil)
- "who are" → /huːr/ (fusion)
- "has" → /əz/ (forma debil, se une a "language")

---

#### Oracion 5: Trabajo

**Formal:**
"A lot of companies have started to offer remote working options to their employees."

**Natural:**
"A lotta companies've started tə offer remote working options tə their employees."

**IPA natural:**
/ə ˈlɒtə ˈkʌmpəniz.əv ˈstɑːrtɪd tə ˈɒfər rɪˈmoʊt ˈwɜːrkɪŋ ˈɒpʃənz tə ðər ɪmˈplɔɪiːz/

**Fenomenos presentes:**
- "a lot of" → /ə ˈlɒtə/ (fusion)
- "have" → /əv/ (contraccion)
- "to" → /tə/ (forma debil, dos veces)
- "their" → /ðər/ (forma debil)

---

#### Oracion 6: Educacion

**Formal:**
"Students who read for pleasure tend to do better in all subjects, not just English."

**Natural:**
"Students who read for pleasure tend tə do better in all subjects, not just English."

**IPA natural:**
/ˈstjuːdənts huː rɛd fər ˈplɛʒər tɛnd tə duː ˈbɛtər ɪn ɔːl ˈsʌbdʒɪkts nɒt dʒʌst ˈɪŋɡlɪʃ/

**Fenomenos presentes:**
- "for" → /fər/ (forma debil)
- "to" → /tə/ (forma debil)
- "not just" → la /t/ de "not" puede desaparecer (elision): /nɒ dʒʌs/

---

#### Oracion 7: Sociedad

**Formal:**
"It is estimated that around a third of all food produced in the world is wasted."

**Natural:**
"It's estimated thət around a third of all food produced in the world's wasted."

**IPA natural:**
/ɪts ˈɛstɪmeɪtɪd ðət əˈraʊnd ə θɜːrd əv ɔːl fuːd prəˈdjuːst ɪn ðə wɜːrldz ˈweɪstɪd/

**Fenomenos presentes:**
- "it is" → "it's"
- "that" → /ðət/ (schwa)
- "of" → /əv/ (forma debil)
- "is" se une a "world" → "world's"

---

#### Oracion 8: Ciencia

**Formal:**
"Experts have been warning about the effects of climate change for decades, but not everyone has been listening."

**Natural:**
"Experts've been warning about the effects of climate change for decades, but not everyone's been listening."

**IPA natural:**
/ˈɛkspɜːrts.əv bɪn ˈwɔːrnɪŋ əˈbaʊt ðɪ ɪˈfɛkts əv ˈklaɪmət tʃeɪndʒ fər ˈdɛkeɪdz bət nɒt ˈɛvriˌwʌnz bɪn ˈlɪsənɪŋ/

**Fenomenos presentes:**
- "have" → /əv/ (contraccion)
- "about" → /əˈbaʊt/ (schwa inicial)
- "of" → /əv/ (forma debil)
- "for" → /fər/ (forma debil)
- "but" → /bət/ (forma debil)
- "everyone" + "has" → "everyone's"

---

#### Oracion 9: Viaje

**Formal:**
"Travelling by train can often be cheaper and more comfortable than flying, especially for shorter distances."

**Natural:**
"Travelling by train can often be cheaper'n more comfortable than flying, especially for shorter distances."

**IPA natural:**
/ˈtrævəlɪŋ baɪ treɪn kən ˈɒfən biː ˈtʃiːpər.ən mɔːr ˈkʌmftərbəl ðən ˈflaɪɪŋ ɪˈspɛʃəli fər ˈʃɔːrtər ˈdɪstənsɪz/

**Fenomenos presentes:**
- "can" → /kən/ (forma debil)
- "often" → /ˈɒfən/ (la /t/ es muda — elision)
- "and" → /ən/ (forma debil)
- "comfortable" → /ˈkʌmftərbəl/ (elision de silaba)
- "than" → /ðən/ (forma debil)
- "especially" → /ɪˈspɛʃəli/ (la primera "e" se reduce)

---

#### Oracion 10: Futuro

**Formal:**
"In the next ten years, artificial intelligence is going to change the way we work, learn, and communicate."

**Natural:**
"In the next ten years, artificial intelligence's gonna change the way we work, learn, and communicate."

**IPA natural:**
/ɪn ðə nɛks tɛn jɪrz ˌɑːrtɪˈfɪʃəl ɪnˈtɛlɪdʒəns.ɪz ˈɡʌnə tʃeɪndʒ ðə weɪ wiː wɜːrk lɜːrn ən kəˈmjuːnɪkeɪt/

**Fenomenos presentes:**
- "next" → /nɛks/ (elision de /t/ antes de /t/)
- "is going to" → "'s gonna"
- "and" → /ən/ (forma debil)

## Ejercicios

### Ejercicio 1: Dictation clasico

Cubre la columna "Formal" y lee la version "Natural" en voz alta. Luego escribe la version formal completa.

Practica con las oraciones 1, 3, 5, 7 y 10 primero (son las mas accesibles).

### Ejercicio 2: Identifica los fenomenos

Para cada oracion, lista al menos 3 fenomenos de reduccion (formas debiles, contracciones, elision, etc.).

Ejemplo para la Oracion 1:
1. "have" → /əv/
2. "that" → /ðət/
3. "than" → /ðən/

Ahora haz lo mismo para las oraciones 4, 6, 8 y 9.

### Ejercicio 3: Velocidad progresiva

Lee la oracion 2 en tres velocidades:
1. **Lenta**: pronuncia cada palabra claramente, forma completa.
2. **Normal**: usa formas debiles pero articula bien.
3. **Rapida**: usa todas las fusiones y reducciones.

Repite con las oraciones 5 y 8.

### Ejercicio 4: Reconstruccion

Solo tienes la IPA. Escribe la oracion en ingles.

1. /ə ˈlɒtə ˈkʌmpəniz.əv ˈstɑːrtɪd tə ˈɒfər rɪˈmoʊt ˈwɜːrkɪŋ/
2. /ˈɛkspɜːrts.əv bɪn ˈwɔːrnɪŋ əˈbaʊt ðɪ ɪˈfɛkts əv ˈklaɪmət tʃeɪndʒ/
3. /ɪts ˈɛstɪmeɪtɪd ðət əˈraʊnd ə θɜːrd əv ɔːl fuːd ɪz ˈweɪstɪd/

<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. "A lot of companies have started to offer remote working."
2. "Experts have been warning about the effects of climate change."
3. "It is estimated that around a third of all food is wasted."

## Repaso

1. En material educativo britanico, las formas debiles de "that", "for", "of", "than", "and" son constantes: /ðət/, /fər/, /əv/, /ðən/, /ən/
2. "have" despues de sustantivos/pronombres se reduce a /əv/: "scientists've", "experts've"
3. "a lot of" siempre suena /ə ˈlɒtə/ — fusion completa
4. "comfortable" incluso en habla educativa se pronuncia con 3 silabas: /ˈkʌmftərbəl/
5. La estrategia de dictation es: idea general → palabras exactas → verificacion
