# Unit 9: Debates entre Nativos — Turnos, Interrupciones y Backchanneling

## Objetivo

Comprender conversaciones entre multiples hablantes nativos que hablan simultaneamente, se interrumpen, usan senales de turno y backchanneling (senales de que estas escuchando). En un debate real entre nativos, rara vez hay silencio entre turnos — los hablantes se solapan, interrumpen y responden con sonidos minimos. Dominar esto es esencial para participar en reuniones, debates y conversaciones grupales a nivel C1.

## Contenido

### Senales de turno (Turn-taking signals)

Estas son las formas en que los nativos indican que quieren hablar, que van a terminar, o que ceden el turno.

| Senal | Funcion | Ejemplo | Nota |
|---|---|---|---|
| Well, ... | Tomar el turno suavemente | "Well, I think that's not quite right." | Pausa minima antes de hablar |
| Actually, ... | Corregir/contradecir | "Actually, the data shows the opposite." | Senal de desacuerdo educado |
| Sorry, but ... | Interrumpir con cortesia | "Sorry, but can I just jump in here?" | Muy comun en reuniones formales |
| If I may ... | Pedir turno formal | "If I may, I'd like to address that point." | Registro formal/academico |
| Hold on ... | Interrumpir directamente | "Hold on, that's not what I said." | Mas directo, puede sonar brusco |
| So basically ... | Resumir y tomar control | "So basically what you're saying is..." | Tecnica de poder conversacional |
| Let me finish ... | Defender tu turno | "Let me finish — I haven't made my point yet." | Cuando te interrumpen |

### Backchanneling — Senales de escucha activa

Los nativos producen sonidos constantemente mientras el otro habla. NO son interrupciones.

| Senal | Significado | Frecuencia |
|---|---|---|
| Uh-huh | Estoy escuchando, continua | Muy frecuente |
| Mm-hmm | Estoy de acuerdo / Entiendo | Muy frecuente |
| Right | Entiendo ese punto | Frecuente |
| Yeah | Estoy siguiendo | Frecuente |
| Sure | Acepto eso | Moderada |
| Exactly | Estoy totalmente de acuerdo | Moderada |
| I see | Entiendo (puede ser neutro) | Moderada |
| Fair enough | Acepto tu punto aunque no estoy 100% de acuerdo | Moderada |
| That's a good point | Reconozco tu argumento | Menos frecuente |

### Vocabulario de desacuerdo a velocidad nativa

| Expresion | Nivel de desacuerdo | Ejemplo en contexto |
|---|---|---|
| I see what you mean, but... | Suave | "I see what you mean, but the evidence doesn't support that." |
| I take your point, however... | Suave-medio | "I take your point, however we can't ignore the cost." |
| With all due respect... | Medio (a menudo sarcástico) | "With all due respect, that's a gross oversimplification." |
| I beg to differ | Medio-fuerte | "I beg to differ — the statistics tell a different story." |
| That's a bit of a stretch | Fuerte | "That's a bit of a stretch, don't you think?" |
| I couldn't disagree more | Muy fuerte | "I couldn't disagree more with that assessment." |
| That's simply not the case | Muy fuerte | "That's simply not the case, and here's why." |
| Come on, that's ridiculous | Informal/fuerte | "Oh, come on, that's ridiculous." |

### Debate simulado: Transcripcion

Lee esta transcripcion de un debate entre tres personas sobre trabajo remoto. Las marcas [overlap] indican habla simultanea.

**MODERATOR (M):** So the question is: should companies mandate a return to the office? Sarah, let's start with you.

**SARAH (S):** Well, I think the evidence is pretty clear at this point. Productivity has not dropped — if anything, it's [overlap] gone up —

**JAMES (J):** [overlap] Sorry, but that — that really depends on the industry, doesn't it?

**S:** Let me finish — I was going to say, it's gone up in knowledge-based roles specifically. I take your point about industry differences, but the blanket mandates we're seeing are — they're not based on data, they're based on control.

**J:** With all due respect, that's a bit of a stretch. Managers aren't trying to "control" people. They're trying to [overlap] maintain culture and —

**S:** [overlap] Mm-hmm —

**M:** Hold on, let James finish his point.

**J:** Thank you. They're trying to maintain company culture, which is genuinely harder when everyone's remote. You can't build relationships over Zoom. Period.

**S:** I beg to differ. I've built stronger relationships with my remote team than I ever did in an office. The idea that you need to be physically present to — to bond — that's, if I may, a very outdated [overlap] perspective.

**J:** [overlap] Outdated? Come on — there's decades of research on the value of face-to-face [overlap] interaction —

**S:** [overlap] Sure, but the world has changed! We have tools now that didn't exist five years ago.

**M:** Fair enough. Let's hear from both sides on the compromise: hybrid models. James?

**J:** So basically what I'm saying is, a hybrid model — three days in, two days remote — gives you the best of both worlds. That's a reasonable middle ground.

**S:** Actually, I'd say two days in and three remote is closer to what employees actually want. But yeah, some form of hybrid — that's a good point, I'll give you that.

### Analisis del debate

| Elemento | Ejemplo del debate | Tecnica |
|---|---|---|
| Interrupcion cortés | "Sorry, but that really depends..." | James usa "sorry, but" |
| Defender turno | "Let me finish" | Sarah defiende su turno |
| Backchanneling | "Mm-hmm" de Sarah | Indica escucha, no acuerdo |
| Desacuerdo suave | "I beg to differ" | Sarah discrepa formalmente |
| Desacuerdo fuerte | "Come on..." | James escala el tono |
| Concesion | "That's a good point, I'll give you that" | Sarah concede parcialmente |
| Resumen de control | "So basically what I'm saying is..." | James retoma el control |

## Repaso

1. En debates nativos, los turnos se solapan constantemente — esto es NORMAL, no falta de educacion
2. El backchanneling (uh-huh, right, sure) indica que escuchas, no que estas de acuerdo
3. Las interrupciones corteses usan "Sorry, but...", "If I may...", "Actually..."
4. El desacuerdo tiene niveles: desde "I see what you mean, but..." hasta "That's ridiculous"
5. "So basically what you're saying is..." es una tecnica de poder para tomar control de la conversacion
6. "With all due respect" frecuentemente precede a un desacuerdo FUERTE, no suave
7. Practica primero identificando estas senales como oyente antes de usarlas como hablante
