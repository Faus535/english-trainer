# Unit 11: Llamadas Telefonicas Reales — Audio Comprimido, Ruido y Velocidad Nativa

## Objetivo

Comprender conversaciones telefonicas reales en ingles, donde la calidad de audio es inferior (compresion, ruido de fondo), los hablantes no articulan tanto como en persona, y debes captar numeros, nombres y detalles criticos sin posibilidad de ver la cara del interlocutor. Las llamadas telefonicas son una de las situaciones mas dificiles de listening porque pierdes TODAS las pistas visuales.

## Contenido

### Por que las llamadas son mas dificiles

| Factor | Problema | Impacto |
|---|---|---|
| Compresion de audio | El telefono corta frecuencias altas y bajas | /s/, /f/, /θ/ suenan casi identicos |
| Sin visual | No ves los labios ni gestos | Pierdes el 30% de la comprension |
| Velocidad | La gente habla mas rapido por telefono | Quieren terminar rapido |
| Interrupciones | Ruido de fondo, cortes de senal | Pierdes palabras clave |
| Numeros | Facil confundir 15/50, 13/30 | Los numeros son criticos |

### Frases telefonicas esenciales a velocidad nativa

#### Abrir la llamada

| Frase formal | Frase informal | Pronunciacion rapida |
|---|---|---|
| Good morning, this is [name] speaking. | Hey, it's [name]. | /ɡʊd ˈmɔːrnɪŋ ðɪs ɪz/ → /ɡəˈmɔːnɪŋ ðɪsɪz/ |
| How may I help you? | What's up? | /haʊ meɪ aɪ ˈhɛlp juː/ → /haʊ mæɪ ˈhɛlpjə/ |
| I'm calling regarding... | I'm calling about... | /aɪm ˈkɔːlɪŋ rɪˈɡɑːrdɪŋ/ → /ɑːm ˈkɔːlɪn rɪˈɡɑːdɪŋ/ |
| Could I speak to...? | Is [name] there? | /kʊd aɪ ˈspiːk tuː/ → /kədaɪ ˈspiːktə/ |
| One moment, please. | Hang on a sec. | /wʌn ˈmoʊ.mənt pliːz/ → /wʌn ˈmoʊmən pliːz/ |

#### Pedir y confirmar informacion

| Frase | Funcion | Pronunciacion rapida |
|---|---|---|
| Could you spell that for me? | Pedir deletreo | /kədʒə ˈspɛl ðæt fər miː/ |
| Let me read that back to you. | Confirmar datos | /lɛmiː ˈriːd ðæt ˈbæk tə jə/ |
| Sorry, did you say 15 or 50? | Aclarar numeros | /ˈsɒri dɪdʒə seɪ fɪfˈtiːn ɔːr ˈfɪfti/ |
| Could you repeat that? | Pedir repeticion | /kədʒə rɪˈpiːt ðæt/ |
| I didn't quite catch that. | No entendiste | /aɪ ˈdɪdn kwɑɪt ˈkætʃ ðæt/ |
| You're breaking up. | Mala senal | /jər ˈbreɪkɪŋ ʌp/ |
| Can you hear me? | Comprobar conexion | /kən jə ˈhɪr miː/ |

#### Cerrar la llamada

| Formal | Informal | Nota |
|---|---|---|
| Thank you for your time. | Thanks, cheers. | "Cheers" = gracias (britanico) |
| I'll send that over by email. | I'll shoot you an email. | "shoot" = enviar rapido |
| Is there anything else I can help with? | Anything else? | Senal de cierre |
| Have a good day. | Take care. / Later. | "Later" = muy informal |

### Numeros por telefono — La trampa mas comun

Los pares que mas se confunden por telefono:

| Par confuso | Truco para distinguir | Ejemplo |
|---|---|---|
| 13 /θɜːrˈtiːn/ vs 30 /ˈθɜːrti/ | thirTEEN (acento final) vs THIRty (acento inicial) | "Flight thirTEEN" vs "Flight THIRty" |
| 14 vs 40 | fourTEEN vs FORty | Misma regla de acento |
| 15 vs 50 | fifTEEN vs FIFty | Misma regla de acento |
| 16 vs 60 | sixTEEN vs SIXty | Misma regla de acento |
| 17 vs 70 | sevenTEEN vs SEVenty | Misma regla de acento |
| 18 vs 80 | eighTEEN vs EIGHty | Misma regla de acento |
| 19 vs 90 | nineTEEN vs NINEty | Misma regla de acento |

**Regla de oro:** Los "-teen" tienen el acento al FINAL. Los "-ty" tienen el acento al PRINCIPIO.

### Deletrear nombres por telefono — NATO Phonetic Alphabet

Los nativos usan palabras para aclarar letras confusas:

| Letra | Palabra NATO | Uso comun alternativo |
|---|---|---|
| B | Bravo | B as in Boy |
| D | Delta | D as in David |
| F | Foxtrot | F as in Frank |
| M | Mike | M as in Mary |
| N | November | N as in Nancy |
| P | Papa | P as in Peter |
| S | Sierra | S as in Sam |
| T | Tango | T as in Tom |
| V | Victor | V as in Victor |

**Ejemplo real:** "My name is McNeil. That's M as in Mary, C, N as in Nancy, E-I-L."

### Llamada simulada 1: Reserva de hotel

**RECEPTIONIST (R):** Good afternoon, Grand Plaza Hotel, how may I help you?

**CALLER (C):** Hi, I'd like to book a room for next weekend, please.

**R:** Certainly. And what dates would that be?

**C:** Checking in on the fifteenth and checking out on the seventeenth.

**R:** The fifteenth to the seventeenth of March. That's two nights. And is that a single or a double?

**C:** Double, please. And could I get a room with a view if possible?

**R:** Let me check... Yes, we have a deluxe double with a city view available. That's a hundred and thirty-five per night.

**C:** Sorry, did you say a hundred and thirty-five or a hundred and fifty?

**R:** One-three-five. A hundred and thirty-five.

**C:** That's fine. Could I book that?

**R:** Of course. Can I take your name?

**C:** It's Whitfield. W-H-I-T-F as in Frank, I-E-L-D.

**R:** Let me read that back: W-H-I-T-F-I-E-L-D, Whitfield?

**C:** That's correct.

**R:** And a phone number we can reach you on?

**C:** Zero-seven-seven-zero, nine-five-three, double-four-one-eight.

**R:** Zero-seven-seven-zero, nine-five-three, four-four-one-eight. Perfect.

## Repaso

1. Las llamadas telefonicas eliminan TODAS las pistas visuales — tu oido trabaja al 100%
2. Los numeros -teen vs -ty se distinguen por el ACENTO: thirTEEN vs THIRty
3. Para confirmar numeros, repite digito a digito: "one-three-five" en vez de "a hundred thirty-five"
4. "I didn't quite catch that" es la forma mas natural de pedir repeticion
5. Deletrea usando "as in": "F as in Frank, S as in Sam"
6. "Let me read that back to you" es ESENCIAL para confirmar datos criticos
7. Las reducciones son mas extremas por telefono: "could you" → /kədʒə/, "did you" → /dɪdʒə/
