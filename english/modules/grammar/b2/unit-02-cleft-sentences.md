# Cleft Sentences — Emphasis and Focus

## Objetivo

Usar oraciones hendidas para enfatizar informacion especifica. Producir "What I need is...", "It was John who...", "The reason is..." sin dudar.

## Activacion rapida

| Tipo | Estructura | Ejemplo |
|------|-----------|---------|
| What-cleft | **What** + S + V + **is/was** + enfasis | **What I need** is a holiday. |
| It-cleft | **It** + be + enfasis + **who/that** | **It was** Maria **who** told me. |
| All-cleft | **All** + S + V + **is/was** | **All I want** is some peace and quiet. |
| The thing-cleft | **The thing/reason/place** + rel. clause | **The reason** I'm calling **is** to ask about the job. |
| The one-cleft | **The one who/that** | **The one who** helped me **was** my sister. |

**Usos reales:**
- Corregir: "Did you call her?" — "No, **it was** Tom **who** called her."
- Enfatizar: "I don't care about money. **What I care about** is my family."
- Aclarar: "**The reason** I was late **is that** my car broke down."
- Limitar: "**All** you have to do **is** sign here."

**Error hispanohablante:** Traducir literalmente "Lo que necesito es..." funciona bastante bien: "What I need is..." Pero cuidado con "All": "All what I want" es incorrecto. Es solo "All I want."

## Ejercicios de produccion

### A. Reescribe enfatizando la parte en MAYUSCULAS

1. JOHN broke the vase. (It-cleft)
2. I need MORE TIME. (What-cleft)
3. She left because SHE WAS TIRED. (The reason-cleft)
4. I just want A SIMPLE ANSWER. (All-cleft)
5. MARY helped me, not Peter. (It-cleft)

### B. Traduce al ingles usando oraciones hendidas

6. Lo que me molesta es su actitud.
7. Fue mi madre quien me enseno a cocinar.
8. Lo unico que quiero es dormir.
9. La razon por la que estoy aqui es para hablar contigo.
10. Lo que no entiendo es por que no me llamaste.

### C. Completa las frases con tu propia idea

11. What I really enjoy about my job is...
12. The reason I'm learning English is...
13. All I need to be happy is...
14. It was ___ who changed my life.

### D. Corrige los errores

15. All what I want is some rest.
16. What do I need is more practice.

---

## Respuestas

**A.**
1. It was John who broke the vase.
2. What I need is more time.
3. The reason she left was that she was tired. / The reason she left is that she was tired.
4. All I want is a simple answer.
5. It was Mary who helped me, not Peter.

**B.**
6. What bothers/annoys me is his/her attitude.
7. It was my mother who taught me to cook.
8. All I want is to sleep. / All I want is some sleep.
9. The reason I'm here is to talk to you.
10. What I don't understand is why you didn't call me.

**C.** (respuestas modelo)
11. What I really enjoy about my job is the flexibility.
12. The reason I'm learning English is that I want to work abroad.
13. All I need to be happy is good health and my family.
14. It was my English teacher who changed my life.

**D.**
15. All I want is some rest. (quitar "what")
16. What I need is more practice. (quitar "do", orden afirmativo)

## Repaso

1. Enfatiza "the food" con What-cleft: I loved the food most about the trip. → ___
2. Enfatiza "Maria" con It-cleft: Maria sent the email, not Carlos. → ___
3. Traduce: Lo unico que tienes que hacer es llamar. → ___
4. Corrige: The reason why I came is because I need help.
5. Completa: What ___ me about this city ___ the people.

**Repaso — Respuestas:**
1. What I loved most about the trip was the food.
2. It was Maria who sent the email, not Carlos.
3. All you have to do is call.
4. The reason (why) I came is **that** I need help. (no "because" despues de "reason is")
5. What **attracts/fascinates** me about this city **is** the people.
