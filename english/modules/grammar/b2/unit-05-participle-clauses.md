# Participle Clauses — Elegant Sentence Reduction

## Objetivo

Reducir clausulas relativas y adverbiales usando participios. Producir frases como "Having finished the work, she left" de forma natural.

## Activacion rapida

| Tipo | Estructura | Ejemplo | Equivalente largo |
|------|-----------|---------|------------------|
| Present participle (-ing) | **V-ing**, S + V | **Walking** home, I saw an accident. | While I was walking home... |
| Past participle (-ed/3rd) | **V-ed/pp**, S + V | **Seen** from above, the city looks tiny. | When it is seen from above... |
| Perfect participle | **Having + pp**, S + V | **Having finished** the report, she went home. | After she had finished... |
| Negative | **Not knowing/Not having** | **Not knowing** what to say, he stayed silent. | Because he didn't know... |

**Cuando usar:**
- Mismo sujeto en ambas clausulas (obligatorio)
- Escritura formal, narraciones, descripciones
- Para evitar repeticion de "because/when/while/after"

**Error hispanohablante:** Cambiar de sujeto. NO: "Walking home, **the rain** started." (la lluvia no camina). SI: "Walking home, **I** got caught in the rain."

## Ejercicios de produccion

### A. Reduce a participle clause

1. Because she didn't know anyone at the party, she felt uncomfortable.
2. After he had read the email, he called his boss immediately.
3. The building, which was designed by Gaudi, is now a UNESCO site.
4. While I was waiting for the bus, I saw my old teacher.
5. Because they had been warned about the weather, they packed warm clothes.

### B. Traduce al ingles usando participle clauses

6. Al no tener dinero, no pudo comprar el billete.
7. Visto desde lejos, el castillo parece enorme.
8. Habiendo terminado sus estudios, decidio viajar por el mundo.
9. Cansado de esperar, se fue a casa.

### C. Expande las participle clauses a oraciones completas

10. Feeling tired, she went to bed early.
11. Written in the 19th century, the novel is still popular today.
12. Having lost his wallet, he couldn't pay for dinner.

### D. Corrige los errores (dangling participles)

13. Walking through the park, the flowers were beautiful.
14. Having eaten lunch, the meeting started.
15. Driving to work, a deer crossed the road.

---

## Respuestas

**A.**
1. Not knowing anyone at the party, she felt uncomfortable.
2. Having read the email, he called his boss immediately.
3. The building, designed by Gaudi, is now a UNESCO site.
4. Waiting for the bus, I saw my old teacher.
5. Having been warned about the weather, they packed warm clothes.

**B.**
6. Not having any money, he couldn't buy the ticket. / Having no money, he couldn't buy the ticket.
7. Seen from a distance, the castle looks enormous.
8. Having finished his/her studies, he/she decided to travel the world.
9. Tired of waiting, he went home.

**C.**
10. Because she felt/was feeling tired, she went to bed early.
11. Although it was written in the 19th century, the novel is still popular today.
12. Because he had lost his wallet, he couldn't pay for dinner.

**D.**
13. Walking through the park, **I noticed that** the flowers were beautiful. / **As I walked** through the park, the flowers were beautiful.
14. **Having eaten** lunch, **we started** the meeting. / After **we had** eaten lunch, the meeting started.
15. **While I was driving** to work, a deer crossed the road. / Driving to work, **I saw** a deer cross the road.

## Repaso

1. Reduce: Because he had never been to London, he was excited about the trip. → ___
2. Reduce: The letter, which was written by hand, was difficult to read. → ___
3. Traduce: No sabiendo que decir, se quedo callado. → ___
4. Corrige el dangling participle: Arriving at the hotel, the room wasn't ready.
5. Reduce: After I had checked my emails, I started working. → ___

**Repaso — Respuestas:**
1. Never having been to London, he was excited about the trip.
2. The letter, written by hand, was difficult to read.
3. Not knowing what to say, he stayed/remained silent.
4. Arriving at the hotel, **we found that** the room wasn't ready. / When **we arrived** at the hotel, the room wasn't ready.
5. Having checked my emails, I started working.
