# B2 Grammar Review — Formal Production

## Objetivo

Demostrar dominio de todas las estructuras B2 en tareas de produccion integrada. Escribir textos formales, enfaticos y academicos con fluidez.

## Activacion rapida

| Estructura | Ejemplo clave |
|-----------|--------------|
| Inversions | Never have I seen such incompetence. |
| Cleft sentences | What we need is a new approach. |
| Future perfect / continuous | By 2030, I will have been working here for 15 years. |
| Mixed conditionals | If I had studied law, I would be a lawyer now. |
| Participle clauses | Having reviewed the data, we made our decision. |
| Subjunctive | I recommend that he be transferred. |
| Discourse markers | Nevertheless, the project proved successful. |

## Ejercicios de produccion

### Task 1: Formal Essay — "The Advantages and Disadvantages of Artificial Intelligence"

Escribe un ensayo formal (12-15 frases, 3 parrafos: introduccion, desarrollo, conclusion).

Incluye obligatoriamente:
- Al menos 2 inversiones (Never, Not only, Rarely...)
- Al menos 2 cleft sentences (What... is, It was... that)
- Al menos 4 discourse markers avanzados (furthermore, nevertheless, consequently...)
- Al menos 1 subjunctivo (It is essential that...)
- Al menos 1 participle clause (Having considered..., Seen from...)

### Task 2: Formal Letter of Complaint

Escribe una carta formal (8-10 frases) quejandote de un servicio malo en un hotel.

Incluye:
- Al menos 1 inversion (At no point, Under no circumstances, Not only)
- Al menos 2 discourse markers formales
- Al menos 1 cleft sentence
- Al menos 1 mixed conditional (If the service had been better, I would...)
- Al menos 1 subjuntivo o participle clause

### Task 3: Quick-Fire Transformations

Reescribe cada frase de forma mas sofisticada:

1. I have never been so disappointed. → (inversion) ___
2. I need more information. That's what I need. → (cleft) ___
3. By next year, she will be working here for 10 years. → (future perfect continuous) ___
4. If I had been more careful, I wouldn't have this problem now. → (mixed conditional — confirma que es correcta o corrige) ___
5. After he had finished the presentation, he answered questions. → (participle clause) ___
6. I suggest he goes home early. → (subjuntivo formal) ___
7. The results were bad. But we learned a lot. → (discourse marker) ___
8. She not only won the prize. She also broke the record. → (inversion) ___
9. I don't understand why they cancelled. That's the thing. → (cleft) ___
10. The project is expensive. Also, it will take too long. → (discourse marker) ___

## Respuestas

### Task 1 (respuesta modelo)

Artificial intelligence has become one of the most discussed topics in recent years. **What makes this technology so controversial** is its potential to transform virtually every aspect of our lives. **Not only does** AI offer remarkable efficiency gains, **but** it also raises profound ethical questions.

**On the one hand**, AI can automate repetitive tasks, **thereby** freeing workers to focus on more creative endeavours. **Furthermore**, in fields such as medicine and environmental science, AI has already demonstrated its capacity to save lives and resources. **Having analysed** vast amounts of data in seconds, AI systems can identify patterns that would take humans years to discover.

**Nevertheless**, the rapid development of AI poses significant risks. **Rarely has** a technology threatened to displace so many jobs in such a short period. **It is essential that** governments and industry leaders work together to ensure a responsible transition. **In light of** these challenges, **what we need** is not less innovation, **but rather** more thoughtful regulation. **All in all**, while the benefits are undeniable, the risks must be carefully managed.

### Task 2 (respuesta modelo)

Dear Sir/Madam,

I am writing **with regard to** my recent stay at your hotel on 15-17 March. **What I expected** was a professional and comfortable experience; unfortunately, **what I received** was quite the opposite. **Not only was** the room unclean upon arrival, **but** the air conditioning was also not functioning. **Having reported** these issues to reception, I was told that no alternative rooms were available. **At no point did** any member of staff apologise or offer compensation. **Furthermore**, the noise from the construction next door made it impossible to sleep. If the service **had been** as advertised, I **would be** recommending your hotel to my colleagues. **Nevertheless**, I must insist that a full refund **be** issued. I trust you will address these concerns promptly.

Yours faithfully,
[Name]

### Task 3

1. **Never have I been** so disappointed.
2. **What I need** is more information.
3. By next year, she **will have been working** here for 10 years.
4. Correcta. (If I had been more careful [past], I wouldn't have this problem now [present] = mixed conditional valida)
5. **Having finished** the presentation, he answered questions.
6. I suggest he **go** home early. (subjuntivo: sin "s")
7. The results were bad. **Nevertheless / Nonetheless**, we learned a great deal.
8. **Not only did** she win the prize, **but** she also broke the record.
9. **What I don't understand** is why they cancelled. / **The thing** I don't understand **is** why they cancelled.
10. The project is expensive. **Moreover / Furthermore**, it will take too long.
