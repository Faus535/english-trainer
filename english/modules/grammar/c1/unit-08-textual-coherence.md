# Unit 08 — Coherencia Textual: Referencia, Sustitucion y Flujo de Informacion

## Objetivo

Escribir y hablar con cohesion real, no solo usando conectores. En C1 necesitas dominar las herramientas "invisibles" que hacen que un texto fluya: referencia, sustitucion, elipsis, cadenas lexicas y estructura tema-rema. Sin estas herramientas, tu ingles suena como una lista de frases sueltas. Con ellas, suena como un hablante avanzado que organiza sus ideas de forma natural.

## 1. Referencia: conectar ideas sin repetir

### Pronombres y determinantes de referencia

| Recurso | Ejemplo | Funcion |
|---|---|---|
| this / these | We launched a new product. **This** generated huge interest. | Referencia a lo inmediatamente anterior |
| that / those | Early results were promising. **Those** findings confirmed our hypothesis. | Referencia a algo mas distante o formal |
| the former / the latter | We interviewed Smith and Jones. **The former** was more experienced; **the latter** was more creative. | Distinguir dos elementos mencionados |
| such | The system crashed three times. **Such** failures are unacceptable. | Referencia clasificadora (= de este tipo) |
| the above / the aforementioned | **The above** examples illustrate the point clearly. | Registro formal/academico |
| the following | Please note **the following** changes to the schedule. | Referencia catacuatorial (a lo que viene despues) |

**Error hispanohablante:** Repetir el sustantivo en vez de usar referencia.
- Malo: "The report was incomplete. The report needed more data. The report was rejected."
- Bien: "The report was incomplete. **It** needed more data and was **consequently** rejected."

### Ejemplo en contexto

> "The company launched two products last quarter: a fitness tracker and a smartwatch. **The former** targeted health-conscious consumers, while **the latter** was aimed at tech enthusiasts. **Both** exceeded sales expectations, though **the latter** by a wider margin. **Such** results suggest a growing demand for wearable technology."

## 2. Sustitucion: reemplazar sin repetir

La sustitucion permite evitar la repeticion usando palabras "comodin":

| Tipo | Recurso | Ejemplo |
|---|---|---|
| Verbal | do so / do the same | "She asked me to review the document and I **did so** immediately." |
| Verbal | do it / do that | "I'll handle the presentation." — "Can you really **do that** in one day?" |
| Nominal | one / ones | "Which laptop? The black **one** or the silver **one**?" |
| Nominal | the same | "He ordered a coffee. I ordered **the same**." |
| Clausal | so / not | "Will it rain tomorrow?" — "I think **so**." / "I hope **not**." |
| Clausal | if so / if not | "Check if the file exists. **If so**, open it. **If not**, create a new one." |

### "So" y "not" como sustitutos de clausulas completas

| Verbo | Afirmativo | Negativo |
|---|---|---|
| think | I think **so**. | I don't think **so**. / I think **not**. (formal) |
| hope | I hope **so**. | I hope **not**. |
| believe | I believe **so**. | I don't believe **so**. |
| be afraid | I'm afraid **so**. | I'm afraid **not**. |
| suppose | I suppose **so**. | I suppose **not**. |
| expect | I expect **so**. | I don't expect **so**. |

**Nota:** "I think not" y "I believe not" suenan muy formales/literarios. En conversacion normal: "I don't think so."

## 3. Elipsis: omitir lo que se sobreentiende

La elipsis es la omision de palabras que el contexto hace innecesarias. Es fundamental en ingles hablado y escrito natural.

| Tipo | Ejemplo completo | Con elipsis |
|---|---|---|
| Nominal | "Some people agreed and some people didn't." | "Some people agreed and some **_** didn't." |
| Verbal | "She can speak French and he can speak German." | "She can speak French and he **_** German." |
| Clausal | "Are you coming?" — "Yes, I am coming." | "Are you coming?" — "Yes, I am **_**." |
| Tras auxiliar | "I haven't finished, but I should have finished." | "I haven't finished, but I should have **_**." |
| En comparaciones | "She works harder than he works." | "She works harder than he does **_**." |
| En respuestas cortas | "Who broke it?" — "Not me." (= It was not me who broke it.) | Omision masiva pero perfectamente natural |

### Elipsis en conversacion natural

| Formal (con todo) | Natural (con elipsis) |
|---|---|
| Would you like to come? | Want to come? / Wanna come? |
| Have you seen it? | Seen it? |
| I have not done it yet. | Not yet. / Haven't yet. |
| Is there anything else? | Anything else? |
| It does not matter. | Doesn't matter. |

**Error hispanohablante:** Ser demasiado explicito. En ingles natural, la elipsis es la norma, no la excepcion. Si repites todo, suenas robotico.

## 4. Tema y rema: el flujo de informacion

### El principio

- **Tema** = lo que ya se conoce (va al principio)
- **Rema** = la informacion nueva (va al final)

El ingles sigue un patron natural: informacion conocida → informacion nueva.

| Flujo pobre | Flujo natural |
|---|---|
| A new virus was discovered. Scientists are worried about **a new virus**. | A new virus was discovered. **The virus** has been linked to respiratory illness. |
| The company lost money. **The company** also fired 200 employees. | The company lost money. **As a result**, 200 employees were let go. |
| Innovation is important. **Innovation** drives growth. **Innovation** creates jobs. | Innovation is important. **It** drives growth and creates jobs. |

### Tecnicas para mantener el flujo

| Tecnica | Ejemplo |
|---|---|
| Repeticion con variacion | "The study examined 500 patients. **These participants** were divided into two groups." |
| Pronominalizacion | "The CEO announced the merger. **She** expressed confidence in the outcome." |
| Sinonimos y reformulaciones | "Unemployment rose sharply. **This increase** affected rural areas the most." |
| Pasiva para mantener el tema | "The vaccine was developed in 2020. **It** was subsequently approved by the FDA." (sin cambiar de sujeto) |
| Tematizacion con adverbiales | "The economy recovered in Q3. **During this period**, consumer spending also increased." |

## 5. Dispositivos cohesivos avanzados

### Cadenas lexicas

Una cadena lexica es una secuencia de palabras relacionadas semanticamente que dan cohesion al texto:

> "The **storm** caused widespread **damage**. **Winds** of over 100 mph **battered** the coast. **Flooding** followed, and **rescue** teams were deployed. The **devastation** was unprecedented."

Palabras de la cadena: storm → damage → winds → battered → flooding → rescue → devastation. Todas pertenecen al mismo campo semantico.

### Estructuras paralelas

El paralelismo da ritmo y cohesion:

| Sin paralelismo | Con paralelismo |
|---|---|
| She likes reading, to swim, and going for walks. | She likes **reading**, **swimming**, and **walking**. |
| The plan was ambitious, it lacked resources, and nobody supported it. | The plan was **ambitious but underfunded** and **unsupported**. |
| We need to reduce costs, improving quality is also important, and we should retain staff. | We need to **reduce costs**, **improve quality**, and **retain staff**. |

## Dialogos

### Dialogo 1 — Reunion de trabajo

> **Boss:** Have you reviewed the proposal?
> **You:** I have, yes. The first section is solid, but the latter part needs work. Such weaknesses could undermine the whole thing.
> **Boss:** Can you fix it by Friday?
> **You:** I think so. What concerns me is the budget section — the figures don't add up. I'll revise those and do the same for the timeline.
> **Boss:** Good. If so, we can present it on Monday.

### Dialogo 2 — Discusion academica

> **Colleague:** Did you read both articles?
> **You:** I did. The former was more theoretical, while the latter had stronger empirical evidence. Both make interesting points, though.
> **Colleague:** Do you think the methodology was sound?
> **You:** In the first article, I believe so. In the second, I'm not entirely convinced. The above issues aside, the findings are promising. Such research warrants further investigation.

## Practica

### A. Sustituye las repeticiones con recursos de referencia

Reescribe este parrafo eliminando la repeticion excesiva:

> "The government introduced a new policy. The policy aimed to reduce emissions. The policy was controversial. Critics of the policy argued that the policy would hurt the economy. Supporters of the policy claimed that the policy was necessary."

<details>
<summary>Respuesta</summary>

> "The government introduced a new policy aimed at reducing emissions. **It** was controversial. **Critics** argued that **it** would hurt the economy, while **supporters** claimed **it** was necessary."

Recursos usados: pronominalizacion (it), elipsis (aimed at reducing — clausula reducida), contraste (critics/supporters sin repetir "of the policy").

</details>

### B. Completa con so, not, do so, one, the same, the former, the latter

1. "Will the project be finished on time?" — "I hope ___."
2. They asked me to submit the report and I ___ immediately.
3. We tested two methods. ___ was cheaper; ___ was more reliable.
4. "Is the meeting cancelled?" — "I'm afraid ___."
5. "Which shirt do you prefer?" — "The blue ___."
6. He ordered a beer. She ordered ___.

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. I hope **so**.
2. I **did so** immediately.
3. **The former** was cheaper; **the latter** was more reliable.
4. I'm afraid **so**.
5. The blue **one**.
6. She ordered **the same**.

</details>

### C. Reorganiza para mejorar el flujo tema-rema

Reescribe estas frases para que la informacion fluya naturalmente (conocido → nuevo):

1. "A brilliant scientist was Marie Curie. She discovered radium. It was in 1898."
2. "The server crashed. Hackers were suspected by the IT team. The company lost data."
3. "Sales increased. The new marketing campaign caused the increase. The CEO was pleased by the increase."

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. "Marie Curie was a brilliant scientist. She discovered radium in 1898."
2. "The server crashed, and the IT team suspected hackers. As a result, the company lost significant data."
3. "The new marketing campaign led to a significant increase in sales. The CEO was pleased with these results."

</details>

### D. Identifica los recursos cohesivos

Lee este parrafo e identifica: 2 referencias, 1 sustitucion, 1 elipsis y 1 estructura paralela.

> "The university launched a new scholarship program. It was designed to support students from low-income families. The former system had been criticized for being too restrictive. Students who applied were required to submit transcripts, write an essay, and attend an interview. Many did, and the results were encouraging."

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

- **Referencias:** "It" (= the program), "The former system" (= el sistema anterior)
- **Sustitucion:** "Many did" (= many applied)
- **Elipsis:** "Many did _" (omision de "apply")
- **Estructura paralela:** "submit transcripts, write an essay, and attend an interview"

</details>

### E. Traduce al ingles con cohesion

1. El primero era mas caro; el segundo era mas practico.
2. Me pidieron que revisara el informe y lo hice inmediatamente.
3. "¿Crees que aprobaremos?" — "Espero que si."
4. Tales resultados sugieren un cambio de tendencia.
5. La empresa lanzo un producto nuevo. Este genero gran interes en el mercado.

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. The former was more expensive; the latter was more practical.
2. They asked me to review the report and I did so immediately.
3. "Do you think we'll pass?" — "I hope so."
4. Such results suggest a change in trend.
5. The company launched a new product. This generated significant interest in the market.

</details>

### F. Produccion: escribe un parrafo cohesivo

Elige un tema (tu empresa, tu ciudad, un problema social) y escribe 6-8 frases usando al menos:
- 2 recursos de referencia (this/that/the former/the latter/such)
- 1 sustitucion (do so / one / the same / so/not)
- 1 elipsis natural
- Buen flujo tema-rema (informacion conocida al principio, nueva al final)

## Repaso

| Concepto | Ejemplo clave | Error tipico |
|---|---|---|
| Referencia con this/such | **Such** failures are unacceptable. | Repetir el sustantivo: "These failure failures..." |
| The former / the latter | **The former** was cheaper; **the latter** more reliable. | No saber cual es cual (former = primero) |
| Sustitucion con "do so" | I was asked to review it and **did so**. | Decir "I did it" cuando "did so" es mas formal |
| Elipsis natural | "Want to come?" en vez de "Do you want to come?" | Ser demasiado explicito y sonar robotico |
| Flujo tema-rema | Conocido al principio, nuevo al final | Empezar frases con informacion nueva |
| Estructuras paralelas | **Reduce** costs, **improve** quality, **retain** staff. | Mezclar formas: "reduce, improving, and to retain" |
