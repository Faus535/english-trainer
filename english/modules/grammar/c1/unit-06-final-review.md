# C1 Final Review — Comprehensive Production Test

## Objetivo

Demostrar dominio C1 completo: registro, hedging, nominalizaciones, naturalidad y ausencia de errores fosilizados. Si puedes hacer estas tareas con fluidez, tu gramatica esta al nivel C1.

## Activacion rapida

| Competencia C1 | Indicador |
|---------------|-----------|
| Register | Puedes decir lo mismo de 3 maneras (informal/neutro/formal) |
| Hedging | Tus claims academicas no son absolutas |
| Nominalizations | Puedes escribir prosa densa cuando el contexto lo requiere |
| Ellipsis | Tus respuestas suenan naturales, no roboticas |
| Error-free | Has eliminado errores fosilizados de hispanohablante |
| Inversions (B2) | Las produces sin pensar |
| Cleft/Emphasis (B2) | Puedes enfocar la atencion donde quieras |

## Ejercicios de produccion

### Task 1: Formal Letter — Write to a university requesting admission

Escribe una carta formal (10-12 frases) a una universidad solicitando admision a un programa de master.

Incluye:
- Registro formal consistente
- Al menos 2 nominalizaciones
- Al menos 2 hedging expressions
- Al menos 1 inversion o cleft sentence
- 0 errores fosilizados de hispanohablante

### Task 2: Opinion Essay — "Should governments invest more in renewable energy?"

Escribe un ensayo de 3 parrafos (15-18 frases total).

Incluye:
- Registro academico formal
- Al menos 4 hedging expressions (could, might, arguably, it would appear...)
- Al menos 3 nominalizaciones (development, implementation, reduction...)
- Al menos 3 discourse markers avanzados (nevertheless, furthermore, consequently...)
- Al menos 1 inversion
- Al menos 1 cleft sentence

### Task 3: Complex Anecdote — Tell a story about a life-changing moment

Escribe una narracion (10-12 frases) en registro neutro-informal sobre un momento que cambio tu vida.

Incluye:
- Mezcla natural de tiempos narrativos
- Al menos 2 participle clauses
- Al menos 2 examples de ellipsis/naturalidad
- 1 mixed conditional (If I had/hadn't..., I would...)
- Variedad de conectores (no solo "and", "but", "so")

### Quick-Fire Corrections (10 exercises)

Corrige el error en cada frase:

1. The education is very important in our society.
2. She said me that she couldn't come.
3. I am agree with your opinion.
4. Actually, I am studying for my master's degree. (quiere decir "ahora mismo")
5. He suggested me to take the earlier flight.
6. I like very much Italian food.
7. She is married with a French man.
8. I don't hope so.
9. He made his homework and then went to play.
10. The people in Spain are very friendly.

## Respuestas

### Task 1 (respuesta modelo)

Dear Admissions Committee,

I am writing to express my interest in the Master's programme in International Relations at your university. Having completed my undergraduate degree in Political Science at the University of Valencia, I believe I am well prepared for the academic rigour this programme demands. Not only did my studies provide me with a strong theoretical foundation, but they also offered practical experience through a six-month internship at the Ministry of Foreign Affairs.

What particularly attracts me to your programme is its emphasis on the development of analytical and research skills. The integration of fieldwork into the curriculum would, I believe, complement my existing experience. Furthermore, I am confident that the international environment at your institution would broaden my perspective considerably.

I would be grateful for the opportunity to discuss my application in greater detail. I have enclosed my academic transcripts and a letter of recommendation. Should you require any further documentation, please do not hesitate to contact me.

Yours faithfully,
[Name]

### Task 2 (respuesta modelo)

The question of whether governments should increase their investment in renewable energy is arguably one of the most pressing issues of our time. It could be argued that the current level of funding is insufficient to address the scale of the environmental crisis. What makes this issue particularly urgent is the growing body of evidence suggesting that fossil fuel consumption is the primary driver of climate change. Furthermore, the development of renewable technologies has the potential to create millions of jobs worldwide.

Nevertheless, the implementation of large-scale renewable energy programmes is not without its challenges. The initial costs might be prohibitively high for some developing nations, and the transition could, to some extent, disrupt existing industries. Never has the need for international cooperation been so critical, as the reduction of carbon emissions requires a coordinated global effort. It would appear that, without significant investment, the achievement of climate targets set by international agreements will remain elusive.

In conclusion, while the economic challenges should not be underestimated, the long-term benefits of renewable energy investment arguably outweigh the short-term costs. There is reason to believe that governments which fail to act decisively now may face far greater economic and environmental consequences in the future. All in all, a substantial increase in renewable energy funding seems not only advisable but essential.

### Task 3 (respuesta modelo)

It happened on a Tuesday morning, about five years ago. I was sitting in my office, staring at a spreadsheet, when my phone rang. It was my old university professor, offering me a research position in London. Having spent nearly a decade in the same company, I had grown comfortable — perhaps too comfortable. I remember my hands shaking as I listened. Not knowing what to say, I asked for a week to think about it. That week was the longest of my life. On the one hand, I had a stable job and a good salary; on the other, I had always dreamt of working abroad. Eventually, I said yes. If I hadn't taken that call, I would still be sitting in that same office today. Looking back, it was the best decision I ever made — or at least, I think so.

### Quick-Fire Corrections

1. **Education** is very important in our society. (sin "the" — concepto general)
2. She **told** me that she couldn't come. (tell + persona)
3. I agree with your opinion. (sin "am")
4. **Currently**, I am studying for my master's degree. (actually = en realidad)
5. He suggested **(that) I (should) take** the earlier flight. (suggest no lleva objeto + infinitivo)
6. I really like Italian food. / I like Italian food very much. (adverbio no va entre verbo y objeto)
7. She is married **to** a French man. (married to, no with)
8. I **hope not**. (no "don't hope so")
9. He **did** his homework and then went to play. (do homework, no make)
10. **People** in Spain are very friendly. (sin "the" — generalizacion)

## Repaso

1. Escribe la misma frase en 3 registros: "This isn't working."
2. Soften: "AI will destroy millions of jobs."
3. Nominaliza: "They refused to cooperate."
4. Responde naturalmente: "Are you coming tonight?"
5. Escribe 1 frase sin NINGUN error fosilizado sobre tu aprendizaje de ingles.

**Repaso — Respuestas:**
1. Informal: This is a total mess. / Neutro: This doesn't seem to be working. / Formal: The current approach appears to be ineffective.
2. AI could potentially lead to significant job displacement in certain sectors.
3. Their refusal to cooperate complicated the negotiations.
4. "Probably." / "Depends." / "I think so." / "Not sure yet."
5. (respuesta libre — verifica: articulos, preposiciones, make/do, say/tell, falsos amigos, orden de palabras)
