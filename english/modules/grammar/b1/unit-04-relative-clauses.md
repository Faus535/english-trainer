# Relative Clauses — Connecting Ideas Fluently

## Objetivo

Combinar frases con who/which/that/where/whose sin pausas. Distinguir defining (sin comas) de non-defining (con comas) y saber cuando omitir "that".

## Activacion rapida

| Pronombre | Se refiere a | Ejemplo |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **who/that** | personas | The woman **who/that** lives next door is a doctor. |
| **which/that** | cosas | The book **which/that** you lent me is great. |
| **where** | lugares | The restaurant **where** we had dinner is closed. |
| **whose** | posesion | The man **whose** car was stolen called the police. |
| **when** | tiempo | I remember the day **when** we met. |

**Defining** (sin comas) = identifica. Es necesaria: The man **who called** is my brother.
**Non-defining** (con comas) = anade info extra. Se puede quitar: My brother, **who lives in London**, is visiting.

**Cuando omitir that/who/which:** Cuando es OBJETO (no sujeto):
- The film (that) I watched = OK omitir (I = sujeto, film = objeto)
- The man who called = NO omitir (who = sujeto)

**Error hispanohablante:** Anadir pronombres redundantes. NO: "The man who I met **him**." SI: "The man who I met."

## Ejercicios de produccion

### A. Combina las dos frases usando un pronombre relativo

1. I have a friend. She speaks five languages.
2. That's the hotel. We stayed there last summer.
3. I met a man. His wife is a famous actress.
4. She showed me the photo. She took it in Paris.
5. This is the company. I work for this company.

### B. Traduce al ingles

6. El restaurante donde cenamos era muy caro.
7. La mujer cuyo hijo gano el premio esta muy orgullosa.
8. El libro que estoy leyendo es muy interesante.
9. Madrid, que es la capital de Espana, tiene mas de 3 millones de habitantes.
10. Ese es el profesor que me ayudo con mi tesis.

### C. Decide: defining o non-defining? Anade comas si es necesario

11. My sister who lives in Barcelona is a lawyer. (tengo varias hermanas / solo una)
12. The Eiffel Tower which was built in 1889 is the most visited monument in France.

### D. Donde puedes omitir el pronombre relativo?

13. The car that I bought last week has a problem.
14. The woman who called you is waiting outside.
15. The film that won the Oscar was directed by a Spanish filmmaker.

---

## Respuestas

**A.**
1. I have a friend who/that speaks five languages.
2. That's the hotel where we stayed last summer.
3. I met a man whose wife is a famous actress.
4. She showed me the photo (that/which) she took in Paris.
5. This is the company (that/which) I work for. / ...for which I work.

**B.**
6. The restaurant where we had dinner was very expensive.
7. The woman whose son won the prize is very proud.
8. The book (that/which) I'm reading is very interesting.
9. Madrid, which is the capital of Spain, has more than 3 million inhabitants.
10. That's the teacher who helped me with my thesis.

**C.**
11. Con varias hermanas: My sister who lives in Barcelona is a lawyer. (defining, sin comas)
    Con solo una: My sister, who lives in Barcelona, is a lawyer. (non-defining, con comas)
12. The Eiffel Tower**,** which was built in 1889**,** is the most visited monument in France. (non-defining — solo hay una Torre Eiffel)

**D.**
13. SI — "that" es objeto: The car I bought last week has a problem.
14. NO — "who" es sujeto.
15. SI — "that" es objeto: The film that won the Oscar... wait — "that" es SUJETO aqui (the film won). NO se puede omitir.

## Repaso

1. Combina: I saw the man. He stole my bag. → ___
2. Traduce: La casa donde naci ya no existe. → ___
3. Omite el pronombre si es posible: The girl who I met at the party was very nice.
4. Corrige: The man who I saw him was wearing a hat.
5. Anade comas si es non-defining: Paris which is the city of love attracts millions of tourists.

**Repaso — Respuestas:**
1. I saw the man who stole my bag.
2. The house where I was born no longer exists.
3. The girl I met at the party was very nice. (omitir "who" — es objeto)
4. The man who I saw **was wearing a hat**. (quitar "him")
5. Paris**,** which is the city of love**,** attracts millions of tourists.
