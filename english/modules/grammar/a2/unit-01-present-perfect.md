# Present Perfect — Activation

## Objetivo

Usar el present perfect con fluidez en los contextos correctos: experiencias, acciones recientes, duracion. Dejar de confundirlo con el preterito espanol.

## Activacion rapida

**Estructura:** S + **have/has** + past participle

| Uso | Ejemplo | NO digas |
|-----|---------|----------|
| Experiencia (alguna vez) | I **have visited** London twice. | ~~I visited London twice.~~ (sin contexto temporal) |
| Accion reciente (just) | She **has just left**. | |
| Nunca | I **have never tried** sushi. | |
| Desde/hace (since/for) | I **have lived** here **since** 2020 / **for** 4 years. | ~~I live here since 2020.~~ |
| Ya/todavia no | I **have already** finished. / I **haven't** finished **yet**. | |

**Error clave del hispanohablante:** En espanol "He ido a Londres" y "Fui a Londres" a veces se intercambian. En ingles, **no**.
- I **have been** to London. = Tengo la experiencia (no importa cuando).
- I **went** to London last year. = Momento especifico en el pasado.

## Ejercicios de produccion

### A. Traduce al ingles

1. Has estado alguna vez en Japon?
2. Nunca he comido comida tailandesa.
3. Acabo de terminar el informe.
4. Vivimos en esta casa desde 2015.
5. Ya has hecho los deberes? — No, todavia no.

### B. Escribe sobre ti (frases completas)

6. 3 cosas que has hecho este ano.
7. 2 cosas que nunca has hecho pero te gustaria hacer.
8. Desde cuando vives en tu ciudad actual? / Desde cuando trabajas en tu empresa?

### C. Corrige los errores

9. I have went to the gym this morning.
10. She has lived here since 5 years.
11. Did you ever been to New York?

### D. Elige: Present Perfect o Past Simple?

12. I ___ (see) that film. It's great. / I ___ (see) that film last Friday.

---

### Answer Key

**A.**
1. Have you ever been to Japan?
2. I have never eaten / I've never eaten Thai food.
3. I have just finished / I've just finished the report.
4. We have lived in this house since 2015.
5. Have you already done your homework? — No, not yet. / No, I haven't finished yet.

**C.**
9. I have **been** to the gym this morning. (o "I **went** to the gym this morning" si ya paso la manana)
10. She has lived here **for** 5 years. (since + punto en el tiempo, for + duracion)
11. **Have** you **ever been** to New York?

**D.**
12. I **have seen** / I've seen that film. / I **saw** that film last Friday.

## Repaso

1. Traduce: "Cuanto tiempo llevas aprendiendo ingles?"
2. Escribe 2 cosas que has hecho hoy.
3. Corrige: "I know him since we were children."
4. Traduce: "Ella acaba de llegar — todavia no se ha sentado."
5. Since o for? "I've worked here ___ March." / "I've worked here ___ three months."

### Repaso — Answer Key

1. How long have you been learning English?
3. I **have known** him since we were children.
4. She has just arrived — she hasn't sat down yet.
5. since March / for three months.
