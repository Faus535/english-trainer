# Session 01 — Reunion de Trabajo

## Objetivo

Integrar tres habilidades en el contexto de una reunion profesional:
- **Listening**: Comprender una reunion con multiples participantes e interrupciones
- **Pronunciacion**: Entonacion profesional, stress para enfasis
- **Grammar**: Reported speech "He said that..." y voz pasiva "It was decided..."

Al final podras participar en reuniones en ingles y resumir lo que se dijo.

## Calentamiento

Piensa antes de empezar:
- ¿Has participado en alguna reunion en ingles? ¿Que fue lo mas dificil?
- ¿Sabes como interrumpir educadamente a alguien en una reunion?
- ¿Conoces la diferencia entre "He says" y "He said"?

## Listening

### Transcripcion del audio

Escucha con TTS sin leer. Hay tres personas hablando — intenta identificar quien dice que.

> **Sarah (manager):** Right, let's get started. Thanks for coming, everyone. So, the main topic today is the product launch. Tom, could you give us an update on the timeline?
> **Tom:** Sure. So, we're currently on track for a March launch. The development team has finished the core features, and we're now in the testing phase. However, there's a potential delay with the payment system — it might take an extra week.
> **Sarah:** I see. How big of a problem is that?
> **Tom:** It's not critical, but —
> **Lisa:** Sorry to interrupt, but I think the payment issue is actually quite important. Our beta testers have reported several bugs in that area.
> **Tom:** That's a fair point. I'll talk to the developers about prioritising it.
> **Sarah:** Good. Let's make that a priority. Lisa, what about the marketing side?
> **Lisa:** We've prepared the social media campaign, and the press release is almost ready. We're planning to start the campaign two weeks before launch. One thing I wanted to raise — I think we need a bigger budget for online advertising.
> **Sarah:** How much are we talking about?
> **Lisa:** About thirty percent more than originally planned.
> **Sarah:** Hmm. I'll need to discuss that with the finance team. Let's revisit that next week. Anything else?
> **Tom:** Just one thing — we need to decide on the launch event. In-person or virtual?
> **Sarah:** Good question. Let's vote on it. All in favour of in-person? ... Okay, it was decided that we'll do a hybrid event. Right, I think that covers everything. I'll send out the minutes by end of day. Thanks, everyone.

### Preguntas de comprension

1. What is the main topic of the meeting?
2. What is the potential problem Tom mentions?
3. Why does Lisa interrupt?
4. What does Lisa want for the marketing campaign?
5. What was decided about the launch event?

## Pronunciacion

### Entonacion profesional en reuniones

En reuniones, la entonacion transmite autoridad y claridad:

**Dar la palabra** (tono descendente, firme):
- "Tom, could you give us an update↘?"
- "Lisa, what about the marketing side↘?"

**Interrumpir educadamente** (tono ascendente al inicio, luego firme):
- "Sorry to inter**rupt**↗, but I think↘..."
- "If I **may**↗, I'd like to add↘..."

**Proponer algo** (tono ascendente al final = abierto a discusion):
- "I think we need a bigger budget↗?"
- "Should we consider a virtual event↗?"

**Decidir algo** (tono descendente = cerrado, definitivo):
- "Let's make that a priority↘."
- "It was decided that we'll do a hybrid event↘."

### Stress para enfasis

En reuniones, cambiar el acento de una frase cambia el significado:

| Frase | Palabra acentuada | Significado |
|-------|-------------------|-------------|
| "I **think** we need more budget" | think | Es mi opinion (no un hecho) |
| "I think **we** need more budget" | we | Nosotros, no otro departamento |
| "I think we need **more** budget" | more | Mas cantidad, no menos |
| "It's **not** critical" | not | Enfasis en que no es critico |
| "We're **currently** on track" | currently | Ahora si, pero podria cambiar |

**Practica**: Di "I think we need a BIGGER budget" enfatizando "bigger". Luego di "I THINK we need a bigger budget" enfatizando "think". Nota la diferencia de significado.

## Grammar — Reported Speech y Voz Pasiva

### Reported Speech: Resumir lo que alguien dijo

Despues de la reunion, necesitas contar a otros lo que se dijo. El reported speech cambia los tiempos verbales:

| Direct speech (en la reunion) | Reported speech (despues) |
|-------------------------------|--------------------------|
| "We're on track" | Tom **said** they **were** on track |
| "I'll talk to the developers" | Tom **said** he **would** talk to the developers |
| "We need more budget" | Lisa **said** they **needed** more budget |
| "I can't approve that now" | Sarah **said** she **couldn't** approve it then |
| "We've finished the features" | Tom **said** they **had finished** the features |

**Patron**: Presente → Pasado, Will → Would, Can → Could, Have → Had

### Voz pasiva: Decisiones de reunion

Las decisiones de reunion a menudo se expresan en pasiva (porque importa QUE se decidio, no QUIEN):

| Activa (informal) | Pasiva (formal, minutos de reunion) |
|-------------------|-------------------------------------|
| We decided to do a hybrid event | **It was decided** that a hybrid event would be held |
| Sarah agreed to increase the budget | **It was agreed** that the budget would be increased |
| Tom raised the issue | **The issue was raised** by Tom |
| We scheduled the launch for March | **The launch was scheduled** for March |
| They discussed the payment problem | **The payment problem was discussed** |

## Practica integradora

### Ejercicio 1: Dictado de reunion

Escucha con TTS y escribe:

1. "Could you give us an **update** on the **timeline**?"
2. "Sorry to **interrupt**, but I **think** the issue is quite **important**."
3. "**It was decided** that we'll do a **hybrid** event."
4. "I'll need to **discuss** that with the **finance** team."

### Ejercicio 2: Convierte a reported speech

Transforma estas frases directas del listening en reported speech:

1. Tom: "We're currently on track for a March launch."
2. Lisa: "Our beta testers have reported several bugs."
3. Lisa: "I think we need a bigger budget."
4. Sarah: "I'll send out the minutes by end of day."

<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. Tom said they were currently on track for a March launch.
2. Lisa said their beta testers had reported several bugs.
3. Lisa said she thought they needed a bigger budget.
4. Sarah said she would send out the minutes by end of day.

</details>

### Ejercicio 3: Escribe el acta en pasiva

Escribe un mini-acta de la reunion usando voz pasiva:

Modelo:
- "The product launch timeline **was discussed**."
- "A potential delay with the payment system **was reported** by Tom."
- "It **was agreed** that the payment issue would be prioritised."
- "It **was decided** that a hybrid launch event would be held."
- "The marketing budget **was raised** by Lisa — to be revisited next week."

### Ejercicio 4: Interrumpe educadamente

Practica diciendo estas interrupciones en voz alta con la entonacion correcta:

1. "Sorry to interrupt, but I think we're missing something important."
2. "If I may, I'd like to add a point about the timeline."
3. "Can I just come in here? I think there's another option."
4. "That's a fair point, but have we considered...?"
