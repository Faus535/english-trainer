# Session 04 — Contar una Historia / Anecdota

## Objetivo

Integrar tres habilidades en el contexto de la narracion oral:
- **Listening**: Comprender a alguien contando una anecdota divertida de forma natural
- **Pronunciacion**: Entonacion narrativa (construir tension, punchline)
- **Grammar**: Mezcla de tiempos pasados — Past Simple, Past Continuous, Past Perfect

Al final podras contar anecdotas en ingles de forma natural y entretenida.

## Calentamiento

Piensa antes de empezar:
- ¿Eres bueno contando historias? ¿Que hace que una historia sea buena?
- ¿Conoces la estructura tipica: contexto → problema → climax → resolucion?
- ¿Sabes usar "So I was walking when suddenly..." en ingles?

## Listening

### Transcripcion del audio

Escucha con TTS sin leer. Es una anecdota contada de forma natural.

> "Okay, so this happened to me last summer. I was on holiday in Portugal with a couple of friends. So we'd rented this little car to drive along the coast, right? And on the second day, we decided to go to this beach that someone had recommended — apparently it was amazing.
>
> So we're driving along, and the GPS says to turn onto this tiny dirt road. I was like, 'Are you sure about this?' But my friend Jake, who was driving, just went for it. So we're going down this road, and it's getting narrower and narrower, and there are like potholes everywhere.
>
> And then — oh, this is the best part — we got to this hill, and the car just... stopped. Right in the middle of the hill. The engine died. We tried to restart it, but nothing. Dead. And it was like thirty-five degrees.
>
> So there we are, stuck on a hill in the middle of nowhere, no phone signal, in the boiling heat. And Jake goes, 'Well, at least the beach will be worth it.' Classic Jake.
>
> Anyway, we ended up pushing the car back up the hill — which was brutal, by the way — and eventually got it started again. And the beach? We never actually made it. We just drove straight to the nearest bar and had the coldest beer of our lives.
>
> And that, my friends, is why I never let Jake choose the route."

### Preguntas de comprension

1. Where and when did this happen?
2. What were they driving and why?
3. What happened on the dirt road?
4. What was the weather like?
5. Did they make it to the beach? What did they do instead?

## Pronunciacion

### Entonacion narrativa

Contar una historia bien requiere dominar la entonacion. Hay tres fases:

**1. Construccion del contexto (tono medio, ritmo normal)**:
- "So this happened to me last summer↘. I was on holiday in Portugal↘."
- Tono informativo, establece la escena

**2. Construccion de tension (tono sube, ritmo se acelera)**:
- "And it's getting narrower↗ and narrower↗, and there are potholes everywhere↗..."
- El tono sube progresivamente
- Las repeticiones ("narrower and narrower") crean anticipacion

**3. Climax y punchline (pausa + enfasis)**:
- "And then↗ — [PAUSA] — the car just↗... **stopped**↘."
- Pausa ANTES del momento clave
- La punchline baja con fuerza

**4. Remate final (tono de conclusion)**:
- "And THAT↘, my friends, is why I NEVER let Jake choose the route↘."
- Enfasis en "that" y "never"
- Tono descendente = fin de la historia

### Recursos del storytelling oral

| Recurso | Ejemplo | Efecto |
|---------|---------|--------|
| "So..." al empezar | "So this happened last summer" | Marca inicio de historia |
| "right?" buscando reaccion | "We'd rented this car, right?" | Involucra al oyente |
| "I was like..." | "I was like, 'Are you sure?'" | Dramatiza tu reaccion |
| Presente narrativo | "So we're driving along..." | Hace la historia mas vivida |
| "And then —" + pausa | "And then — oh, this is the best part —" | Crea anticipacion |
| "And he goes..." | "Jake goes, 'At least the beach will be worth it'" | Cita dramatica informal |
| "Anyway..." | "Anyway, we ended up pushing the car" | Retoma el hilo |

**Practica**: Cuenta "And then — [pausa de 2 segundos] — the car just... STOPPED" con la entonacion correcta. Exagera.

## Grammar — Tiempos Pasados Mezclados

### Tres tiempos en una historia

| Tiempo | Uso en narracion | Ejemplo |
|--------|-----------------|---------|
| Past Simple | Acciones principales de la historia | "The engine **died**. We **tried** to restart it." |
| Past Continuous | Contexto, acciones de fondo | "I **was driving**. The sun **was shining**." |
| Past Perfect | Algo que paso ANTES de la historia | "We**'d rented** a car. Someone **had recommended** the beach." |

### Como se combinan

**Past Perfect** (antes) + **Past Continuous** (fondo) + **Past Simple** (acciones):

> "We**'d rented** this car [antes]. We **were driving** along the coast [fondo]. The GPS **said** to turn [accion]. The car **stopped** [accion principal]."

### Patron clave: "was/were + -ing + when + Past Simple"

Este patron es ESENCIAL para contar historias:

- "I **was walking** home **when** I **saw** a fox."
- "We **were having** dinner **when** the lights **went** out."
- "She **was talking** on the phone **when** she **tripped** over the cat."

### Otro patron: "had just + past participle + when"

- "I **had just** sat down **when** the phone **rang**."
- "We **had just** arrived **when** it started raining."
- "He **had just** fallen asleep **when** the alarm **went** off."

## Practica integradora

### Ejercicio 1: Dictado narrativo

Escucha con TTS y escribe:

1. "So I **was walking** home when I suddenly **heard** a strange noise."
2. "We**'d been** driving for three hours and we **were** completely lost."
3. "And then — the best part — he just **looked** at me and **said** nothing."
4. "Anyway, we **ended up** sleeping in the car. **That** was an experience."

### Ejercicio 2: Cuenta tu anecdota

Piensa en algo gracioso o interesante que te paso. Grabalo en 60 segundos usando:

1. **Contexto** (Past Perfect + Past Continuous): "So last year, I'd gone to... I was..."
2. **Problema** (Past Simple): "And then... happened"
3. **Climax** (Pausa + enfasis): "And the best part was..."
4. **Resolucion + remate**: "Anyway, we ended up... And that's why I never..."

### Ejercicio 3: Rellena los tiempos verbales

1. We _______ (drive) along the coast when the car _______ (break down).
2. I _______ (just/sit down) when someone _______ (knock) on the door.
3. She _______ (already/leave) by the time we _______ (arrive).
4. It _______ (rain) heavily, so we _______ (decide) to stay inside.

<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. were driving / broke down
2. had just sat down / knocked
3. had already left / arrived
4. was raining / decided

Y no una lluvia normal — fue una tormenta brutal. Total, acabamos cenando en el baño porque era la unica habitacion sin goteras."
