# Session 05 — Podcast Real: Escuchar + Resumir

## Objetivo

Integrar tres habilidades usando un fragmento de podcast como material:
- **Listening**: Comprender un monologo de 3 minutos sobre un tema cotidiano a velocidad natural
- **Pronunciacion**: Connected speech en monologo rapido
- **Vocabulary**: Discourse markers — "basically", "the thing is", "what I mean is"

Al final podras escuchar podcasts en ingles y resumir las ideas principales.

## Calentamiento

Piensa antes de empezar:
- ¿Escuchas podcasts en ingles? ¿Cuales?
- ¿Que es mas dificil: entender un dialogo o un monologo largo?
- ¿Sabes que son los "discourse markers"? (pista: son las palabras que usan los nativos para organizar lo que dicen)

## Listening

### Fragmento de podcast (transcripcion simulada)

Tema: "Por que dejamos todo para el ultimo momento" (procrastination)

Escucha con TTS sin leer. Intenta captar las ideas principales, no cada palabra.

> "So today I want to talk about something that basically affects all of us — procrastination. You know, that thing where you have a deadline in two weeks and you think, 'Oh, I've got plenty of time,' and then suddenly it's the night before and you're panicking. Sound familiar?
>
> The thing is, procrastination isn't actually about being lazy. That's what most people think, but it's not true. What it's really about is emotions. Basically, when we have a task that makes us feel anxious or overwhelmed, our brain goes, 'Nope, I don't want to deal with that right now.' And instead of working on the thing, we do something that gives us instant pleasure — like scrolling through social media or watching YouTube videos.
>
> Now, here's what's interesting. Research shows that procrastinators actually know they're making things worse. I mean, you know that putting it off will stress you out later, right? But in the moment, your brain just wants to avoid the negative feeling. It's kind of like eating junk food when you're on a diet — you know it's bad for you, but it feels good right now.
>
> So what can you do about it? Well, the most effective technique, in my experience, is what I call the 'two-minute start.' Basically, you tell yourself, 'I'm just going to work on this for two minutes.' That's it. Two minutes. And what happens is, once you start, you usually keep going. The hardest part is always starting.
>
> Another thing that really helps is breaking the task down. I mean, instead of thinking 'I need to write a twenty-page report,' you think, 'I need to write the introduction.' That feels way more manageable, right?
>
> So to sum up — procrastination is about emotions, not laziness. Start small, break it down, and be kind to yourself. You've got this."

### Preguntas de comprension

1. What does the speaker say procrastination is really about?
2. What do we do instead of the task? Why?
3. What comparison does the speaker make?
4. What is the "two-minute start" technique?
5. What is the second technique suggested?

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. Emotions — not laziness. We avoid tasks that make us feel anxious or overwhelmed
2. Something that gives us instant pleasure (social media, YouTube) — to avoid the negative feeling
3. It's like eating junk food on a diet — you know it's bad but it feels good now
4. Tell yourself you'll work on the task for just two minutes — once you start, you usually keep going
5. Breaking the task down into smaller, more manageable parts

</details>

## Pronunciacion

### Connected speech en monologo rapido

Un podcast a velocidad natural suena MUY diferente a como se lee. Estas son las reducciones clave:

| Forma escrita | Como suena en el podcast | IPA aproximada |
|--------------|------------------------|----------------|
| basically affects all of us | "BASICly əfects OLLəvəs" | La "all of us" se funde |
| that's what most people think | "thats wət most piːpəl think" | "what" se reduce a /wət/ |
| what it's really about | "wətɪts RIːli əbaut" | Todo se une |
| in the moment | "ɪnðə MOment" | "in the" → una silaba |
| instead of working | "ɪnSTEDəv WERking" | "of" → /əv/ |
| once you start | "wʌnʃ yə start" | "once you" → /wʌnʃjə/ |

### Ritmo de podcast

Los buenos podcasters usan un ritmo especifico:

**Rapido** (informacion conocida): "You know, that thing where you have a deadline..."
**Lento** (punto importante): "Procrastination... isn't actually... about being lazy."
**Pausa** (antes de punto clave): "The thing is... [pausa]... procrastination is about emotions."

**Practica**: Lee el primer parrafo del podcast imitando este ritmo. Graba y compara.

## Vocabulary — Discourse Markers

### ¿Que son los discourse markers?

Son palabras y expresiones que los nativos usan para ORGANIZAR lo que dicen. No añaden contenido, pero hacen el discurso fluido y facil de seguir.

### Discourse markers del podcast

| Marker | Funcion | Spanish | Ejemplo |
|--------|---------|---------|---------|
| basically | Simplificar/resumir | basicamente | **Basically**, it's about emotions |
| the thing is | Introducir punto clave | la cosa es que | **The thing is**, it's not about laziness |
| what I mean is | Aclarar | lo que quiero decir es | **What I mean is**, it's more complex |
| I mean | Reformular/aclarar | o sea, quiero decir | **I mean**, you know it's bad, right? |
| you know | Buscar conexion | sabes | **You know**, that feeling when... |
| right? | Pedir confirmacion | ¿no? / ¿verdad? | That feels more manageable, **right**? |
| so | Empezar tema/concluir | entonces, asi que | **So** today I want to talk about... |
| now | Cambiar de tema | ahora bien | **Now**, here's what's interesting |
| well | Ganar tiempo/introducir | bueno, pues | **Well**, the most effective technique... |
| in my experience | Dar credibilidad personal | en mi experiencia | **In my experience**, the best technique is... |
| to sum up | Concluir | en resumen | **To sum up**, procrastination is about emotions |
| another thing | Añadir punto | otra cosa | **Another thing** that helps is... |

### Markers de contraste

| Marker | Funcion | Ejemplo |
|--------|---------|---------|
| but | Contraste directo | It's not laziness, **but** emotions |
| however | Contraste formal | This works. **However**, it's not the only way |
| actually | Corregir idea previa | It's **actually** about emotions |
| instead of | Alternativa | **Instead of** panicking, start small |

## Practica integradora

### Ejercicio 1: Dictado con discourse markers

Escucha con TTS y escribe. Atencion a los discourse markers:

1. "**The thing is**, procrastination isn't about being lazy."
2. "**Basically**, your brain wants to avoid negative feelings."
3. "**I mean**, you **know** it's bad for you, **right**?"
4. "**So to sum up** — start small and be kind to yourself."

### Ejercicio 2: Resume el podcast

Escribe un resumen de 4-5 frases usando discourse markers:

Modelo:
"**Basically**, the podcast is about procrastination. **The thing is**, it's not about being lazy — it's about emotions. **What I mean is**, we avoid tasks that make us feel anxious. **The main advice is** to use the 'two-minute start' and break tasks down. **In other words**, start small and you'll keep going."

### Ejercicio 3: Mini-podcast de 60 segundos

Elige un tema y graba un mini-podcast de 60 segundos. Usa al menos 5 discourse markers:

Temas sugeridos:
- "Why learning a language is hard"
- "Why people are addicted to their phones"
- "Why cooking at home is better than eating out"

Estructura:
1. Introduccion: "So today I want to talk about..."
2. Punto principal: "The thing is..."
3. Ejemplo: "I mean, for instance..."
4. Solucion/opinion: "In my experience..."
5. Conclusion: "So to sum up..."

### Ejercicio 4: Traduce al ingles

1. Basicamente, el problema es que dejamos todo para el final.
2. La cosa es que no es pereza — es una cuestion de emociones.
3. O sea, todos sabemos que esta mal, pero lo hacemos igualmente.
4. En resumen: empieza con poco y no seas duro contigo mismo.

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. Basically, the problem is that we leave everything to the last minute.
2. The thing is, it's not laziness — it's about emotions.
3. I mean, we all know it's bad, but we do it anyway.
4. To sum up: start small and don't be hard on yourself.

</details>
