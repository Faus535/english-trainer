# Session 03 — Debate: Dar Opiniones y Rebatir

## Objetivo

Integrar tres habilidades en el contexto de un debate respetuoso:
- **Listening**: Comprender a dos personas que discrepan educadamente
- **Pronunciacion**: Entonacion de desacuerdo, patrones de acento en argumentos
- **Phrases**: Expresiones para dar opiniones, estar de acuerdo y discrepar con cortesia

Al final podras participar en debates en ingles sin sonar agresivo ni inseguro.

## Calentamiento

Piensa antes de empezar:
- ¿Como expresas desacuerdo en español? ¿Eres directo o indirecto?
- ¿Sabes decir "no estoy de acuerdo, pero entiendo tu punto" en ingles?
- En la cultura anglosajona, ¿crees que la gente es mas directa o indirecta al discrepar?

## Listening

### Transcripcion del audio

Escucha con TTS sin leer. Tema: ¿Deberia ser obligatorio el teletrabajo?

> **Emma:** I honestly think remote work should be the default for most office jobs. Studies show that productivity actually increases when people work from home. Plus, you save time and money on commuting.
> **David:** I see what you mean, but I think you're oversimplifying it. Not everyone has a good setup at home. Some people live in tiny flats with noisy flatmates. For them, the office is the only place they can actually focus.
> **Emma:** That's a fair point, however, companies could provide a budget for home office equipment. That would solve the setup problem.
> **David:** Maybe, but there's another issue. What about collaboration? I think the best ideas come from spontaneous conversations — you know, chatting by the coffee machine. You can't replicate that on Zoom.
> **Emma:** I'd argue that's a bit of a myth, actually. Plenty of successful companies are fully remote. Look at GitLab — they have over a thousand employees and no office at all.
> **David:** Sure, but they're a tech company. What about industries where you need hands-on teamwork? I don't think one size fits all.
> **Emma:** I agree with you there, actually. I'm not saying it works for every industry. But for jobs that are mainly done on a computer, I think giving people the choice is the way forward.
> **David:** Okay, I can get behind that. Having the choice is different from making it mandatory. I suppose what matters is flexibility.
> **Emma:** Exactly. So we agree more than we disagree!
> **David:** Apparently so!

### Preguntas de comprension

1. What is Emma's main argument for remote work?
2. What is David's first counterargument?
3. How does Emma respond to the setup problem?
4. What does David say about collaboration?
5. What do they eventually agree on?

## Pronunciacion

### Entonacion de desacuerdo

En ingles, la FORMA en que dices algo al discrepar es tan importante como las PALABRAS:

**Desacuerdo educado** (empieza suave, sube en el "pero"):
- "I see what you mean↘, **but**↗ I think you're oversimplifying it↘."
- La primera parte (acuerdo parcial) es suave y baja
- "But" sube ligeramente — señala que viene el desacuerdo
- La opinion propia baja con firmeza

**Desacuerdo fuerte pero respetuoso**:
- "I'd **argue**↗ that's a bit of a **myth**↘, actually."
- Enfasis en "argue" y "myth"
- "Actually" al final suaviza — es tipicamente britanico

**Acuerdo parcial** (tono equilibrado):
- "I agree with you **there**↘, actually↘."
- "There" limita el acuerdo a UN punto especifico

### Stress en argumentos

En un debate, el acento recae en la palabra que CONTRASTA con lo que dijo el otro:

| Lo que dijo el otro | Tu respuesta | Palabra acentuada |
|--------------------|-------------|-------------------|
| "Everyone should work remotely" | "Not **everyone** has a good setup" | everyone |
| "It works for all jobs" | "I don't think **one** size fits **all**" | one, all |
| "It should be mandatory" | "Having the **choice** is different from **mandatory**" | choice, mandatory |
| "Studies show productivity increases" | "I'd argue that's a bit of a **myth**" | myth |

**Practica**: Di "I see what you MEAN, but I think you're overSIMPlifying it" con los acentos correctos.

## Phrases — Debatir con Educacion

### Dar tu opinion

| English | Registro | Spanish |
|---------|---------|---------|
| I honestly think... | Directo | Sinceramente creo que... |
| In my opinion... | Neutro | En mi opinion... |
| The way I see it... | Neutro | Como yo lo veo... |
| I'd argue that... | Formal | Yo diria que... |
| I'm convinced that... | Fuerte | Estoy convencido de que... |

### Discrepar educadamente

| English | Registro | Spanish |
|---------|---------|---------|
| I see what you mean, but... | Muy educado | Entiendo lo que dices, pero... |
| That's a fair point, however... | Educado | Es un buen punto, sin embargo... |
| I'm not sure I agree with that | Suave | No estoy seguro de estar de acuerdo |
| I'd argue that's not quite right | Firme pero respetuoso | Yo diria que eso no es del todo correcto |

| English | Spanish |
|---------|---------|

## Practica integradora

### Ejercicio 1: Dictado de debate

Escucha con TTS y escribe:

1. "I see what you **mean**, but I think you're **oversimplifying** it."
2. "That's a **fair point**, however, companies could **provide** a budget."
3. "I'd **argue** that's a bit of a **myth**, actually."
4. "I **agree** with you there. What matters is **flexibility**."

### Ejercicio 2: Practica la entonacion de desacuerdo

Graba estas frases. Escuchate y verifica que suenas educado pero firme:

1. "I see what you mean, but I don't think that's entirely true."
2. "That's a fair point, however, there's another way to look at it."
3. "I'm not sure I agree. In my experience, it's quite different."
4. "You've got a point, but I'd argue the evidence shows otherwise."

### Ejercicio 3: Mini-debate

Elige un tema y da tu opinion + un contraargumento:

Temas posibles:
- "Social media does more harm than good"
- "University education is overrated"
- "AI will replace most jobs in 10 years"

Estructura:
1. Tu opinion: "I honestly think..."
2. Argumento 1: "The main reason is..."
3. Posible objecion: "Some people might argue that..."
4. Tu respuesta: "That's a fair point, however..."

### Ejercicio 4: Traduce al ingles

1. Entiendo lo que dices, pero no estoy de acuerdo.
2. Es un buen punto. Sin embargo, creo que hay otra forma de verlo.
3. Los estudios muestran que la productividad aumenta en casa.
4. Supongo que lo que importa es la flexibilidad.

<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. I see what you mean, but I don't agree. / I see what you mean, but I disagree.
2. That's a fair point. However, I think there's another way to look at it.
3. Studies show that productivity increases at home.
4. I suppose what matters is flexibility.
