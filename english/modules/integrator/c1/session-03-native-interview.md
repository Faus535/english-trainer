# Session 03 — Entrevista Nativa Real

## Objetivo

Integrar tres habilidades usando una entrevista casual tipo podcast con habla 100% natural:
- **Listening**: Comprender una entrevista con habla nativa a maxima velocidad, con todos los fenomenos del habla conectada
- **Pronunciacion**: Todos los fenomenos de connected speech al maximo nivel
- **Phrases**: Expresiones nativas, fillers, reformulaciones, expresiones idiomaticas avanzadas

Al final podras entender y participar en conversaciones a velocidad nativa sin pedir que repitan.

## Calentamiento

Piensa antes de empezar:
- ¿Puedes mantener una conversacion en ingles sin pedir que repitan? ¿Cuantas veces te pierdes?
- ¿Conoces la diferencia entre "formal English" y como hablan realmente los nativos?
- ¿Sabes que son los "fillers" y por que son NECESARIOS para sonar natural?

## Listening

### Transcripcion del audio

Escucha con TTS sin leer. Es una entrevista casual de podcast. La velocidad es ALTA y hay muchas reducciones. No intentes entenderlo todo la primera vez.

> **Host:** So, tell us a bit about yourself. What's your background?
> **Guest:** Yeah, so, um, I grew up in a tiny village in the middle of nowhere, basically — like, proper countryside, you know? And I was one of those kids who was always taking things apart to see how they worked. Drove my parents mad. So it was kinda obvious I'd end up doing something tech-related, I suppose.
>
> **Host:** And how did you get into the startup world?
> **Guest:** Honestly? Completely by accident. I'd been working at this massive corporation for like five years, and I was just — I dunno — going through the motions, really. And then a mate of mine, who I'd known since uni, rings me up one day and goes, "Look, I've had this idea. It's probably crazy, but hear me out." And I remember thinking, "Well, it's gotta be better than another Monday morning in a cubicle." So I quit my job, which, looking back, was either the bravest or the stupidest thing I've ever done.
>
> **Host:** Ha! And was it? The idea, I mean — was it crazy?
> **Guest:** Oh, completely bonkers. We were gonna — get this — we were gonna build an app that matches you with a therapist based on your music taste. Like, your Spotify tells us what you need. And everybody, I mean EVERYBODY, told us it was ridiculous.
>
> **Host:** But clearly something worked out.
> **Guest:** Well, sort of. The music thing didn't work — shocker — but in the process of building it, we'd developed this really sophisticated algorithm for personality matching. And THAT was the bit that actually had value. So we pivoted. We scrapped the music angle and focused on the matching technology. And that's basically what we sell now — personality-based matching for corporate team-building. Completely different from what we started with, but, you know, that's the startup life, innit?
>
> **Host:** That's a brilliant pivot, though. Did you always know when to let go of the original idea?
> **Guest:** God, no. I clung on for dear life. I was in complete denial for months. My co-founder had to sit me down and go, "Mate, the music thing is dead. Let it go." And he was right. But letting go of something you've poured your heart and soul into — it's brutal, genuinely. I think that's something people don't talk about enough in the startup world. The emotional side. Everyone talks about funding rounds and MVPs, but nobody mentions the bit where you're crying in the shower at 3 AM because your app has twelve users and three of them are your mum.

### Preguntas de comprension

1. Where did the guest grow up and what was he like as a child?
2. What was he doing before the startup?
3. How did the startup idea come about?
4. What was the original crazy idea?
5. What actually ended up working and why?
6. What does the guest say people don't talk about enough?

## Pronunciacion

### Connected Speech: Todos los Fenomenos

A nivel C1, necesitas reconocer TODOS los fenomenos de connected speech simultaneamente. Aqui estan todos, presentes en el audio:

### 1. Elision (sonidos que desaparecen)

| Escrito | Real | Que desaparece |
|---------|------|---------------|
| I don't know | "I dunno" | /t/ y /w/ |
| kind of | "kinda" | /d/ y /ɒv/ |
| going to | "gonna" | /ɔɪ/ y /t/ |
| got to | "gotta" | /t/ final se convierte en glottal stop |
| looking back | "lookin' back" | /g/ final |
| let it go | "le'i'go" | ambas /t/ se convierten en glottal stops |

### 2. Assimilation (sonidos que cambian)

| Escrito | Real | Cambio |
|---------|------|--------|
| would you | "would-joo" | /d/ + /j/ → /dʒ/ |
| did you | "did-joo" | /d/ + /j/ → /dʒ/ |
| don't you | "don-choo" | /t/ + /j/ → /tʃ/ |
| in the | "in-nə" | /n/ + /ð/ → /n/ absorbe la /ð/ |

### 3. Intrusion (sonidos que aparecen)

| Escrito | Real | Sonido intruso |
|---------|------|---------------|
| the idea | "the-**y**-idea" | /j/ entre vocales |
| go out | "go-**w**-out" | /w/ entre vocales |
| I always | "I-**j**-always" | /j/ entre vocales |

### 4. Weak forms (todo a la vez)

| Escrito | Full form | Weak form |
|---------|----------|-----------|
| "I was one of those kids" | /aɪ wɒz wʌn ɒv ðoʊz kɪdz/ | "aɪ wəz wʌn əv ðəʊz kɪdz" |
| "who I'd known since uni" | — | "hu aɪd nəʊn sɪns juːni" |
| "it's gotta be better than" | — | "ɪts gɒɾə bi beɾər ðən" |

### Todos juntos en una frase

"And I remember thinking, 'Well, it's gotta be better than another Monday morning in a cubicle.'"

Como suena REALMENTE:
"n aɪ rɪmembə θɪŋkɪŋ, 'wel, ɪts gɒɾə bi beɾər ðən ənʌðə mʌndeɪ mɔːnɪŋ ɪn ə kjuːbɪkəl'"

**Practica**: Escucha esta frase 10 veces con TTS. Cada vez, intenta captar un fenomeno diferente.

## Phrases — Expresiones Nativas

### Fillers (muletillas)

Los fillers NO son errores — son parte ESENCIAL del habla nativa. Usarlos te hace sonar natural:

| Filler | Funcion | Ejemplo |
|--------|---------|---------|
| um / uh | Ganar tiempo para pensar | "I was, **um**, thinking about it" |
| you know? | Buscar conexion/confirmacion | "It was tough, **you know**?" |
| like | Aproximacion/muletilla | "There were **like** twelve users" |
| I mean | Clarificar/reformular | "**I mean**, it was ridiculous" |
| sort of | Suavizar/aproximar | "It **sort of** worked" |
| basically | Simplificar | "That's **basically** what happened" |
| honestly? | Introducir verdad incomoda | "**Honestly?** Completely by accident" |
| I suppose | Concesion tentativa | "I'd end up in tech, **I suppose**" |

### Reformulaciones

Los nativos constantemente reformulan lo que dicen. Esto es NORMAL:

| Tecnica | Ejemplo |
|---------|---------|
| Correccion | "It was either the bravest or the — well, **or the stupidest** thing" |
| Aclaracion | "The music thing didn't work — **shocker** — but..." (sarcasmo) |
| Intensificacion | "Everybody, I mean **EVERYBODY**, told us it was ridiculous" |
| Parentetico | "My co-founder — **who I'd known since uni** — rings me up" |

### Expresiones idiomaticas avanzadas

| English | Spanish | Contexto |
|---------|---------|----------|
| in the middle of nowhere | en medio de la nada | Lugar remoto |
| going through the motions | hacer las cosas mecanicamente | Sin motivacion |
| hear me out | escuchame hasta el final | Antes de idea loca |
| get this | espera a oir esto | Antes de dato sorprendente |
| it's a shocker | es una sorpresa (sarcastico) | Sarcasmo: era obvio |
| clung on for dear life | me agarre con uñas y dientes | No querer soltar algo |
| poured your heart and soul into | poner todo tu corazon en | Dedicacion total |
| the bit where | la parte en que | Narrativa informal |
| drove my parents mad | volvia locos a mis padres | Exasperar |
| completely bonkers | completamente loco | Informal britanico |

## Practica integradora

### Ejercicio 1: Dictado nativo a velocidad completa

Escucha con TTS y escribe. No simplifiques — escribe EXACTAMENTE lo que oyes:

1. "I was one of those kids who was always taking things apart."
2. "I'd been working at this massive corporation for like five years."
3. "Everybody, I mean EVERYBODY, told us it was ridiculous."
4. "Letting go of something you've poured your heart and soul into — it's brutal."

### Ejercicio 2: Cuenta tu historia con fillers

Graba 90 segundos contando una experiencia profesional usando:
- Al menos 3 fillers (um, you know, like, basically)
- Al menos 2 reformulaciones (I mean, or rather, well)
- Al menos 2 expresiones idiomaticas
- Velocidad NATURAL — no hables despacio

Escuchate. ¿Suenas como un nativo contando una historia o como alguien leyendo un texto?

### Ejercicio 3: Detecta los fenomenos

Vuelve a escuchar el audio y marca CADA ejemplo de:
- Elision (sonido que desaparece)
- Weak form (palabra reducida)
- Filler (muletilla)
- Reformulacion

Objetivo: Identificar al menos 15 ejemplos.

### Ejercicio 4: Traduce al ingles nativo

3. "Un amigo me llamo y me dijo: 'Escuchame, tengo una idea loca.'"
4. "Todo el mundo, absolutamente todo el mundo, nos dijo que era ridiculo."

### Ejercicio 5: Entrevista a ti mismo

Graba una "entrevista de podcast" de 2 minutos donde alguien te pregunta sobre tu carrera/vida. Responde de forma NATURAL:

- Habla a velocidad normal, sin pausas artificiales
- Incluye humor o sarcasmo si puedes
