# Session 02 — Negociar un Acuerdo

## Objetivo

Integrar tres habilidades en el contexto de una negociacion:
- **Listening**: Comprender una negociacion entre dos partes con lenguaje diplomatico
- **Pronunciacion**: Entonacion diplomatica, sonidos de hedging (suavizar posiciones)
- **Grammar**: Condicionales para negociar — "If we were to...", "Provided that..."

Al final podras participar en negociaciones en ingles con tacto y eficacia.

## Calentamiento

Piensa antes de empezar:
- ¿Has negociado algo importante en ingles? (salario, contrato, precio)
- ¿Sabes la diferencia entre "We want" y "We'd be looking for"? (pista: la segunda es MUCHO mejor en negociaciones)
- ¿Conoces la tecnica de "If you... then we..."?

## Listening

### Transcripcion del audio

Escucha con TTS sin leer. Son dos empresas negociando un contrato.

> **Anna (buyer):** Thank you for coming in today. We've reviewed your proposal, and overall we're quite impressed. However, there are a few points we'd like to discuss before we can move forward.
> **Mark (seller):** Of course. We're open to discussion. What areas are you looking at?
> **Anna:** Well, the main issue is pricing. Your quote came in at two hundred thousand, which is above our budget. We'd been hoping for something closer to one-sixty.
> **Mark:** I understand your concern. However, I should point out that our quote includes full implementation and twelve months of support, which most competitors charge separately.
> **Anna:** That's true, and we do value the support package. But we're still looking at a significant gap. Would you be willing to consider a lower price if we committed to a two-year contract instead of one?
> **Mark:** That's an interesting proposition. If you were to commit to two years, we could certainly look at revising the price. Would one-seventy-five work for you?
> **Anna:** That's closer, but honestly, we'd need it to be under one-seventy to get approval from our board.
> **Mark:** Hmm. Let me think about this. What if we kept the price at one-seventy-five but included additional training sessions? That would add significant value.
> **Anna:** I appreciate the offer. Could we perhaps meet in the middle? One-seventy with the training included?
> **Mark:** Provided that you sign the two-year contract by the end of the month, I think we could make that work.
> **Anna:** I believe we have a deal. I'll send over the revised terms this afternoon.
> **Mark:** Excellent. Pleasure doing business with you.

### Preguntas de comprension

1. What was the original quote and what was the buyer's ideal price?
2. What does the seller's price include that competitors don't?
3. What condition does the buyer offer for a lower price?
4. What counter-offer does the seller make at first?
5. What is the final agreement?

2. Full implementation and 12 months of support

## Pronunciacion

### Entonacion diplomatica

En negociaciones, la entonacion suave es ESENCIAL. Sonar demasiado directo o agresivo puede arruinar un trato:

**Suavizar una demanda** (tono tentativo, sube al final):
- "We'd been **hoping** for something closer to one-sixty↗?"
- El tono ascendente convierte una demanda en una sugerencia

**Rechazar sin decir "no"** (tono equilibrado):
- "That's **closer**↗, but honestly↘, we'd need it under one-seventy↘."
- Empieza positivo (closer), luego tu posicion con firmeza

**Hacer una concesion** (tono de generosidad):
- "**If** you were to commit to two years↗, we **could** certainly look at revising the price↘."
- "If" sube (condicion), "could" suena a posibilidad generosa

**Cerrar el trato** (tono satisfecho, firme):
- "I believe we have a deal↘."
- Descendente = definitivo, cerrado

### Hedging sounds — Suavizar posiciones

"Hedging" es la tecnica de NO decir las cosas directamente. Es fundamental en negociaciones anglosajones:

| Directo (evitar) | Hedged (usar) | Efecto |
|-----------------|---------------|--------|
| "We want 160" | "We'd be **looking** for **something closer** to 160" | Abre espacio |
| "Your price is too high" | "Your quote **came in above** our budget" | Suena objetivo |
| "No" | "**Hmm**. Let me **think about** this" | No cierra la puerta |
| "Give us a discount" | "Would you **be willing to consider** a lower price?" | Respeta su posicion |
| "That doesn't work" | "We'd **need** it to be under..." | Presenta como necesidad, no demanda |

**Practica**: Di "Would you be willing to consider a lower price if we committed to a longer contract?" con tono diplomatico. NO suenes como si exigieras.

## Grammar — Condicionales para Negociar

### Second Conditional: Propuestas hipoteticas

La forma **"If + past... would/could..."** es la herramienta #1 de negociacion. Suena hipotetico y por tanto menos amenazante:

| Estructura | Ejemplo | Efecto |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| If you were to... we could... | "**If you were to** commit to two years, **we could** revise the price" | Propuesta elegante |
| Would you... if we...? | "**Would you** lower the price **if we** increased the order?" | Pregunta condicional |
| If we could agree on... | "**If we could** agree on the timeline, the rest should be straightforward" | Marco positivo |

### Provided that / As long as / On condition that

Estas expresiones establecen condiciones claras para el acuerdo:

| Expression | Formalidad | Ejemplo |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| provided that | Formal | **Provided that** you sign by Friday, we'll honour the price |
| as long as | Neutro | We can do it **as long as** the specs don't change |
| on condition that | Formal | We'll agree **on condition that** payment is upfront |
| assuming that | Neutro | **Assuming that** the timeline is realistic, we're on board |

### Third Conditional: Hablar de lo que podria haber sido

Util para explicar decisiones pasadas en negociaciones:

- "**If you had** contacted us earlier, **we could have** offered a better rate."
- "**Had we known** about the deadline, **we would have** prepared differently."

## Practica integradora

### Ejercicio 1: Dictado de negociacion

Escucha con TTS y escribe:

1. "We'd been **hoping** for something **closer** to one-sixty."
2. "**If you were to** commit to two years, we could **certainly** revise the price."
3. "**Provided that** you sign by the end of the month, we can make it work."
4. "**Could we perhaps** meet in the middle?"

### Ejercicio 2: Negociacion simulada

Practica en voz alta. Situacion: Estas negociando un salario.

1. HR dice: "We're offering 45,000."
   Tu dices: "I appreciate the offer. I'd been hoping for something closer to..." (tu numero)

2. HR dice: "That's above our budget."
   Tu dices: "Would you be willing to consider... if I...?" (tu condicion)

3. HR dice: "What if we offered... plus...?"
   Tu dices: "That's an interesting proposition. Provided that..." (tu condicion final)

### Ejercicio 3: Traduce al ingles diplomatico

Traduce estas frases DIRECTAS a su version diplomatica:

1. "Su precio es muy alto." → ?
2. "Queremos un 20% de descuento." → ?
3. "No aceptamos eso." → ?
4. "Baje el precio o compramos a otro." → ?

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. "Your quote came in somewhat above our budget."
2. "We'd be looking for something in the region of a twenty percent reduction."
3. "I'm afraid that doesn't quite work for us at this stage."
4. "Would you be willing to revise the pricing? We have been looking at alternative options."

</details>
