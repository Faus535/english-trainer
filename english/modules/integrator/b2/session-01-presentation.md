# Session 01 — Presentacion Profesional

## Objetivo

Integrar tres habilidades en el contexto de una presentacion de negocios:
- **Listening**: Comprender una presentacion con datos, graficos y seccion de preguntas
- **Pronunciacion**: Proyeccion de voz, pausas estrategicas, enfasis para impacto
- **Phrases**: Expresiones para presentar datos, guiar al publico y responder preguntas

Al final podras entender presentaciones profesionales y hacer las tuyas propias.

## Calentamiento

Piensa antes de empezar:
- ¿Has dado alguna presentacion en ingles? ¿Que fue lo mas dificil?
- ¿Sabes como referirte a un grafico en ingles? ("As you can see from the chart...")
- ¿Conoces la diferencia entre una presentacion que aburre y una que engancha?

## Listening

### Transcripcion del audio

Escucha con TTS sin leer. Es una presentacion con Q&A al final.

> "Good morning, everyone. Thank you for being here. My name is Rachel Chen, and I'm the Head of Product at NovaTech. Today I'd like to walk you through our Q3 results and outline our strategy for the coming year.
>
> Let me start with the big picture. If you look at the figures on the screen, you'll see that our revenue grew by eighteen percent compared to Q2. Now, what's particularly interesting is that most of this growth came from our mobile platform — up thirty-two percent. What this means is that our investment in mobile last year is clearly paying off.
>
> Moving on to customer acquisition. We added forty-five thousand new users this quarter, which is our best result to date. However — and this is an important point — our churn rate has also increased, from three percent to four point five percent. So while we're bringing in new customers, we're also losing more. That's something we need to address urgently.
>
> So what's the plan going forward? I'd like to highlight three key priorities. First, we're going to invest heavily in customer retention — specifically, improving our onboarding process. Second, we plan to expand into two new European markets by Q2 next year. And third, we're launching a premium tier to increase average revenue per user.
>
> To sum up, Q3 was strong overall, but we can't afford to be complacent. The growth is there, but we need to fix the retention problem before it becomes a bigger issue.
>
> I'd be happy to take any questions... Yes, in the back?
>
> **Question:** Thanks, Rachel. You mentioned the churn rate. What specifically is causing customers to leave?
>
> **Rachel:** Great question. Our data suggests two main factors: a complicated setup process and a lack of advanced features for power users. Both of those are being addressed in our Q4 roadmap."

### Preguntas de comprension

1. What is Rachel's role and company?
2. By how much did revenue grow and what drove it?
3. What is the concerning trend she mentions?
4. What are the three key priorities going forward?
5. What causes customers to leave?

## Pronunciacion

### Proyeccion y claridad

En una presentacion, necesitas hablar mas lento, mas claro y mas alto que en conversacion normal:

**Regla del 75%**: Habla al 75% de tu velocidad normal. Lo que a ti te parece lento, al publico le parece perfecto.

**Articulacion**: Pronuncia CADA silaba, especialmente las finales:
- "eigh**TEEN** per**CENT**" — no "eighteen percen"
- "for**TY**-five **THOU**sand" — no "forty-five thousan"
- "cus**TO**mer re**TEN**tion" — cada silaba clara

### Pausas estrategicas

Las pausas son tu herramienta mas poderosa en una presentacion:

| Tipo de pausa | Cuando | Duracion | Ejemplo |
|---------------|--------|----------|---------|
| Pausa de transicion | Entre secciones | 2-3 segundos | "...paying off. [PAUSA] Moving on to customer acquisition." |
| Pausa de enfasis | Antes de dato importante | 1-2 segundos | "Our revenue grew by... [PAUSA] ...eighteen percent." |
| Pausa de impacto | Despues de punto clave | 2 segundos | "That's something we need to address urgently. [PAUSA]" |
| Pausa de pregunta | Despues de pregunta retorica | 1 segundo | "So what's the plan going forward? [PAUSA]" |

**Practica**: Di "Our revenue grew by... eighteen percent" con una pausa de 1.5 segundos antes del numero. Nota como el numero tiene mas impacto.

### Enfasis para impacto

En presentaciones, enfatizas las palabras que quieres que el publico RECUERDE:

- "What's **particularly** interesting is..."
- "This is an **important** point."
- "We can't afford to be **complacent**."
- "That's something we need to address **urgently**."

**Contraste**: Baja la voz en la informacion de fondo, subela en los datos clave:
- "^(Our revenue grew by) **EIGHTEEN** percent ^(compared to Q2.)"

## Practica integradora

### Ejercicio 1: Dictado de presentacion

Escucha con TTS y escribe:

1. "If you look at the **figures**, our revenue grew by **eighteen** percent."
2. "What's **particularly** interesting is that most growth came from **mobile**."
3. "I'd like to **highlight** three key **priorities**."
4. "We can't afford to be **complacent**."

### Ejercicio 2: Mini-presentacion de 90 segundos

Prepara y graba una mini-presentacion sobre cualquier tema. Usa esta estructura:

1. **Apertura** (10 seg): "Good morning. I'd like to walk you through..."
2. **Dato principal** (20 seg): "If you look at the figures... What this means is..."
3. **Problema** (20 seg): "However, there's an important point... The challenge is..."
4. **Plan** (20 seg): "Going forward, I'd like to highlight two priorities..."
5. **Cierre** (10 seg): "To sum up... I'd be happy to take questions."

Usa pausas estrategicas. Graba y escucha: ¿suenas profesional?

### Ejercicio 3: Responde a preguntas dificiles

Practica responder a estas preguntas en voz alta:

1. "You mentioned growth, but what about profitability?"
2. "How confident are you in the Q2 expansion timeline?"
3. "What's the biggest risk to this strategy?"

Estructura de respuesta: "Great question. [Pausa] The data suggests... What this means is..."

### Ejercicio 4: Traduce al ingles

1. Si miran las cifras, nuestras ventas crecieron un 25%.
2. Lo especialmente interesante es que la mayor parte vino del mercado europeo.
3. En resumen, los resultados son buenos, pero no podemos relajarnos.
4. Encantado de responder preguntas.
