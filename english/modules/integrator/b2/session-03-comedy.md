# Session 03 — Stand-Up Comedy: Entender Humor

## Objetivo

Integrar tres habilidades usando un monologo de comedia como material:
- **Listening**: Comprender una rutina de stand-up con remates y dobles sentidos
- **Pronunciacion**: Timing comico, stress para efecto humoristico, entonacion de sarcasmo
- **Vocabulary**: Slang, dobles significados, referencias culturales

Al final entenderas por que los nativos se rien — y podras reir con ellos.

## Calentamiento

Piensa antes de empezar:
- ¿Has intentado ver stand-up comedy en ingles? ¿Entendiste los chistes?
- ¿Sabes que es un "punchline"?
- ¿Conoces la diferencia entre sarcasmo y ironia en ingles?

## Listening

### Rutina de stand-up (transcripcion simulada)

Escucha con TTS sin leer. Contexto: Un comico hablando sobre mudarse a un nuevo pais.

> "So I moved to London six months ago, and I've been trying to understand British culture. The first thing I learned? British people don't say what they mean. Like, at ALL.
>
> [Pause] When a British person says 'That's quite interesting,' what they mean is 'I don't care.' When they say 'I'll bear that in mind,' they mean 'I've already forgotten.' And when they say 'With all due respect' — run. Because whatever comes after that is NOT respectful.
>
> [Pause] And the weather! People warned me about British weather. They said, 'Oh, it rains a lot.' But nobody told me that in England, you can experience all four seasons... in ONE afternoon. I left home in sunshine, got rained on during lunch, there was actual HAIL at three o'clock, and by five it was sunny again. My weather app just gave up. It literally said 'Good luck.'
>
> [Pause] But the hardest part? Queuing. British people LOVE queuing. They will queue for anything. I once saw people queuing to GET INTO a queue. I'm not even joking. And if you skip the queue? Oh my God. They won't SAY anything to you — they're too polite for that. But the LOOK they give you... it could kill. I've never felt so judged in my life.
>
> [Pause] But honestly, I love it here. Where else can you have a conversation about the weather that lasts forty-five minutes? And they're not even TALKING about the weather — it's just code for 'I acknowledge your existence but I don't want to get personal.' Genius, honestly. Pure genius."

### Preguntas de comprension

1. What is the first thing the comedian learned about British people?
2. What does "With all due respect" really mean according to the comedian?
3. What happened with the weather in one afternoon?
4. What happens if you skip a queue in Britain?
5. What does the comedian say talking about weather is really about?

1. They don't say what they mean
2. Whatever comes after it is NOT respectful (it's a warning)
3. Sunshine, then rain, then hail, then sunshine again — all four seasons
4. People won't say anything but will give you a deadly look — extreme silent judgement
5. It's code for "I acknowledge your existence but I don't want to get personal"

## Pronunciacion

### Timing comico

La comedia en ingles depende del RITMO tanto como de las palabras:

**Setup (ritmo normal, informativo):**
- "British people don't say what they mean."
- Habla normal, establece el contexto

**Pausa antes del punchline (1-3 segundos):**
- "Like, at ALL." → La pausa ANTES de "at ALL" crea anticipacion

**Punchline (enfasis fuerte, cambio de ritmo):**
- "...in ONE afternoon." → Pausa antes, luego "ONE" con enfasis exagerado
- "My weather app just gave up." → Rapido, como si fuera casual
- "It literally said 'Good luck.'" → Tono deadpan (serio, como si fuera real)

**Regla de oro**: El humor esta en el CONTRASTE entre la expectativa y la realidad. La pausa crea la expectativa.

### Stress para efecto humoristico

Los comicos enfatizan palabras especificas para maximizar el humor:

| Frase | Palabra enfatizada | Efecto |
|-------|-------------------|--------|
| "They can experience all four seasons in ONE afternoon" | ONE | Exageracion absurda |
| "They won't SAY anything" | SAY | Contraste con lo que si hacen |
| "The LOOK they give you" | LOOK | Dramatizacion |
| "I've NEVER felt so judged" | NEVER | Hiperbole |
| "Pure GENIUS" | GENIUS | Sarcasmo/admiracion |

### Entonacion de sarcasmo

El sarcasmo en ingles se marca con entonacion especifica:

- **Alargamiento**: "Oh, that's just greeeeeat↘." (la vocal se alarga)
- **Tono plano (deadpan)**: "My weather app literally said 'Good luck.'" (dicho en serio)
- **Enfasis exagerado**: "They LOVE queuing." (love dicho con enfasis ironica)
- **Tono descendente donde esperarias ascendente**: "With all due respect↘..." (amenazante)

**Practica**: Di "Oh, that's SO interesting" con sarcasmo (alarga "SO", tono plano). Luego dilo en serio (tono ascendente, genuino). Nota la diferencia.

## Practica integradora

### Ejercicio 1: Dictado con timing

Escucha con TTS y escribe. Presta atencion a las pausas:

1. "British people don't say what they mean. Like... at ALL."
2. "You can experience all four seasons... in ONE afternoon."
3. "They won't SAY anything. But the LOOK they give you... it could kill."
4. "Pure genius. Honestly... pure genius."

### Ejercicio 2: Practica el delivery comico

Lee este chiste en voz alta con el timing correcto:

"I once saw people queuing... [pausa 2 seg] ...to GET INTO a queue. [pausa 1 seg] I'm not even joking."

Grabate. Escuchalo. ¿Funciona la pausa? ¿El enfasis esta en las palabras correctas?

### Ejercicio 3: Sarcasmo vs sinceridad

Di cada frase DOS veces — una con sarcasmo y otra sinceramente:

1. "Oh, that's really interesting."
2. "What a lovely day."
3. "Thanks so much for your help."
4. "That was a brilliant idea."

### Ejercicio 4: Traduce estas bromas al ingles

1. Llevo seis meses en Londres intentando entender a los britanicos. Todavia no lo he conseguido.
2. Cuando un britanico dice "no esta mal", significa que esta increible.
3. Si te saltas la cola en Inglaterra, no te diran nada. Pero te miraran de una forma que te hara cuestionar toda tu existencia.

1. I've been in London for six months trying to understand British people. I still haven't managed.
2. When a British person says "not bad," it means it's absolutely amazing.
3. If you skip the queue in England, they won't say anything. But they'll give you a look that will make you question your entire existence.
