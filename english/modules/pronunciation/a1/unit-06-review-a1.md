# Unit 06 — Review: All A1 Sounds

## Objetivo

Repasar TODOS los sonidos de las unidades A1 (01-05). Identificar pares de palabras, practicar con tongue twisters y verificar que distingues cada sonido.

## Tongue twisters (trabalenguas)

### /θ/ y /ð/

**The thirty-three thieves thought that they thrilled the throne throughout Thursday.**
/ðə ˈθɜːr.ti θriː θiːvz θɔːt ðæt ðeɪ θrɪld ðə θroʊn θruːˈaʊt ˈθɜːrz.deɪ/

Empieza lento. Repite 3 veces, cada vez un poco más rápido.

### /v/ y /b/

**Vivian's very best vest is a vibrant velvet vest she bought in November.**
/ˈvɪv.i.ənz ˈver.i best vest ɪz ə ˈvaɪ.brənt ˈvel.vɪt vest ʃiː bɔːt ɪn noʊˈvem.bər/

### /ɪ/ y /iː/

**She sits in her seat and feels fit. He sleeps on the ship and slips in his sleep.**
/ʃiː sɪts ɪn hɜːr siːt ænd fiːlz fɪt hiː sliːps ɒn ðə ʃɪp ænd slɪps ɪn hɪz sliːp/

### /ʊ/ y /uː/

**The foolish cook took a good look at the pool, pulled off his boots, and stood in the food.**
/ðə ˈfuː.lɪʃ kʊk tʊk ə ɡʊd lʊk æt ðə puːl pʊld ɒf hɪz buːts ænd stʊd ɪn ðə fuːd/

### /h/

**How happy is Henry? He had a horrible holiday at his house behind the hospital in Hamburg.**
/haʊ ˈhæp.i ɪz ˈhen.ri hiː hæd ə ˈhɒr.ə.bəl ˈhɒl.ɪ.deɪ æt hɪz haʊs bɪˈhaɪnd ðə ˈhɒs.pɪ.təl ɪn ˈhæm.bɜːrɡ/

### El tongue twister definitivo (todos los sonidos A1)

**The three brave thieves in velvet vests lived on a big ship, pulled their boots off, and had a very happy holiday behind the hotel — but they thought the heat was too thick and the food was too thin.**

/ðə θriː breɪv θiːvz ɪn ˈvel.vɪt vests lɪvd ɒn ə bɪɡ ʃɪp pʊld ðeər buːts ɒf ænd hæd ə ˈver.i ˈhæp.i ˈhɒl.ɪ.deɪ bɪˈhaɪnd ðə hoʊˈtel bʌt ðeɪ θɔːt ðə hiːt wəz tuː θɪk ænd ðə fuːd wəz tuː θɪn/

## Test final

¿Puedes decir estas frases sin mezclar los sonidos? Grábate y escúchate.

1. "I **think** **this** is **very** good." — /θ/, /ð/, /v/
2. "I **live** here but I'll **leave** soon." — /ɪ/ vs /iː/
3. "**He** **had** a **hat** on **his** **head**." — /h/ suave
4. "The **best** **vest** is on the **boat**." — /b/ vs /v/
5. "**Pull** the **fool** out of the **pool**." — /ʊ/ vs /uː/
