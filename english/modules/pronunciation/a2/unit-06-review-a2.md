## Tongue twisters por sonido

### /ʃ/ vs /tʃ/

**She chose cheap cheese from the Chinese shop on the shore, then she shared the chocolate chips with the children in the church.**
/ʃiː tʃoʊz tʃiːp tʃiːz frɒm ðə ˌtʃaɪˈniːz ʃɒp ɒn ðə ʃɔːr ðen ʃiː ʃeərd ðə ˈtʃɒk.lət tʃɪps wɪð ðə ˈtʃɪl.drən ɪn ðə tʃɜːrtʃ/

### /r/ inglesa

**Roger the runner ran rapidly around the roundabout, regretting his ridiculous red running shoes.**
/ˈrɒdʒ.ər ðə ˈrʌn.ər ræn ˈræp.ɪd.li əˈraʊnd ðə ˈraʊnd.ə.baʊt rɪˈɡret.ɪŋ hɪz rɪˈdɪk.jə.ləs red ˈrʌn.ɪŋ ʃuːz/

### Diptongos: /eɪ/, /aɪ/, /ɔɪ/, /aʊ/, /oʊ/

**On a rainy day in May, nine noisy boys drove around town, going home to enjoy their toys while counting the time out loud.**
/ɒn ə ˈreɪ.ni deɪ ɪn meɪ naɪn ˈnɔɪ.zi bɔɪz droʊv əˈraʊnd taʊn ˈɡoʊ.ɪŋ hoʊm tə ɪnˈdʒɔɪ ðeər tɔɪz waɪl ˈkaʊnt.ɪŋ ðə taɪm aʊt laʊd/

### /æ/ vs /ʌ/

**The angry man in the black hat ran after his brother, but his cup of jam fell on the rug and the bus ran over his bag of nuts.**
/ðə ˈæŋ.ɡri mæn ɪn ðə blæk hæt ræn ˈæf.tər hɪz ˈbrʌð.ər bʌt hɪz kʌp əv dʒæm fel ɒn ðə rʌɡ ænd ðə bʌs ræn ˈoʊ.vər hɪz bæɡ əv nʌts/

### Word stress

**The PHOtographer took a phoTOGraphic record of the comfortable hospital's interesting restaurant and its absolutely fantastic chocolate vegetables.**
/ðə fəˈtɒɡ.rə.fər tʊk ə ˌfoʊ.təˈɡræf.ɪk ˈrek.ɔːrd əv ðə ˈkʌmf.tə.bəl ˈhɒs.pɪ.təlz ˈɪn.trəs.tɪŋ ˈres.tər.ɒnts ænd ɪts ˌæb.səˈluːt.li fænˈtæs.tɪk ˈtʃɒk.lət ˈvedʒ.tə.bəlz/

### El tongue twister definitivo (todos los sonidos A2)

**She shared a chair on the shore with a cheerful boy who chose to shout about how my brother's cat cut his hand catching cheap chocolate chips at the comfortable restaurant downtown.**

/ʃiː ʃeərd ə tʃeər ɒn ðə ʃɔːr wɪð ə ˈtʃɪər.fəl bɔɪ huː tʃoʊz tə ʃaʊt əˈbaʊt haʊ maɪ ˈbrʌð.ərz kæt kʌt hɪz hænd ˈkætʃ.ɪŋ tʃiːp ˈtʃɒk.lət tʃɪps æt ðə ˈkʌmf.tə.bəl ˈres.tər.ɒnt ˌdaʊnˈtaʊn/

## Test de word stress

Di estas palabras en voz alta. La sílaba en MAYÚSCULAS es la fuerte.

1. **COM**-fortable (no com-FOR-ta-ble)
2. **IN**-teresting (no in-te-REST-ing)
3. **VEG**-table (no ve-ge-TA-ble)
4. in-for-**MA**-tion (no in-FOR-ma-tion)
5. **RES**-taurant (no res-tau-RANT)
6. **HOS**-pital (no hos-PI-tal)
7. **CHOC**-late (no cho-co-LA-te)
8. e-**CON**-omy (no e-co-NO-my)
9. **TEL**-ephone (no te-LE-phone)
10. ab-so-**LUTE**-ly (no AB-so-lute-ly)

## Test final: frase con todos los sonidos A2

Grábate diciendo esta frase y escúchate:

"She watched the show at the comfortable restaurant, where my brother ran to catch the bus downtown. The boy enjoyed his chocolate, but the man in the hat cut his hand on a sharp chip."

/ʃiː wɒtʃt ðə ʃoʊ æt ðə ˈkʌmf.tə.bəl ˈres.tər.ɒnt weər maɪ ˈbrʌð.ər ræn tə kætʃ ðə bʌs ˌdaʊnˈtaʊn ðə bɔɪ ɪnˈdʒɔɪd hɪz ˈtʃɒk.lət bʌt ðə mæn ɪn ðə hæt kʌt hɪz hænd ɒn ə ʃɑːrp tʃɪp/

Comprueba:
- /ʃ/ vs /tʃ/: "she" vs "watched", "show" vs "chocolate", "sharp" vs "chip"
- /r/: "brother", "restaurant", "sharp" — lengua curvada, sin tocar
- /æ/ vs /ʌ/: "man/hat" vs "but/cut/bus"
- Diptongos: "show" /oʊ/, "my" /aɪ/, "boy" /ɔɪ/, "downtown" /aʊ/
- Word stress: **COM**fortable, **RES**taurant, **CHOC**olate

| /ʃ/ vs /tʃ/ | /ʃ/ = aire continuo ("shhh"); /tʃ/ = golpe seco |

| Diptongos | La vocal se MUEVE. Exagera el movimiento. |
| /æ/ vs /ʌ/ | /æ/ = boca MUY abierta (dentista); /ʌ/ = relajada ("uh") |
