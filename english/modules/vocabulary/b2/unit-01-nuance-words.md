# Unit 01 — Nuance Words

## Objetivo

Dominar 10 adjetivos sofisticados que te permiten expresar ideas con matices. En B2 ya no basta con "good/bad/important". Necesitas palabras que transmitan grados, actitudes y perspectivas. Estas son las que separan al estudiante intermedio del hablante avanzado.

## Palabras

| English | IPA | Spanish | Example | Trampa/Note |
|---------|-----|---------|---------|-------------|
| subtle | /ˈsʌt̬.əl/ | sutil | There's a **subtle** difference between "big" and "large". | La "b" es MUDA: /ˈsʌt̬.əl/, no /ˈsʌb.təl/. Error muy comun. |
| thorough | /ˈθɜːr.oʊ/ | exhaustivo, minucioso | She did a **thorough** analysis of the data. | Pronunciacion: /ˈθɜːr.oʊ/, NO como "through" /θruː/. Son palabras diferentes. |
| inevitable | /ɪˈnev.ə.t̬ə.bəl/ | inevitable | Climate change is **inevitable** if we don't act now. | Misma palabra en espanol e ingles, pero pronunciacion muy diferente: /ɪˈnev.ə.t̬ə.bəl/. |
| profound | /prəˈfaʊnd/ | profundo (intelectual) | The book had a **profound** impact on my thinking. | Para cosas fisicas usa "deep". "Profound" es para ideas, emociones, impacto intelectual. |
| reluctant | /rɪˈlʌk.tənt/ | reacio, reticente | He was **reluctant** to accept the job offer. | "Reluctant TO + verb". No confundir con "reluciente" (shiny). |
| compelling | /kəmˈpel.ɪŋ/ | convincente, fascinante | She made a **compelling** argument for the change. | Mas fuerte que "interesting" o "convincing". Algo "compelling" te arrastra, no puedes ignorarlo. |
| spontaneous | /spɑːnˈteɪ.ni.əs/ | espontaneo | Let's be **spontaneous** — let's go on a road trip right now! | Pronunciacion: /spɑːnˈteɪ.ni.əs/. Cuatro silabas. La "e" suena /eɪ/. |
| legitimate | /lɪˈdʒɪt̬.ə.mət/ | legitimo, valido | That's a **legitimate** concern — we should address it. | Coloquialmente abreviado como "legit" /ˈledʒ.ɪt/: "That's legit" = eso es real/valido. |
| arbitrary | /ˈɑːr.bə.trer.i/ | arbitrario | The deadline seems completely **arbitrary** — why March 15th? | Acento en primera silaba: AR-bi-tra-ry. Implica "sin razon logica". |
| ambiguous | /æmˈbɪɡ.ju.əs/ | ambiguo | The instructions were **ambiguous** — nobody knew what to do. | "Ambiguous" = puede interpretarse de varias formas. "Vague" = simplemente poco claro. |

## Ejercicios

### Fill in the blank

1. The _______ between the two colors is so _______ that most people can't tell them apart.
2. After a _______ investigation, the police found the missing evidence.
3. Change is _______ — you can't avoid it forever.
4. The documentary had a _______ effect on how I see the world.
5. She was _______ to share her personal information online.
6. His speech was so _______ that everyone changed their minds.
7. The trip was completely _______ — we just decided to go that morning.
8. You have a _______ right to disagree with your boss.
9. The rules seem _______ — there's no logical reason behind them.
10. The contract language is _______, so we need a lawyer to clarify it.

### Translate to English

1. Hay una diferencia sutil entre estas dos palabras.
2. Ella hizo un analisis exhaustivo del informe.
3. Fue reacio a admitir su error.
4. Las reglas parecen completamente arbitrarias.
5. El contrato es ambiguo — necesitamos aclararlo.

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. There's a subtle difference between these two words.
2. She did a thorough analysis of the report.
3. He was reluctant to admit his mistake.
4. The rules seem completely arbitrary.
5. The contract is ambiguous — we need to clarify it.

</details>

### Pronunciation challenge — Mark the silent/tricky letters

1. subtle → the _______ is silent
2. thorough → sounds like _______, NOT like "through"
3. inevitable → stress is on the _______ syllable

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. "b" is silent: /ˈsʌt̬.əl/
2. sounds like "THUR-oh" /ˈθɜːr.oʊ/, NOT like "through" /θruː/
3. stress on the SECOND syllable: i-NEV-i-ta-ble

</details>
