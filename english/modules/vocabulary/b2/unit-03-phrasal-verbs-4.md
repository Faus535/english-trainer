# Unit 03 — Phrasal Verbs 4

## Objetivo

Dominar 10 phrasal verbs de nivel B2 que son mas abstractos y sofisticados que los de B1. Estos no se pueden adivinar por sus partes — hay que aprenderlos. Son los que usan los nativos en reuniones, debates y conversaciones reflexivas. Cuando los domines, sonaras natural en contextos profesionales.

## Palabras

| English | IPA | Spanish | Example | Trampa/Note |
|---------|-----|---------|---------|-------------|
| account for | /əˈkaʊnt fɔːr/ | explicar, justificar; representar | Online sales **account for** 60% of our revenue. / How do you **account for** the missing money? | Dos significados: 1) representar un porcentaje, 2) dar explicacion de algo. |
| boil down to | /bɔɪl ˈdaʊn tuː/ | reducirse a | The problem **boils down to** a lack of communication. | Informal pero muy usado. Significa "en esencia, el problema es...". |
| come across | /kʌm əˈkrɑːs/ | encontrar por casualidad; parecer/dar impresion | I **came across** an interesting article. / She **comes across** as very confident. | Dos significados: 1) encontrar algo sin buscarlo, 2) la impresion que das. |
| dawn on | /dɔːn ɒn/ | caer en cuenta, darse cuenta | It suddenly **dawned on** me that I had left my wallet at home. | Siempre "dawn ON someone". Implica realizacion repentina. "It dawned on me" es la estructura tipica. |
| fall through | /fɔːl θruː/ | fracasar, caerse (plan/acuerdo) | The deal **fell through** at the last minute. | Solo para planes/acuerdos que no se completan. No para personas. |
| go along with | /ɡoʊ əˈlɔːŋ wɪð/ | aceptar, seguir la corriente | I don't agree, but I'll **go along with** the majority. | Implica aceptar algo aunque no estes totalmente de acuerdo. |
| iron out | /ˈaɪ.ɚn aʊt/ | resolver (problemas menores), pulir | We need to **iron out** a few details before signing the contract. | Para problemas pequenos o detalles. No para crisis graves. Metafora: planchar arrugas. |
| live up to | /lɪv ˈʌp tuː/ | estar a la altura de | The movie didn't **live up to** my expectations. | "Live up to expectations/standards/promises". Puede ser positivo o negativo. |
| rule out | /ruːl ˈaʊt/ | descartar | We can't **rule out** the possibility of delays. | Muy comun en negocios y medicina. "Rule out" = eliminar como opcion. |
| stem from | /stem frɒm/ | derivar de, tener origen en | His anger **stems from** childhood experiences. | Formal. Para hablar de causas y origenes. "The problem stems from poor planning." |

## Ejercicios

### Fill in the blank

1. Online shopping _______ _______ over 70% of total sales last year.
2. The issue _______ _______ _______ a simple misunderstanding.
3. I _______ _______ an old friend at the supermarket yesterday.
4. It _______ _______ me that I had been approaching the problem wrong.
5. The partnership _______ _______ because they couldn't agree on terms.
6. I'll _______ _______ _______ whatever the team decides.
7. Let's _______ _______ the remaining issues before the launch.
8. The sequel didn't _______ _______ _______ the original film.
9. The doctors haven't _______ _______ cancer yet — more tests are needed.
10. Most of his problems _______ _______ a difficult childhood.

1. accounted for
2. boils down to
3. came across
4. dawned on
5. fell through
6. go along with
7. iron out
8. live up to
9. ruled out
10. stem from

### Translate to English

1. El problema se reduce a falta de dinero.
2. De repente cai en cuenta de que tenia razon.
3. No podemos descartar esa posibilidad.
4. La pelicula no estuvo a la altura de las expectativas.
5. Su inseguridad deriva de experiencias de la infancia.
