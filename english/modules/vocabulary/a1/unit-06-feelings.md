# Unit 06 — Feelings

## Objetivo

Activar 10 adjetivos emocionales que reconoces al leer pero que sustituyes por "happy", "sad" o "good" cuando hablas. Tu rango emocional en ingles es probablemente de 5 palabras — vamos a ampliarlo a 15.

## Palabras

| English | IPA | Spanish | Example |
|---------|-----|---------|---------|
| angry | /ˈæŋ.ɡri/ | enfadado, enojado | She was really **angry** when she found out he lied. |
| worried | /ˈwʌr.id/ | preocupado | I'm **worried** about my exam results. |
| excited | /ɪkˈsaɪ.tɪd/ | emocionado, ilusionado | The kids are so **excited** about the trip to Disneyland! |
| bored | /bɔːrd/ | aburrido (la persona) | I'm **bored**. There's nothing to do here. |
| surprised | /sərˈpraɪzd/ | sorprendido | I was **surprised** to see her at the party. I thought she was abroad. |
| confused | /kənˈfjuːzd/ | confundido | I'm **confused**. Can you explain it again? |
| proud | /praʊd/ | orgulloso | Her parents are very **proud** of her achievements. |
| jealous | /ˈdʒel.əs/ | celoso, envidioso | He gets **jealous** when she talks to other men. |
| relieved | /rɪˈliːvd/ | aliviado | I was so **relieved** when the doctor said everything was fine. |
| embarrassed | /ɪmˈbær.əst/ | avergonzado | I was **embarrassed** when I called my teacher "Mom" by mistake. |

### Trampas y notas

- **bored vs. boring**: "I'm bored" = yo estoy aburrido (yo siento aburrimiento). "I'm boring" = yo soy aburrido (yo aburro a los demas). ENORME diferencia. Lo mismo aplica a: excited/exciting, confused/confusing, surprised/surprising, etc.
- **embarrassed** ≠ "embarazada": FALSO AMIGO CLASICO. "Embarrassed" = avergonzado. "Pregnant" = embarazada. Uno de los errores mas famosos del espanol al ingles.
- **jealous** vs. **envious**: "Jealous" = celoso (miedo de perder algo/alguien). "Envious" = envidioso (querer lo que otro tiene). En conversacion informal se usan casi como sinonimos.
- **proud**: Se dice "proud OF someone/something" (no "proud about" ni "proud for").
- **worried**: Se dice "worried ABOUT something" (no "worried for" ni "worried of").

## Ejercicios

### Fill in the blank

1. I'm so _______ about the concert next week! I can't wait!
2. She was _______ when nobody remembered her birthday.
3. Don't be _______. Let me explain it one more time.
4. He was _______ because his flight was delayed three hours.
5. I'm _______ about my job interview tomorrow.
6. The movie was terrible. I was _______ the whole time.
7. We were _______ to hear that everyone was safe after the earthquake.
8. She felt _______ when she tripped in front of everyone.
9. I'm very _______ of my son. He graduated top of his class.
10. She's _______ of her sister because she has a bigger house.

2. angry / surprised
3. confused
4. angry
5. worried

8. embarrassed

### Translate to English

1. Estoy preocupado por mi madre. Esta en el hospital.
2. Estoy aburrido. ¿Hacemos algo?
3. Estoy orgulloso de mi equipo.
4. Me senti avergonzado cuando me equivoque de nombre.
5. Estaba aliviada cuando termino el examen.

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. I'm worried about my mother. She's in the hospital.
2. I'm bored. Shall we do something?
3. I'm proud of my team.
4. I felt embarrassed when I got the name wrong.
5. She was relieved when the exam was over.

</details>
