# Unit 01 — Daily Actions

## Objetivo

Activar 10 verbos de acciones cotidianas que ya reconoces al leer pero no produces de forma natural al hablar. Estas son las acciones que haces TODOS los dias — necesitas decirlas sin pensar.

## Palabras

| English | IPA | Spanish | Example |
|---------|-----|---------|---------|
| wake up | /weɪk ʌp/ | despertarse | I **wake up** at 6:30 every morning, but I don't get out of bed until 7. |
| get up | /ɡet ʌp/ | levantarse | Come on, **get up**! You're going to be late for work. |
| go | /ɡoʊ/ | ir | I **go** to the gym three times a week. |
| come | /kʌm/ | venir | Can you **come** to my house after work? |
| eat | /iːt/ | comer | We usually **eat** dinner at 8 pm. |
| drink | /drɪŋk/ | beber | I **drink** two cups of coffee before noon. |
| sleep | /sliːp/ | dormir | I need to **sleep** at least seven hours or I feel terrible. |
| work | /wɜːrk/ | trabajar | She **works** from home on Fridays. |
| buy | /baɪ/ | comprar | I need to **buy** some groceries on my way home. |
| pay | /peɪ/ | pagar | Can I **pay** with my credit card? |

### Trampas y notas

- **wake up vs. get up**: "Wake up" es abrir los ojos. "Get up" es levantarte de la cama. Son dos acciones diferentes. "I woke up at 6 but got up at 6:30."
- **go vs. come**: En espanol usamos "venir" cuando alguien se acerca. En ingles, "come" se usa desde la perspectiva del oyente: "I'm coming!" (voy para alla, hacia ti).
- **pay**: Se dice "pay FOR something" (pagar por algo), no "pay something" directamente. "I paid for the tickets" (no "I paid the tickets").
- **buy vs. pay**: "Buy" = adquirir el producto. "Pay" = dar el dinero. "I bought a shirt and paid with cash."

## Ejercicios

### Fill in the blank

1. I usually _______ at 7 am, but on weekends I _______ at 9.
2. Do you want to _______ to the cinema tonight?
3. She doesn't _______ coffee. She prefers tea.
4. How many hours do you _______ every night?
5. I need to _______ some new shoes. These are broken.
6. Can we _______ the bill, please? — Sure, how would you like to _______?
7. My husband _______ in a hospital. He's a nurse.
8. _______ here! I want to show you something.
9. The kids don't want to _______ their vegetables.
10. Please _______ _______! We're leaving in five minutes!
