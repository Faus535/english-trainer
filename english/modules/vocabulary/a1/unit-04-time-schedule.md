# Unit 04 — Time & Schedule

## Objetivo

Activar 10 adverbios y expresiones de tiempo que reconoces perfectamente al leer pero que no usas cuando hablas. Estos son los "conectores temporales" que hacen que tu ingles suene natural en vez de robotico.

## Palabras

| English | IPA | Spanish | Example |
|---------|-----|---------|---------|
| early | /ˈɜːr.li/ | temprano, pronto | I arrived **early**, so I had time for a coffee. |
| late | /leɪt/ | tarde | Sorry I'm **late**. There was a lot of traffic. |
| soon | /suːn/ | pronto | The meeting will start **soon**. Please take your seats. |
| already | /ɔːlˈred.i/ | ya | I've **already** finished the report. You can review it now. |
| yet | /jet/ | todavia, aun | Have you eaten **yet**? — No, not **yet**. |
| still | /stɪl/ | todavia, aun (algo continua) | She's **still** working. She hasn't left the office. |
| often | /ˈɒf.ən/ | a menudo | I **often** forget my keys at home. |
| seldom | /ˈsel.dəm/ | rara vez, pocas veces | We **seldom** go to the cinema anymore. We watch Netflix instead. |
| immediately | /ɪˈmiː.di.ət.li/ | inmediatamente | Call me **immediately** if anything goes wrong. |
| eventually | /ɪˈven.tʃu.ə.li/ | finalmente, al final | We got lost, but we **eventually** found the restaurant. |

### Trampas y notas

- **still vs. yet vs. already**: Este trio es la pesadilla del hispanohablante. "Still" = algo SIGUE pasando (I'm still waiting). "Yet" = algo NO ha pasado todavia, en preguntas y negaciones (Have you finished yet? / I haven't finished yet). "Already" = algo ya ha pasado, antes de lo esperado (I've already done it).
- **eventually** ≠ "eventualmente": FALSO AMIGO GRAVE. "Eventually" = al final, despues de mucho tiempo. "Eventualmente" en espanol (posiblemente) = "possibly" o "perhaps" en ingles.
- **late** vs. **lately**: "Late" = tarde (llegar tarde). "Lately" = ultimamente. "I've been busy lately" (no "late").
- **seldom**: Mas formal que "rarely". En conversacion normal se usa mas "rarely" o "hardly ever".
- **soon**: No confundir con "early". "Soon" = en poco tiempo desde ahora. "Early" = antes de la hora esperada.

## Ejercicios

### Fill in the blank

1. Have you called the doctor _______? — No, not _______.
2. I've _______ seen that movie. I don't want to see it again.
3. It's 9:15 and the meeting was at 9:00. You're _______.
4. She's _______ sleeping. Don't wake her up.
5. We _______ eat out. Maybe once or twice a year.
6. The bus leaves _______. We need to hurry.
7. I woke up _______ this morning because I couldn't sleep.
8. If there's a fire, leave the building _______.
9. I _______ go for a walk after dinner. Maybe four times a week.
10. He didn't pass the first time, but he _______ got his driving license.

2. already
3. late
4. still
5. seldom

7. early
8. immediately
9. often

### Translate to English

1. Todavia no he terminado. (algo que no ha pasado)
2. Todavia estoy esperando el autobus. (algo que continua)
3. Ya he comprado los billetes.
4. Al final encontramos un hotel barato.
5. Rara vez como comida rapida.

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. I haven't finished yet.
2. I'm still waiting for the bus.
3. I've already bought the tickets.
4. We eventually found a cheap hotel.
5. I seldom eat fast food. / I rarely eat fast food.

</details>

## Repaso

| Front | Back |
|-------|------|
| ya (algo ha pasado) | **already** — /ɔːlˈred.i/ — I've already eaten, thanks. |
| todavia no (en pregunta/negacion) | **yet** — /jet/ — I haven't decided yet. |
| todavia (algo continua) | **still** — /stɪl/ — He's still at work. |
| finalmente, al final | **eventually** — /ɪˈven.tʃu.ə.li/ — We eventually agreed on a price. |
| inmediatamente | **immediately** — /ɪˈmiː.di.ət.li/ — Please reply immediately. |
