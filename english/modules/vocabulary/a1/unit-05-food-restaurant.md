# Unit 05 — Food & Restaurant

## Objetivo

Activar 10 palabras esenciales para comer fuera de casa. Las reconoces al leer un menu o una resena, pero cuando estas en un restaurante real en ingles, te bloqueas. Este vocabulario te permite SOBREVIVIR pidiendo comida en cualquier pais anglosajono.

## Palabras

| English | IPA | Spanish | Example |
|---------|-----|---------|---------|
| meal | /miːl/ | comida (el acto de comer) | We had a lovely **meal** at that Italian restaurant. |
| dish | /dɪʃ/ | plato (de comida) | What's the most popular **dish** on the menu? |
| recipe | /ˈres.ɪ.pi/ | receta | I found a great **recipe** for chocolate cake online. |
| ingredients | /ɪnˈɡriː.di.ənts/ | ingredientes | What **ingredients** do I need for this soup? |
| waiter | /ˈweɪ.tər/ | camarero | Excuse me, could we ask the **waiter** for more water? |
| bill | /bɪl/ | cuenta (del restaurante) | Can we have the **bill**, please? |
| tip | /tɪp/ | propina | In the US, you usually leave a 15-20% **tip**. |
| appetizer | /ˈæp.ɪ.taɪ.zər/ | aperitivo, entrante | Would you like an **appetizer** before your main course? |
| dessert | /dɪˈzɜːrt/ | postre | For **dessert**, I'll have the cheesecake. |
| takeaway | /ˈteɪ.kə.weɪ/ | comida para llevar | I don't feel like cooking. Let's order a **takeaway**. |

### Trampas y notas

- **recipe**: La pronunciacion NO es "re-saip". Es /ˈres.ɪ.pi/ — tres silabas, acento en la primera.
- **dessert** /dɪˈzɜːrt/ vs. **desert** /ˈdez.ərt/: "Dessert" (postre) tiene doble S y acento en la segunda silaba. "Desert" (desierto) tiene una S y acento en la primera. Un truco: dessert tiene doble S porque siempre quieres MAS.
- **bill** vs. **check**: En UK se dice "the bill". En US se dice "the check". Ambos significan la cuenta.
- **dish** vs. **plate**: "Dish" = el plato como comida preparada ("a fish dish"). "Plate" = el objeto fisico de ceramica donde comes.
- **takeaway** (UK) vs. **takeout** (US): Mismo significado, diferente variante. Ambos son correctos.
- **waiter/waitress**: "Waitress" es femenino, pero hoy en dia muchos usan "server" como termino neutro.

## Ejercicios

### Fill in the blank

1. The _______ was so good that I asked for the _______ to make it at home.
2. Could you call the _______? I'd like to order.
3. We had a three-course _______: an _______, a main course, and a _______.
4. Can I have the _______, please? — Sure. Would you like to pay by card?
5. In Spain, leaving a _______ is optional. In the US, it's expected.
6. I don't want to eat here. Let's get a _______ instead.
7. What _______ do I need to make a tortilla espanola?
8. Their best _______ is the grilled salmon. You have to try it.
9. We had a wonderful _______ at the new restaurant downtown.
10. For _______, we shared a chocolate mousse.

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. dish / recipe
2. waiter
3. meal / appetizer / dessert
4. bill
5. tip
6. takeaway
7. ingredients
8. dish
9. meal
10. dessert

</details>

### Translate to English

1. ¿Puedes traer la cuenta, por favor?
2. Dejamos una propina del 15%.
3. ¿Cual es el plato del dia?
4. Necesito comprar los ingredientes para la cena.
5. Pedimos comida para llevar porque estabamos cansados.

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. Can we have the bill, please?
2. We left a 15% tip.
3. What's the dish of the day?
4. I need to buy the ingredients for dinner.
5. We ordered takeaway because we were tired.

</details>
