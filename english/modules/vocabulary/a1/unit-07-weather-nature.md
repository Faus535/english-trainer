# Unit 07 — Weather & Nature

## Objetivo

Activar 10 palabras de clima y naturaleza que reconoces al leer noticias o previsiones del tiempo pero que no produces cuando quieres describir lo que ves por la ventana o planificar actividades al aire libre.

## Palabras

| English | IPA | Spanish | Example |
|---------|-----|---------|---------|
| storm | /stɔːrm/ | tormenta | There's a big **storm** coming tonight. Don't forget your umbrella. |
| fog | /fɒɡ/ | niebla | The **fog** was so thick I couldn't see the road ahead. |
| breeze | /briːz/ | brisa | There's a nice **breeze** today. It's not too hot. |
| drought | /draʊt/ | sequia | The **drought** lasted three months. The rivers were almost dry. |
| flood | /flʌd/ | inundacion | Heavy rain caused **floods** in several towns along the coast. |
| sunset | /ˈsʌn.set/ | puesta de sol | We watched the **sunset** from the top of the hill. It was beautiful. |
| cliff | /klɪf/ | acantilado | Be careful near the edge of the **cliff**. It's a 100-meter drop. |
| valley | /ˈvæl.i/ | valle | The village is in a green **valley** between two mountains. |
| soil | /sɔɪl/ | suelo, tierra (de cultivo) | The **soil** in this area is perfect for growing tomatoes. |
| forest | /ˈfɒr.ɪst/ | bosque | They went hiking through a dense **forest** in the mountains. |

### Trampas y notas

- **drought**: La pronunciacion es /draʊt/ — suena como "draut", NO como "drought" leido en espanol. La "gh" es completamente muda.
- **flood** /flʌd/: No se pronuncia "flu-d". La doble O aqui suena como /ʌ/ (como la "u" de "but").
- **soil** vs. **floor** vs. **ground**: "Soil" = tierra para cultivar. "Floor" = suelo de un edificio. "Ground" = suelo exterior en general. "The plant needs good soil" (no "good floor" ni "good ground").
- **forest** vs. **wood(s)**: "Forest" = bosque grande. "Wood" o "woods" = bosque pequeno. "We went for a walk in the woods" (cerca de casa). "The Amazon forest" (enorme).
- **breeze** vs. **wind**: "Breeze" = viento suave y agradable. "Wind" = viento en general (puede ser fuerte). "A gentle breeze" vs. "strong wind".

## Ejercicios

### Fill in the blank

1. The _______ destroyed several houses and knocked down trees across the city.
2. Visibility is very low because of the _______. Drive slowly.
3. There's a cool _______ coming from the sea. It feels great.
4. The _______ has affected farmers badly. They've had no rain for months.
5. After the heavy rain, the river overflowed and caused _______s in the town.
6. Let's go to the _______ to watch the _______ this evening.
7. Don't walk too close to the _______! It's dangerous.
8. The _______ was full of beautiful wildflowers in spring.
9. This _______ is too dry to grow anything. It needs more water.
10. We got lost in the _______ because there were no signs on the trail.

### Translate to English

1. Hay mucha niebla esta manana. No se ve nada.
2. La sequia ha durado todo el verano.
3. Paseamos por el bosque durante dos horas.
4. El valle estaba cubierto de nieve en invierno.
5. La puesta de sol desde el acantilado fue increible.

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. There's a lot of fog this morning. You can't see anything.
2. The drought has lasted all summer.
3. We walked through the forest for two hours.
4. The valley was covered in snow in winter.
5. The sunset from the cliff was incredible.
