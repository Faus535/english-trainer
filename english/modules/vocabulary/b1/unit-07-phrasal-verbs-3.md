# Unit 07 — Phrasal Verbs 3

## Objetivo

Tercera ronda de phrasal verbs. Estos 10 son los que escucharas en peliculas, podcasts y conversaciones casuales constantemente. Son imprescindibles para entender ingles hablado real y dejar de sonar a "libro de texto".

## Palabras

| English | IPA | Spanish | Example | Trampa/Note |
|---------|-----|---------|---------|-------------|
| back up | /bæk ˈʌp/ | respaldar; hacer copia; retroceder | Can you **back up** your argument with data? / **Back up** your files regularly. | Tres significados: 1) apoyar/respaldar, 2) copia de seguridad, 3) ir hacia atras. |
| blow up | /bloʊ ˈʌp/ | explotar; enfadarse mucho; hacerse viral | The building **blew up**. / He **blew up** when he heard the news. / That video **blew up** on TikTok. | Tres significados. El tercero (hacerse viral) es moderno y muy comun. |
| break through | /breɪk ˈθruː/ | abrirse paso, lograr un avance | Scientists **broke through** with a new cancer treatment. | "Breakthrough" (sustantivo, una palabra) = avance importante. "Break through" (verbo, dos palabras). |
| catch up | /kætʃ ˈʌp/ | ponerse al dia | I need to **catch up** on my emails. / Let's **catch up** over coffee. | Dos usos: 1) ponerse al dia con trabajo/noticias, 2) quedar con alguien para hablar. "Catch up WITH/ON". |
| cut off | /kʌt ˈɒf/ | cortar (comunicacion, suministro); interrumpir | They **cut off** our electricity. / Don't **cut me off** when I'm speaking! | Separable: "cut someone off". Tambien: "cutoff" (sustantivo) = limite. |
| drop out | /drɑːp ˈaʊt/ | abandonar (estudios, competicion) | He **dropped out** of university in his second year. | "Drop out OF" algo. Un "dropout" (sustantivo) = persona que abandono los estudios. Steve Jobs was a college dropout. |
| end up | /end ˈʌp/ | terminar, acabar (resultado inesperado) | We got lost and **ended up** in the wrong city. | "End up + -ing" o "end up + in/at". Siempre implica resultado no planeado. |
| fill in | /fɪl ˈɪn/ | rellenar (formulario); poner al dia | Please **fill in** this form. / Can you **fill me in** on what happened? | "Fill in" (britanico) = "fill out" (americano) para formularios. "Fill someone in" = informar. |
| get over | /ɡet ˈoʊ.vɚ/ | superar (enfermedad, ruptura) | It took her months to **get over** the breakup. | "Get over it!" = !superalo ya! Muy coloquial. No confundir con "overcome" (mas formal). |
| hold on | /hoʊld ˈɒn/ | esperar; agarrarse | **Hold on** — I'll be right back. / **Hold on** tight! | "Hold on" por telefono = espera. "Hold on to" algo = agarrarse. Muy comun en conversacion. |

## Ejercicios

### Fill in the blank

1. _______ _______ — let me check that for you.
2. The video _______ _______ overnight and got 10 million views.
3. She _______ _______ of the race because of a knee injury.
4. We _______ _______ eating pizza at 2 AM — we didn't plan it.
5. Please _______ _______ the application form and submit it online.
6. I need to _______ _______ on sleep — I've been working too much.
7. It took him a year to _______ _______ losing his job.
8. _______ _______ your claims with facts, not opinions.
9. They _______ _______ our internet connection without warning.
10. The team finally _______ _______ after years of failed experiments.

<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. Hold on
2. blew up
3. dropped out
4. ended up
5. fill in
6. catch up
7. get over
8. Back up
9. cut off
10. broke through

</details>

### Translate to English

1. Necesito ponerme al dia con el trabajo.
2. Al final acabamos yendo a otro restaurante.
3. No me interrumpas cuando estoy hablando.
4. Ella abandono la universidad para montar su empresa.
5. Tienes que superar esa ruptura.

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. I need to catch up on work.
2. We ended up going to a different restaurant.
3. Don't cut me off when I'm speaking.
4. She dropped out of university to start her own company.
5. You need to get over that breakup.

</details>

### Multiple meaning — Which meaning is used?

1. "Back up your files every day." → a) respaldar b) copia de seguridad c) retroceder
2. "He blew up when I told him." → a) explotar fisicamente b) enfadarse c) hacerse viral
3. "Let's catch up next week." → a) ponerse al dia con trabajo b) quedar para hablar

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. b) copia de seguridad
2. b) enfadarse
3. b) quedar para hablar

</details>

## Repaso

| Front | Back |
|-------|------|
| respaldar / hacer copia | **back up** — /bæk ˈʌp/ — Back up your data regularly. |
| explotar / hacerse viral | **blow up** — /bloʊ ˈʌp/ — The meme blew up online. |
| ponerse al dia | **catch up** — /kætʃ ˈʌp/ — I need to catch up on emails. |
| acabar (inesperadamente) | **end up** — /end ˈʌp/ — We ended up staying home. |
| superar | **get over** — /ɡet ˈoʊ.vɚ/ — Get over it and move on. |
