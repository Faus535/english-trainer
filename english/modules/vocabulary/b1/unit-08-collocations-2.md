# Unit 08 — Collocations 2

## Objetivo

Dominar 10 colocaciones (combinaciones verbo + sustantivo) de nivel B1. Las colocaciones son lo que separa al estudiante del hablante natural. Un nativo dice "raise awareness", nunca "increase awareness". Decir la combinacion correcta te hace sonar fluido al instante.

## Palabras

| English | IPA | Spanish | Example | Trampa/Note |
|---------|-----|---------|---------|-------------|
| raise awareness | /reɪz əˈwer.nəs/ | concienciar, crear conciencia | The campaign aims to **raise awareness** about mental health. | No "increase awareness" ni "create awareness". Siempre "RAISE awareness". |
| meet a deadline | /miːt ə ˈded.laɪn/ | cumplir un plazo | We need to work overtime to **meet the deadline**. | No "complete a deadline" ni "finish a deadline". Se "cumple" (meet) o se "incumple" (miss). |
| reach a conclusion | /riːtʃ ə kənˈkluː.ʒən/ | llegar a una conclusion | After hours of debate, they finally **reached a conclusion**. | Tambien: "come to a conclusion", "draw a conclusion". Pero NO "arrive to a conclusion". |
| face a challenge | /feɪs ə ˈtʃæl.ɪndʒ/ | enfrentar un reto | The team **faced a major challenge** when the budget was cut. | "Face" = afrontar directamente. Tambien: "face a problem", "face the truth". |
| gain experience | /ɡeɪn ɪkˈspɪr.i.əns/ | adquirir experiencia | She **gained valuable experience** during her internship. | No "win experience" ni "earn experience". "Gain" = adquirir gradualmente. |
| draw attention | /drɔː əˈten.ʃən/ | llamar la atencion | The report **drew attention** to the growing inequality. | Tambien: "attract attention", "catch attention". Pero "draw attention TO" (hacia algo especifico). |
| pose a threat | /poʊz ə θret/ | representar una amenaza | Climate change **poses a serious threat** to coastal cities. | "Pose" aqui = presentar/representar. Tambien: "pose a risk", "pose a question". |
| take responsibility | /teɪk rɪˌspɑːn.səˈbɪl.ə.t̬i/ | asumir la responsabilidad | You need to **take responsibility** for your mistakes. | No "have responsibility" (que existe pero es diferente: "I have the responsibility of..." = es mi tarea). |
| break a habit | /breɪk ə ˈhæb.ɪt/ | romper un habito | It's hard to **break a bad habit** like smoking. | "Break" para habitos malos. Para habitos buenos: "develop/build a habit". |
| set a goal | /set ə ɡoʊl/ | fijar un objetivo | At the beginning of the year, I always **set new goals**. | Tambien: "set a target", "set a deadline". "Set" = establecer formalmente. |

## Ejercicios

### Fill in the blank (use the correct verb)

1. The organization works to _______ awareness about climate change.
2. If we don't _______ the deadline, we'll lose the client.
3. After reviewing all the data, the committee _______ a conclusion.
4. Starting a business means _______ many challenges.
5. Working abroad helps you _______ international experience.
6. The bold headline _______ attention to the scandal.
7. Nuclear weapons _______ a threat to all humanity.
8. A good leader _______ responsibility when things go wrong.
9. I'm trying to _______ the habit of checking my phone first thing in the morning.
10. Every January, millions of people _______ goals for the new year.

### Translate to English

1. Necesitamos concienciar sobre la salud mental.
2. No pudimos cumplir el plazo porque faltaba personal.
3. Tienes que asumir la responsabilidad de tus errores.
4. Quiero romper el habito de comer comida basura.
5. Me fije como objetivo hablar ingles con fluidez este ano.

2. We couldn't meet the deadline because we were short-staffed.

### Correct the collocation error

1. "We need to increase awareness about recycling." →
2. "She won a lot of experience at her last job." →
3. "I want to complete the deadline on time." →
4. "He doesn't want to have responsibility." →

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. "We need to **raise** awareness about recycling."
2. "She **gained** a lot of experience at her last job."
3. "I want to **meet** the deadline on time."
4. "He doesn't want to **take** responsibility."

</details>
