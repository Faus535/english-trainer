# Unit 03 — Phrasal Verbs 2

## Objetivo

Dominar 10 phrasal verbs esenciales de nivel B1. Ya conoces los basicos de A2 (look for, turn on, give up). Ahora toca los que usas a diario en conversaciones reales pero que muchos hispanohablantes evitan porque "suenan raro". Estos te hacen sonar natural, no como un libro de texto.

## Palabras

| English | IPA | Spanish | Example | Trampa/Note |
|---------|-----|---------|---------|-------------|
| bring up | /brɪŋ ˈʌp/ | sacar un tema; criar | Don't **bring up** politics at dinner. / She **brought up** three kids alone. | Dos significados muy diferentes. Contexto lo aclara. No confundir con "bring" solo. |
| carry on | /ˈkær.i ɒn/ | continuar, seguir | **Carry on** — you're doing great! | Similar a "go on" y "keep on". "Carry on" es muy britanico. Tambien: "carry-on luggage" (equipaje de mano). |
| deal with | /diːl wɪð/ | lidiar con, gestionar | I can't **deal with** this stress anymore. | Mas coloquial que "handle" o "manage". Muy comun en conversacion. |
| find out | /faɪnd ˈaʊt/ | descubrir, enterarse | I just **found out** that she's leaving the company. | Diferente de "find" (encontrar fisicamente). "Find out" = descubrir informacion. |
| go through | /ɡoʊ θruː/ | pasar por (experiencia); revisar | She's **going through** a difficult time. / Let's **go through** the document together. | Dos significados: experiencia dificil o revisar algo. |
| hang out | /hæŋ ˈaʊt/ | pasar el rato, quedar | We usually **hang out** at the park on weekends. | Informal. No lo uses en contextos formales. "Hang out WITH someone" no "hang out someone". |
| let down | /let ˈdaʊn/ | decepcionar, defraudar | I trusted him, but he **let me down**. | Separable: "let someone down". El objeto va en medio. "Don't let me down." |
| make up | /meɪk ˈʌp/ | inventar; reconciliarse; maquillarse | He **made up** an excuse. / They argued but **made up** the next day. | Tres significados. "Make up a story" = inventar. "Make up with someone" = reconciliarse. "Make-up" = maquillaje. |
| pick up | /pɪk ˈʌp/ | recoger; aprender (informal) | Can you **pick me up** at the station? / She **picked up** some Spanish while traveling. | Separable. Muchos significados: recoger (persona/objeto), aprender sin esfuerzo, mejorar ("business picked up"). |
| show off | /ʃoʊ ˈɒf/ | presumir, lucirse | He's always **showing off** his new car. | Connotacion negativa: alardear. "Show-off" (sustantivo) = presumido. |

## Ejercicios

### Fill in the blank

1. Please don't _______ _______ that topic — it makes her uncomfortable.
2. I need to _______ _______ all these emails before the end of the day.
3. We _______ _______ at the mall last Saturday — it was fun.
4. She _______ _______ that her flight was cancelled just two hours before.
5. Sorry I _______ you _______. I promise it won't happen again.
6. He _______ _______ the whole story. None of it was true.
7. She's _______ _______ a really tough divorce right now.
8. Stop _______ _______ — nobody's impressed.
9. Can you _______ _______ the kids from school today?
10. Just _______ _______ working — you're almost done.

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. bring up
2. go through
3. hung out
4. found out
5. let ... down
6. made up
7. going through
8. showing off
9. pick up
10. carry on

</details>

### Translate to English

1. Acabo de enterarme de que nos mudamos de oficina.
2. No me decepciones esta vez, por favor.
3. Ella crio a sus hijos sola despues del divorcio.
4. No puedo lidiar con tanto trabajo.
5. Dejad de presumir y poneos a trabajar.

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. I just found out that we're moving offices.
2. Don't let me down this time, please.
3. She brought up her children alone after the divorce.
4. I can't deal with so much work.
5. Stop showing off and get to work.

</details>

### Match the phrasal verb to its meaning

1. bring up → a) decepcionar
2. let down → b) inventar
3. make up → c) sacar un tema
4. pick up → d) pasar por (experiencia)
5. go through → e) recoger

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1-c, 2-a, 3-b, 4-e, 5-d

</details>

## Repaso

| Front | Back |
|-------|------|
| sacar un tema / criar | **bring up** — /brɪŋ ˈʌp/ — Don't bring up the past. |
| descubrir, enterarse | **find out** — /faɪnd ˈaʊt/ — I found out the truth. |
| decepcionar | **let down** — /let ˈdaʊn/ — She never lets me down. |
| inventar / reconciliarse | **make up** — /meɪk ˈʌp/ — He made up an excuse. |
| recoger / aprender informalmente | **pick up** — /pɪk ˈʌp/ — Pick me up at 8. |
