# Unit 02 — Describing People

## Objetivo

Aprender 10 adjetivos de personalidad que van mas alla de "good", "bad", "nice". En B1 necesitas describir personas con precision: en entrevistas, al hablar de companeros de trabajo, al contar historias. Estos adjetivos te permiten ser especifico y expresivo.

## Palabras

| English | IPA | Spanish | Example | Trampa/Note |
|---------|-----|---------|---------|-------------|
| stubborn | /ˈstʌb.ɚn/ | terco, testarudo | He's too **stubborn** to admit he was wrong. | Connotacion negativa. Si quieres ser positivo, usa "determined" o "persistent". |
| generous | /ˈdʒen.ɚ.əs/ | generoso | She's very **generous** — she always pays for everyone. | Pronunciacion: la "g" suena /dʒ/ como en "gentle", no /g/ como en "go". |
| shy | /ʃaɪ/ | timido | He's too **shy** to speak in public. | No confundir con "ashamed" (avergonzado). "Shy" es rasgo de personalidad, "ashamed" es por algo que hiciste. |
| outgoing | /ˈaʊt.ɡoʊ.ɪŋ/ | extrovertido, sociable | My sister is really **outgoing** — she talks to everyone. | Es lo opuesto a "shy". No confundir con "outgoing" (que se va): "the outgoing president". |
| ambitious | /æmˈbɪʃ.əs/ | ambicioso | She's very **ambitious** — she wants to be CEO by 40. | En ingles puede ser positivo o negativo segun el contexto. En espanol suele ser mas negativo. |
| humble | /ˈhʌm.bəl/ | humilde | Despite his success, he remains very **humble**. | La "h" se pronuncia. No es "umble". Y no confundir con "humiliate" (humillar). |
| selfish | /ˈsel.fɪʃ/ | egoista | Don't be so **selfish** — share with your brother! | No confundir con "self-ish" y "shellfish" (marisco). Pronunciacion diferente. |
| patient | /ˈpeɪ.ʃənt/ | paciente | You need to be **patient** — learning takes time. | Misma forma que "patient" (paciente de hospital). El contexto aclara. |
| loyal | /ˈlɔɪ.əl/ | leal, fiel | She's a **loyal** friend — she's always there for me. | Pronunciacion: dos silabas /ˈlɔɪ.əl/, no tres. "Royal" rima con "loyal". |
| determined | /dɪˈtɜːr.mɪnd/ | decidido, determinado | He's **determined** to finish his degree this year. | Version positiva de "stubborn". "Determined" = admiracion, "stubborn" = frustracion. |

## Ejercicios

### Fill in the blank

1. She never shares her things. She's really _______.
2. He donated half his salary to charity. He's incredibly _______.
3. My dog is very _______ — he won't change direction once he starts walking.
4. She's too _______ to raise her hand in class, but she knows all the answers.
5. He's very _______ — he wants to start his own company before he's 30.
6. Despite winning the award, she was very _______ about it.
7. Being a teacher requires being very _______ with children.
8. She's the most _______ person I know — she makes friends everywhere.
9. A _______ employee stays with the company through good and bad times.
10. She's _______ to learn English, and nothing will stop her.

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. selfish
2. generous
3. stubborn
4. shy
5. ambitious
6. humble
7. patient
8. outgoing
9. loyal
10. determined

</details>

### Translate to English

1. Mi jefe es muy terco, nunca escucha otras opiniones.
2. Es una persona muy generosa y humilde.
3. Soy demasiado timido para hablar en publico.
4. Ella esta decidida a conseguir ese trabajo.
5. Necesitas ser paciente y leal en una relacion.

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. My boss is very stubborn — he never listens to other opinions.
2. He/She is a very generous and humble person.
3. I'm too shy to speak in public.
4. She's determined to get that job.
5. You need to be patient and loyal in a relationship.

</details>

### Context usage — Positive or negative?

Clasifica cada adjetivo como positivo (+), negativo (−) o depende del contexto (~):

1. stubborn
2. generous
3. ambitious
4. selfish
5. determined

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. stubborn → (−) negativo
2. generous → (+) positivo
3. ambitious → (~) depende del contexto (positivo en ingles, a veces negativo en espanol)
4. selfish → (−) negativo
5. determined → (+) positivo

</details>
