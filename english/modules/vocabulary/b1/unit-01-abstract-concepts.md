# Unit 01 — Abstract Concepts

## Objetivo

Dominar 10 sustantivos abstractos que aparecen constantemente en textos, reuniones y conversaciones serias. Estas palabras te permiten hablar de ideas, problemas y soluciones sin recurrir siempre a "thing", "problem" o "way". Son el salto de A2 a B1: pasar de describir lo visible a expresar lo invisible.

## Palabras

| English | IPA | Spanish | Example | Trampa/Note |
|---------|-----|---------|---------|-------------|
| purpose | /ˈpɜːr.pəs/ | proposito, finalidad | The **purpose** of this meeting is to discuss the new strategy. | No confundir con "propose" (proponer). "Purpose" = finalidad, "propose" = sugerir. |
| issue | /ˈɪʃ.uː/ | problema, asunto, tema | We need to address this **issue** before launching the product. | No es "salida" (exit). Un "issue" es mas formal que "problem" y menos grave. |
| effort | /ˈef.ɚt/ | esfuerzo | She put a lot of **effort** into learning English. | Incontable en muchos contextos: "much effort" no "many efforts" (aunque "efforts" existe en plural formal). |
| lack | /læk/ | falta, carencia | The project failed due to a **lack** of funding. | Puede ser sustantivo y verbo: "a lack of" / "he lacks experience". |
| benefit | /ˈben.ɪ.fɪt/ | beneficio, ventaja | One **benefit** of remote work is saving time on commuting. | El acento va en la primera silaba: BEN-e-fit, no "be-NE-fit". |
| factor | /ˈfæk.tɚ/ | factor | Stress is a major **factor** in many health problems. | Pronunciacion: /ˈfæk.tɚ/ no /ˈfak.tor/. La "o" suena como schwa. |
| opportunity | /ˌɑː.pɚˈtuː.nə.t̬i/ | oportunidad | This job is a great **opportunity** to grow professionally. | "Opportunity" no lleva "h" como "oportunidad". Y se dice "opportunity TO do" no "opportunity OF doing". |
| approach | /əˈproʊtʃ/ | enfoque, planteamiento | We need a different **approach** to solve this problem. | Tambien es verbo: "approach the problem" = abordar el problema. No confundir con "reproach" (reproche). |
| process | /ˈprɑː.ses/ | proceso | The hiring **process** takes about three weeks. | En ingles americano: /ˈprɑː.ses/. En britanico: /ˈprəʊ.ses/. Plural: processes /ˈprɑː.ses.ɪz/. |
| outcome | /ˈaʊt.kʌm/ | resultado, desenlace | The **outcome** of the experiment was unexpected. | Mas preciso que "result". "Outcome" enfatiza la consecuencia final de un proceso. |

## Ejercicios

### Fill in the blank

1. The _______ of education is to prepare people for life, not just exams.
2. There's a serious _______ we need to discuss at the next meeting.
3. Despite all his _______, he didn't pass the exam.
4. The _______ of experience made it hard for her to find a job.
5. One key _______ of exercise is better mental health.
6. Weather is an important _______ in outdoor event planning.
7. Don't miss this _______ — it may not come again.
8. I think we should try a completely new _______ to this project.
9. Learning a language is a long _______, but it's worth it.
10. We're still waiting to see the _______ of the negotiations.

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. purpose
2. issue
3. effort
4. lack
5. benefit
6. factor
7. opportunity
8. approach
9. process
10. outcome

</details>

### Translate to English

1. El proposito de esta reunion es resolver el problema.
2. La falta de comunicacion es el factor principal.
3. Necesitamos un enfoque diferente para aprovechar esta oportunidad.
4. El resultado del proceso fue mejor de lo esperado.
5. El esfuerzo vale la pena cuando ves los beneficios.

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. The purpose of this meeting is to solve the problem (issue).
2. The lack of communication is the main factor.
3. We need a different approach to take advantage of this opportunity.
4. The outcome of the process was better than expected.
5. The effort is worth it when you see the benefits.

</details>

### Context usage — Choose the best word

1. "We have a _______ of volunteers. We need more people." (lack / issue)
2. "The main _______ of the delay was poor planning." (factor / approach)
3. "What was the _______ of the meeting? Did they agree?" (outcome / process)

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. lack — (denota carencia/ausencia)
2. factor — (causa contribuyente)
3. outcome — (resultado final)

</details>

## Repaso

| Front | Back |
|-------|------|
| proposito, finalidad | **purpose** — /ˈpɜːr.pəs/ — The purpose of this trip is to relax. |
| problema, asunto (formal) | **issue** — /ˈɪʃ.uː/ — We found a technical issue in the system. |
| falta, carencia | **lack** — /læk/ — There's a lack of trust between them. |
| enfoque, planteamiento | **approach** — /əˈproʊtʃ/ — Let's try a new approach. |
| resultado, desenlace | **outcome** — /ˈaʊt.kʌm/ — The outcome depends on several factors. |
