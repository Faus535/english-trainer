# Unit 04 — Academic Words

## Objetivo

Incorporar 10 palabras del registro academico y profesional que encontraras en articulos, informes, presentaciones y textos universitarios. Estas palabras son la puerta de entrada al ingles "serio": el que se usa para argumentar, analizar y convencer.

## Palabras

| English | IPA | Spanish | Example | Trampa/Note |
|---------|-----|---------|---------|-------------|
| research | /rɪˈsɜːrtʃ/ (v) /ˈriː.sɜːrtʃ/ (n) | investigacion; investigar | Recent **research** shows that sleep affects memory. | Incontable en ingles: "research" no "researches". NO "make a research" → "do/conduct research". |
| evidence | /ˈev.ɪ.dəns/ | evidencia, pruebas | There's strong **evidence** that exercise reduces stress. | Incontable: "evidence" no "evidences". "A piece of evidence" para una sola prueba. |
| analysis | /əˈnæl.ə.sɪs/ | analisis | The **analysis** revealed several problems. | Plural irregular: "analyses" /əˈnæl.ə.siːz/. No es "analisys" (error comun de escritura). |
| theory | /ˈθɪr.i/ | teoria | Darwin's **theory** of evolution changed science forever. | La "th" es /θ/ (como morder la lengua). Hispanohablantes suelen decir /ˈtiri/ — practica el sonido. |
| significant | /sɪɡˈnɪf.ɪ.kənt/ | significativo, importante | The company reported a **significant** increase in profits. | No significa "significante" (que significa poco en espanol). Significa "importante, considerable". |
| conclude | /kənˈkluːd/ | concluir | The report **concluded** that more investment was needed. | Mas formal que "finish". "Conclude" = llegar a una conclusion o terminar formalmente. |
| demonstrate | /ˈdem.ən.streɪt/ | demostrar | The experiment **demonstrated** that the theory was correct. | No es "manifestarse" (to protest). "Demonstrate" = mostrar/probar algo con evidencia. |
| contribute | /kənˈtrɪb.juːt/ | contribuir | Everyone should **contribute** to the team effort. | Acento en segunda silaba: con-TRIB-ute. "Contribute TO" no "contribute in". |
| assume | /əˈsuːm/ | suponer, asumir | I **assumed** you knew about the meeting. | Falso amigo parcial: en ingles solo significa "suponer" o "asumir un cargo/rol", nunca "aceptar algo que te dicen". |
| evaluate | /ɪˈvæl.ju.eɪt/ | evaluar | We need to **evaluate** the results before making a decision. | Mas formal y sistematico que "judge". Se usa en contextos profesionales y academicos. |

## Ejercicios

### Fill in the blank

1. The _______ suggests that reading improves vocabulary significantly.
2. There is no _______ to support that claim.
3. After careful _______, we found the root cause of the problem.
4. In _______, smoking causes cancer — this is a proven fact, not just a _______.
5. The study _______ that diet plays a _______ role in health.
6. Each team member must _______ to the final report.
7. Don't _______ everyone agrees with you — ask first.
8. We need to _______ all candidates before making an offer.
9. The professor _______ the concept using a simple experiment.
10. The researchers _______ that further study was necessary.

<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. research
2. evidence
3. analysis
4. theory (primera); theory (segunda)
5. demonstrated; significant
6. contribute
7. assume
8. evaluate
9. demonstrated
10. concluded

</details>

### Translate to English

1. La investigacion demuestra que el ejercicio es beneficioso.
2. No hay evidencia suficiente para esta teoria.
3. El analisis concluyo que el problema era significativo.
4. No asumas que todos van a contribuir.
5. Necesitamos evaluar los resultados de la investigacion.

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. Research demonstrates that exercise is beneficial.
2. There isn't enough evidence for this theory.
3. The analysis concluded that the problem was significant.
4. Don't assume that everyone will contribute.
5. We need to evaluate the results of the research.

</details>

### Common mistakes — Correct the error

1. "We need to make more researches." →
2. "The evidences are clear." →
3. "She contributed in the project." →
4. "The analisys was wrong." →

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. "We need to do/conduct more research." (incontable, no "make")
2. "The evidence is clear." (incontable)
3. "She contributed to the project." (preposicion: to)
4. "The analysis was wrong." (ortografia: analysis)

</details>
