### Section B — Choose the correct word (6 points)

9. "He (allegedly / presumably) stole the money — the trial starts next week."
    - a) allegedly (acusacion no probada, contexto legal)
    - b) presumably

10. "The two proposals are (essentially / inherently) the same — only minor details differ."
    - a) essentially (en esencia)
    - b) inherently

11. "He was (gaslighting / triggering) her — making her believe she imagined the conversation."
    - a) gaslighting (manipular la percepcion)
    - b) triggering

12. "That's pure (conjecture / discourse) — there's absolutely no evidence."
    - a) conjecture (suposicion sin pruebas)
    - b) discourse

13. "She gave a very (pragmatic / rhetoric) response — focused on what we can actually do."
    - a) pragmatic (practico, realista)
    - b) rhetoric

14. "His speech was full of empty (nuance / rhetoric) — lots of words, no substance."
    - a) nuance
    - b) rhetoric (palabras vacias)

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

9. a) allegedly
10. a) essentially
11. a) gaslighting
12. a) conjecture
13. a) pragmatic
14. b) rhetoric

### Section D — Define in English (3 points)

Write a 1-2 sentence definition in English (no Spanish):

20. paradigm: _______
21. gaslighting: _______
22. connotation: _______

<details>
<summary>Respuestas sugeridas</summary>

20. **paradigm** — A model, framework, or way of thinking that shapes how we understand something. "A paradigm shift" means a fundamental change in approach.
21. **gaslighting** — A form of psychological manipulation where someone makes another person doubt their own memory, perception, or sanity.
22. **connotation** — The emotional or cultural feeling that a word carries beyond its literal dictionary definition. For example, "cheap" and "affordable" mean similar things but feel very different.

</details>

### Section E — Explain the difference (3 points)

23. What's the difference between "allegedly" and "supposedly"?
24. What's the difference between "accountability" and "responsibility"?
25. What's the difference between "essentially" and "inherently"?

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

23. **Allegedly** is used in legal/news contexts for unproven accusations ("He allegedly committed fraud"). **Supposedly** is more casual and implies personal doubt ("She's supposedly the best, but I'm not sure").

24. **Responsibility** is your duty or obligation ("It's my responsibility to finish this"). **Accountability** is being answerable to others for your actions ("There's no accountability — nobody faces consequences"). You can have responsibility without accountability.

25. **Essentially** means "at its core, when you simplify it" ("The two plans are essentially the same"). **Inherently** means "by its very nature, as a built-in quality" ("Risk is inherently part of investing"). Something "essentially true" is true when simplified. Something "inherently true" is true by nature and can't be separated.

</details>
