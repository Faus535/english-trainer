# Unit 04 — Cultural Vocabulary

## Objetivo

Aprender 10 palabras que llevan peso cultural en el ingles contemporaneo (especialmente americano). Estas palabras aparecen constantemente en redes sociales, noticias, podcasts y debates. No son "vocabulario politico" — son conceptos que necesitas entender para seguir conversaciones actuales, entender articulos de opinion y participar en debates sin sentirte perdido.

## Palabras

| English | IPA | Spanish | Example | Trampa/Note |
|---------|-----|---------|---------|-------------|
| entitlement | /ɪnˈtaɪ.t̬əl.mənt/ | sentido de merecimiento; derecho | He has such a sense of **entitlement** — he thinks the world owes him everything. | Dos usos: 1) negativo: creer que mereces cosas sin esfuerzo, 2) neutro/legal: derecho a algo (pension, beneficios). |
| privilege | /ˈprɪv.əl.ɪdʒ/ | privilegio | **Privilege** is often invisible to those who have it. | En debate social moderno: ventajas que tienes por tu raza, genero, clase social, etc. "Check your privilege" = reconoce tus ventajas. |
| accountability | /əˌkaʊn.t̬əˈbɪl.ə.t̬i/ | responsabilidad, rendicion de cuentas | There needs to be more **accountability** in government. | Diferente de "responsibility". "Accountability" = que te pidan cuentas por tus acciones. "Hold someone accountable" = pedir cuentas. |
| gaslighting | /ˈɡæs.laɪ.t̬ɪŋ/ | manipulacion psicologica | He's **gaslighting** her — making her doubt her own memory. | Verbo: "to gaslight someone". Manipular a alguien para que dude de su propia percepcion. Viene de la pelicula "Gaslight" (1944). |
| boundaries | /ˈbaʊn.dɚ.iz/ | limites (personales) | You need to set **boundaries** with your boss — working until midnight isn't healthy. | "Set boundaries" = establecer limites. "Respect someone's boundaries" = respetar los limites de alguien. Muy usado en psicologia moderna. |
| self-care | /ˌself ˈker/ | autocuidado | Taking a day off for **self-care** is not selfish — it's necessary. | Actividades deliberadas para cuidar tu salud mental y fisica. Puede ser desde meditar hasta tomar un bano. |
| triggered | /ˈtrɪɡ.ɚd/ | provocado, activado emocionalmente | Loud noises **trigger** her anxiety because of a past trauma. | Uso serio: respuesta a trauma real. Uso coloquial/satirico: "estoy triggered" = me ofendi (a veces usado de forma burlona). "Trigger warning" = aviso de contenido sensible. |
| woke | /woʊk/ | concienciado, despierto socialmente | She considers herself **woke** — she's very aware of social injustice. | Originalmente positivo (consciente de injusticias). Ahora tambien usado negativamente: "too woke" = excesivamente politicamente correcto. Muy polarizado. |
| cancel culture | /ˈkæn.səl ˌkʌl.tʃɚ/ | cultura de la cancelacion | Many celebrities fear **cancel culture** and avoid controversial opinions. | Boicot publico a alguien por algo que dijo o hizo. "Get cancelled" = ser cancelado. Debate activo: es justicia social o censura? |
| virtue signaling | /ˈvɜːr.tʃuː ˌsɪɡ.nəl.ɪŋ/ | postureo moral | Posting about charity without donating is just **virtue signaling**. | Mostrar tus "valores" publicamente sin actuar en consecuencia. Siempre negativo: implica hipocresía. |

## Ejercicios

### Fill in the blank

1. He has an incredible sense of _______ — he expects special treatment without earning it.
2. Many people don't recognize their own _______ because they've never experienced its absence.
3. We need more _______ from politicians — they should be held responsible for their promises.
4. She finally realized her partner was _______ her — making her doubt her own perception of events.
5. Setting healthy _______ with family is one of the hardest things to do.
6. Taking a walk in nature is my favorite form of _______.
7. Fireworks _______ his PTSD from his time in the military.
8. Some people call her _______ because she speaks up about inequality; others think she's going too far.
9. He was a victim of _______ _______ after an old tweet resurfaced.
10. Posting a black square on Instagram without taking real action is _______ _______.

2. privilege
3. accountability

### Translate to English

1. Necesitas establecer limites con tu familia.
2. Hay que pedir cuentas a los politicos por sus decisiones.
3. El la estaba manipulando para que dudara de si misma.
4. El autocuidado no es egoismo — es necesario.
5. Eso no es activismo real — es postureo moral.

### Context and nuance — Is the word being used seriously or critically?

1. "She's very woke — she donates to three charities and volunteers every weekend." → _______ (serious/critical)
2. "They're so woke they got offended by a pizza topping." → _______ (serious/critical)
3. "The article triggered an important conversation about race." → _______ (serious/critical)
4. "He said he was triggered by the font choice." → _______ (serious/critical)

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. serious — uso genuino, describiendo accion real
2. critical — uso satirico/despectivo
3. serious — "trigger" usado en sentido literal (iniciar/provocar)
4. critical — uso exagerado/burlesco

</details>

### Discussion questions (para practicar produccion)

2. Where is the line between setting boundaries and being selfish?
3. Can someone be too woke? Where do you draw the line?
