# Unit 01 — Academic Advanced

## Objetivo

Dominar 10 palabras del registro academico avanzado que encontraras en articulos de investigacion, ensayos universitarios, discursos TED y debates intelectuales. Estas palabras no son "dificiles por ser dificiles" — son herramientas de precision que te permiten expresar ideas complejas con exactitud. Usarlas correctamente es lo que distingue a un hablante C1.

## Palabras

| English | IPA | Spanish | Example | Trampa/Note |
|---------|-----|---------|---------|-------------|
| paradigm | /ˈper.ə.daɪm/ | paradigma | The internet created a new **paradigm** for communication. | Pronunciacion: /ˈper.ə.daɪm/ — la "g" es MUDA. No es /ˈpa.ra.dig.ma/. "Paradigm shift" = cambio de paradigma. |
| discourse | /ˈdɪs.kɔːrs/ | discurso (academico), debate | The **discourse** around climate change has shifted dramatically. | No es un discurso oral (speech). "Discourse" = el debate/conversacion colectiva sobre un tema en la sociedad. |
| empirical | /ɪmˈpɪr.ɪ.kəl/ | empirico | There is no **empirical** evidence to support that claim. | Basado en observacion y experimentacion, no en teoria. "Empirical data/evidence/research". |
| ubiquitous | /juːˈbɪk.wə.t̬əs/ | ubicuo, omnipresente | Smartphones have become **ubiquitous** in modern life. | Pronunciacion: /juːˈbɪk.wə.t̬əs/. Significa "en todas partes". Mas elegante que "everywhere". |
| dichotomy | /daɪˈkɑː.t̬ə.mi/ | dicotomia | The **dichotomy** between work and life is a false one. | Division en dos partes opuestas. "False dichotomy" = falsa dicotomia (falacia logica muy comun). |
| pragmatic | /præɡˈmæt̬.ɪk/ | pragmatico | We need a **pragmatic** approach, not an idealistic one. | Enfocado en lo practico y lo realista. "Pragmatic" es siempre positivo o neutro en ingles. |
| rhetoric | /ˈret̬.ɚ.ɪk/ | retorica | His speech was full of empty **rhetoric** and no substance. | Puede ser neutro (el arte de persuadir) o negativo (palabras vacias). "Political rhetoric" suele ser negativo. |
| nuance | /ˈnuː.ɑːns/ | matiz | The debate lacks **nuance** — everything is black or white. | Pronunciacion: /ˈnuː.ɑːns/. "Nuanced" (adj) = con matices. "A nuanced argument" = un argumento con matices. |
| caveat | /ˈkæv.i.æt/ | advertencia, salvedad | The study has one major **caveat**: the sample size was small. | Pronunciacion: /ˈkæv.i.æt/ (tres silabas). Condicion o advertencia que limita algo. Muy comun en academico y legal. |
| conjecture | /kənˈdʒek.tʃɚ/ | conjetura, suposicion | That's pure **conjecture** — there's no evidence for it. | Mas formal que "guess". Implica especulacion sin pruebas. Puede ser sustantivo o verbo. |

## Ejercicios

### Fill in the blank

1. The digital revolution has created a completely new _______ for education.
2. The public _______ on immigration has become increasingly polarized.
3. We need _______ evidence, not just theoretical speculation.
4. Social media has become _______ — it's impossible to avoid.
5. The _______ between nature and nurture is oversimplified.
6. Let's take a _______ approach and focus on what we can actually do.
7. The politician's speech was full of _______ but lacked concrete proposals.
8. This issue requires _______ — it's not as simple as "yes" or "no".
9. The report is excellent, but with one _______: the data is from 2019.
10. Without evidence, his theory remains pure _______.

<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. paradigm
2. discourse
3. empirical
4. ubiquitous
5. dichotomy
6. pragmatic
7. rhetoric
8. nuance
9. caveat
10. conjecture

</details>

### Translate to English

1. No hay evidencia empirica que respalde esa teoria.
2. El debate carece de matices — todo es blanco o negro.
3. Los smartphones se han vuelto omnipresentes.
4. Necesitamos un enfoque pragmatico, no idealista.
5. Eso es pura conjetura — no hay datos que lo confirmen.

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. There is no empirical evidence to support that theory.
2. The debate lacks nuance — everything is black or white.
3. Smartphones have become ubiquitous.
4. We need a pragmatic approach, not an idealistic one.
5. That's pure conjecture — there's no data to confirm it.

</details>

### Pronunciation challenge

Write the silent or tricky parts:

1. paradigm → the _______ is silent
2. ubiquitous → stress on syllable _______
3. dichotomy → the "ch" sounds like _______
4. rhetoric → stress on syllable _______
5. caveat → has _______ syllables

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. "g" is silent: /ˈper.ə.daɪm/
2. stress on 2nd syllable: u-BIK-wi-tus
3. "ch" sounds like /k/: di-KOT-o-my
4. stress on 1st syllable: RET-o-rik
5. three syllables: KAV-ee-at

</details>
