# Unit 02 — Sophistication

## Objetivo

Dominar 10 adverbios y expresiones que los hablantes nativos educados usan para sonar matizado, cauteloso y sofisticado. Estas palabras son "hedging tools" — herramientas para suavizar afirmaciones, mostrar que piensas de forma critica y evitar sonar dogmatico. Cuando las uses, sonaras como alguien que piensa antes de hablar.

## Palabras

| English | IPA | Spanish | Example | Trampa/Note |
|---------|-----|---------|---------|-------------|
| allegedly | /əˈledʒ.ɪd.li/ | supuestamente, presuntamente | He **allegedly** stole $2 million from the company. | Usado en noticias y legal. Implica "se dice que, pero no esta probado". Protege de acusaciones de difamacion. |
| presumably | /prɪˈzuː.mə.bli/ | presumiblemente, se supone que | **Presumably**, they'll announce the results tomorrow. | Tu deduccion logica, pero no estas 100% seguro. Mas formal que "I guess". |
| supposedly | /səˈpoʊ.zɪd.li/ | supuestamente | She's **supposedly** the best doctor in the city. | Similar a "allegedly" pero menos legal. Implica ligera duda: "eso dicen, pero yo no estoy tan seguro". |
| remarkably | /rɪˈmɑːr.kə.bli/ | sorprendentemente, notablemente | **Remarkably**, nobody was injured in the accident. | Enfatiza que algo es sorprendente. Mas elegante que "surprisingly". |
| arguably | /ˈɑːr.ɡju.ə.bli/ | posiblemente, podria decirse que | This is **arguably** the best restaurant in the city. | "Se podria argumentar que...". Suaviza una opinion fuerte. Muy util en debates. |
| granted | /ˈɡræn.t̬ɪd/ | es cierto que, vale que | **Granted**, it's expensive, but the quality is worth it. | Concedes un punto antes de contraargumentar. "Granted, ... but..." es la estructura tipica. |
| admittedly | /ədˈmɪt̬.ɪd.li/ | hay que admitir que | **Admittedly**, I could have handled the situation better. | Reconoces algo desfavorable sobre ti o tu argumento. Muestra honestidad intelectual. |
| essentially | /ɪˈsen.ʃəl.i/ | esencialmente, basicamente | The two plans are **essentially** the same. | Mas formal que "basically". Usa "essentially" en escritura y presentaciones, "basically" en conversacion. |
| fundamentally | /ˌfʌn.dəˈmen.t̬əl.i/ | fundamentalmente | I **fundamentally** disagree with that approach. | Mas fuerte que "essentially". Implica "en lo mas profundo, en la base". "Fundamentally different" = radicalmente diferente. |
| inherently | /ɪnˈhɪr.ənt.li/ | inherentemente, por naturaleza | Technology is not **inherently** good or bad. | Algo que es asi por naturaleza, no por circunstancias. "Inherently dangerous" = peligroso por naturaleza. |

## Ejercicios

### Fill in the blank

1. The suspect _______ attacked the victim at midnight — the trial is still ongoing.
2. _______, they'll finish the project by Friday, but I'm not 100% sure.
3. She's _______ the most talented singer of her generation — many would agree.
4. _______, the plan has some flaws, but overall it's solid.
5. _______, I made mistakes, but I learned from them.
6. The two proposals are _______ identical — only minor details differ.
7. I _______ disagree with the decision — it goes against our core values.
8. Social media is not _______ harmful, but it can be misused.
9. _______, nobody expected the team to win the championship.
10. He's _______ a genius, according to his colleagues — I've never met him.

### Translate to English

1. Supuestamente, el fue quien robo el dinero (contexto legal).
2. Este es, posiblemente, el mejor libro del ano.
3. Es cierto que es caro, pero merece la pena.
4. Los dos planes son esencialmente iguales.
5. La tecnologia no es inherentemente mala.

4. The two plans are essentially the same.

### Nuance check — Choose the best word

1. Context: news report about a crime. "The man _______ robbed three banks."
   - a) allegedly b) supposedly c) presumably

2. Context: you're making an argument. "This is _______ the biggest challenge we face."
   - a) remarkably b) arguably c) admittedly

3. Context: you're conceding a point. "_______, I was wrong about the deadline."
   - a) Granted b) Admittedly c) Essentially

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. a) allegedly (contexto legal/noticias)
2. b) arguably (suaviza una opinion fuerte)
3. b) Admittedly (reconoces tu error) — "Granted" tambien podria funcionar si le sigue un "but"

### Build sophisticated sentences

Rewrite these simple sentences using the word in parentheses:
