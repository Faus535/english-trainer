# Unit 07 — Vocabulario Figurativo: Metaforas y Analogias

## Objetivo

Dominar las metaforas y analogias mas comunes del ingles en contextos de negocios, ciencia y vida cotidiana. El ingles nativo esta LLENO de lenguaje figurativo — los hablantes C1 no solo entienden estas expresiones, sino que las usan activamente para comunicar ideas complejas con precision e impacto. Sin este vocabulario, te pierdes capas enteras de significado.

## Contenido

### Metaforas cotidianas imprescindibles

| Metafora | Significado literal | Significado real | Equivalente en espanol | Ejemplo |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| tip of the iceberg | la punta del iceberg | solo la parte visible de un problema mayor | la punta del iceberg | "The complaints we've received are just the **tip of the iceberg**." |
| elephant in the room | el elefante en la habitacion | tema obvio que nadie quiere mencionar | el elefante en la habitacion | "Let's address the **elephant in the room** — we're losing money." |
| the ball is in your court | la pelota esta en tu campo | es tu turno de actuar/decidir | la pelota esta en tu tejado | "I've made my offer. **The ball is in your court**." |
| a slippery slope | una pendiente resbaladiza | una accion que lleva a consecuencias cada vez peores | una pendiente peligrosa | "Allowing exceptions is **a slippery slope**." |
| the last straw | la ultima pajita | lo que finalmente colma la paciencia | la gota que colma el vaso | "Being late again was **the last straw** — she quit." |
| a double-edged sword | una espada de doble filo | algo con ventajas y desventajas | un arma de doble filo | "Social media is **a double-edged sword** for businesses." |
| barking up the wrong tree | ladrar al arbol equivocado | estar equivocado en tu enfoque | ir por mal camino | "If you think I did it, you're **barking up the wrong tree**." |
| burning bridges | quemar puentes | destruir relaciones de forma permanente | quemar las naves | "Don't resign angrily — you'd be **burning bridges**." |
| light at the end of the tunnel | luz al final del tunel | esperanza despues de un periodo dificil | luz al final del tunel | "After months of losses, we're seeing **light at the end of the tunnel**." |
| a blessing in disguise | una bendicion disfrazada | algo malo que resulta ser bueno | no hay mal que por bien no venga | "Losing that job was **a blessing in disguise** — I found a better one." |

### Metaforas de negocios y trabajo

| Metafora | Significado | Ejemplo |
|---|---|---|
| move the needle | causar un impacto significativo | "Will this campaign actually **move the needle** on sales?" |
| raise the bar | elevar el estandar | "Their new product has **raised the bar** for the industry." |
| hit the ground running | empezar con energia desde el primer dia | "We need someone who can **hit the ground running**." |
| back to square one | volver al punto de partida | "The deal fell through — we're **back to square one**." |
| the bottom line | lo esencial / la conclusion | "**The bottom line** is we can't afford it." |
| think outside the box | pensar de forma creativa | "We need to **think outside the box** on this one." |
| low-hanging fruit | lo facil de conseguir | "Let's start with the **low-hanging fruit** before tackling the big issues." |
| a level playing field | condiciones iguales para todos | "We need **a level playing field** for all competitors." |
| the big picture | la vision general | "Don't get lost in details — focus on **the big picture**." |
| cut corners | tomar atajos (con implicacion negativa) | "They **cut corners** on safety, and now people are paying the price." |

### Metaforas de ciencia y tecnologia

| Metafora | Significado | Ejemplo |
|---|---|---|
| a watershed moment | momento decisivo / punto de inflexion | "The invention of the internet was **a watershed moment**." |
| the tipping point | el punto de inflexion | "We've reached **the tipping point** for renewable energy." |
| a catalyst for change | algo que acelera el cambio | "The pandemic was **a catalyst for** remote work." |
| a domino effect | efecto en cadena | "One factory closing created **a domino effect** across the supply chain." |
| a litmus test | prueba definitiva | "This election will be **a litmus test** for public opinion." |
| under the radar | sin ser detectado | "The issue flew **under the radar** for years." |

### Analogias comunes para explicar ideas

Las analogias comparan dos cosas diferentes para hacer una idea mas clara.

| Analogia | Significado / Uso | Ejemplo |
|---|---|---|
| It's like trying to boil the ocean | Intentar algo imposiblemente grande | "Fixing every bug at once? That's like **trying to boil the ocean**." |
| It's like rearranging deck chairs on the Titanic | Hacer cambios inutiles ante un desastre | "Changing the logo while sales crash is like **rearranging deck chairs on the Titanic**." |
| It's like comparing apples and oranges | Comparar dos cosas que no son comparables | "Comparing our startup to Google is like **comparing apples and oranges**." |
| It's a marathon, not a sprint | Requiere esfuerzo sostenido, no rapido | "Learning a language is **a marathon, not a sprint**." |
| It's the tip of the iceberg | Solo ves una pequena parte | "These numbers are just **the tip of the iceberg**." |
| You can't put the genie back in the bottle | No puedes revertir algo ya hecho | "AI is here to stay — you **can't put the genie back in the bottle**." |

### Ejercicio 1: Empareja la metafora con la situacion

1. Tu equipo intenta resolver un problema enorme sin plan → ___
2. Tu jefe ignora un problema grave que todos ven → ___
3. Las quejas de los clientes son solo una parte del problema → ___
4. Perdiste tu trabajo pero encontraste uno mejor → ___
5. Un competidor ha establecido un nuevo estandar de calidad → ___
6. Necesitas resultados rapidos, no proyectos a largo plazo → ___
7. Cambias de estrategia por completo despues de un fracaso → ___
8. Tu decision tiene tanto ventajas como desventajas → ___

a) tip of the iceberg, b) elephant in the room, c) boil the ocean, d) blessing in disguise, e) raised the bar, f) low-hanging fruit, g) back to square one, h) double-edged sword

</details>

### Ejercicio 5: Detecta la metafora

En cada frase, identifica la metafora y explica su significado:

1. "The new regulations are a double-edged sword for small businesses."
2. "We need to stop rearranging deck chairs and address the real problem."
3. "His resignation was the catalyst that brought all the other issues to light."
4. "You're barking up the wrong tree if you think marketing is the problem."
5. "Let's not cut corners on this project — quality matters."

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. double-edged sword = tiene ventajas y desventajas para pequenas empresas
2. rearranging deck chairs (on the Titanic) = haciendo cambios superficiales en vez de resolver el problema real
3. catalyst = su renuncia acelero/provoco que salieran otros problemas
4. barking up the wrong tree = estas equivocado en tu diagnostico del problema
5. cut corners = tomar atajos que comprometen la calidad

</details>
