# Unit 08 — Phrasal Verbs 1

## Objetivo

Activar 10 phrasal verbs esenciales que entiendes al leer pero que sustituyes por verbos simples (o directamente evitas) cuando hablas. Los phrasal verbs son lo que separa el ingles de libro del ingles real. Un nativo usa docenas al dia — tu necesitas al menos estos 10.

## Palabras

| English | IPA | Spanish | Example |
|---------|-----|---------|---------|
| look up | /lʊk ʌp/ | buscar (informacion) | I don't know what this word means. Let me **look it up** in the dictionary. |
| figure out | /ˈfɪɡ.ər aʊt/ | descubrir, resolver, entender | I can't **figure out** how to use this new app. |
| come up with | /kʌm ʌp wɪð/ | ocurrirsele, idear | We need to **come up with** a solution before Friday. |
| give up | /ɡɪv ʌp/ | rendirse, dejar (un habito) | Don't **give up**! You're almost there. |
| put off | /pʊt ɒf/ | posponer, aplazar | Stop **putting off** your homework. Do it now. |
| turn down | /tɜːrn daʊn/ | rechazar | She **turned down** the job offer because the salary was too low. |
| set up | /set ʌp/ | configurar, montar, organizar | Can you help me **set up** my new laptop? |
| break down | /breɪk daʊn/ | averiarse, descomponerse | My car **broke down** on the highway. I had to call a tow truck. |
| run out of | /rʌn aʊt ɒv/ | quedarse sin | We've **run out of** milk. Can you buy some on your way home? |
| get along with | /ɡet əˈlɒŋ wɪð/ | llevarse bien con | I **get along with** most of my colleagues, except one. |

### Trampas y notas

- **Separables vs. inseparables**: Algunos phrasal verbs permiten meter el objeto en medio. "Look it up" o "look up the word" — ambos correctos. Pero con pronombres, SIEMPRE en medio: "look IT up" (nunca "look up it"). Los inseparables nunca se separan: "run out of milk" (nunca "run milk out of").
- **give up**: Dos usos. 1) Rendirse: "I give up. This is too hard." 2) Dejar un habito: "She gave up smoking last year."
- **put off** vs. **postpone**: Mismo significado, pero "put off" es informal/oral y "postpone" es formal/escrito. En conversacion, usa "put off."
- **turn down** vs. **reject**: "Turn down" es mas suave y comun en conversacion. "She turned down my invitation" suena mas natural que "She rejected my invitation."
- **figure out**: No tiene equivalente exacto en espanol. Mezcla "descubrir", "entender" y "resolver". "I figured out the answer" / "I can't figure out why she's angry."
- **break down**: Tambien puede significar "derrumbarse emocionalmente": "She broke down and cried."

## Ejercicios

### Fill in the blank

1. I don't know this word. Let me _______ it _______ online.
2. Can you help me _______ _______ the projector for the presentation?
3. My washing machine _______ _______ last week. I need to buy a new one.
4. We've _______ _______ _______ coffee. Can you get some from the shop?
5. She _______ _______ the promotion because she didn't want to relocate.
6. I can't _______ _______ why this code isn't working.
7. He decided to _______ _______ sugar for a month as a challenge.
8. Stop _______ it _______! The deadline is tomorrow.
9. She _______ _______ _______ everyone at the office. She's very friendly.
10. We need to _______ _______ _______ a name for the new product.
