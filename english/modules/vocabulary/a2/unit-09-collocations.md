# Unit 09 — Collocations

## Objetivo

Activar 10 colocaciones (combinaciones fijas de palabras) que son la FIRMA de un hablante fluido. Un hispanohablante dice "do a decision" (traduccion literal de "tomar una decision"), pero un nativo dice "make a decision." Estas combinaciones no siguen logica — hay que memorizarlas.

## Palabras

| English | IPA | Spanish | Example |
|---------|-----|---------|---------|
| make a decision | /meɪk ə dɪˈsɪʒ.ən/ | tomar una decision | We need to **make a decision** by Friday. We can't wait any longer. |
| take a break | /teɪk ə breɪk/ | tomarse un descanso | You've been working for three hours. **Take a break**. |
| do research | /duː rɪˈsɜːrtʃ/ | investigar, hacer una investigacion | She's **doing research** on climate change at the university. |
| have an argument | /hæv ən ˈɑːr.ɡjə.mənt/ | tener una discusion | They **had an argument** about money again last night. |
| pay attention | /peɪ əˈten.ʃən/ | prestar atencion | **Pay attention**! This is important for the exam. |
| catch a cold | /kætʃ ə koʊld/ | pillar un resfriado | I **caught a cold** from my son. Now the whole family is sick. |
| miss the point | /mɪs ðə pɔɪnt/ | no entender la cuestion, no captar la idea | You're **missing the point**. The problem isn't the price — it's the quality. |
| keep in mind | /kiːp ɪn maɪnd/ | tener en cuenta | **Keep in mind** that the store closes at 8 pm on Sundays. |
| make sense | /meɪk sens/ | tener sentido | This explanation doesn't **make sense**. Can you try again? |
| take advantage | /teɪk ədˈvæn.tɪdʒ/ | aprovechar (algo) / aprovecharse (de alguien) | You should **take advantage of** the sale and buy a new laptop. |

### Trampas y notas

- **make vs. do**: La pesadilla del hispanohablante. Regla general (con excepciones): "MAKE" = crear/producir algo (make a cake, make a decision, make a mistake). "DO" = realizar una actividad (do homework, do research, do exercise). Pero hay muchas excepciones — memoriza las colocaciones.
- **have an argument** ≠ "tener un argumento": FALSO AMIGO. "Argument" en ingles = discusion/pelea. Para "argumento" (razon logica) se usa "point" o "reasoning." "That's a good point" (no "a good argument" en conversacion).
- **take advantage OF**: Tiene dos sentidos. 1) Aprovechar algo bueno: "Take advantage of the opportunity." 2) Aprovecharse de alguien (negativo): "He took advantage of her kindness." El contexto decide.
- **catch a cold**: No se dice "get a cold" normalmente (aunque se entiende). La colocacion natural es "catch a cold." Igual que en espanol no dices "obtener un resfriado" sino "pillar un resfriado."
- **pay attention TO**: Siempre con "to." "Pay attention to the teacher" (no "pay attention the teacher").

## Ejercicios

### Fill in the blank (usa la colocacion completa)

1. Stop checking your phone and _______ _______ to the meeting!
2. I _______ a _______ after walking in the rain without a jacket.
3. Let's _______ a _______ and get some coffee. We've been working since 8 am.
4. Before you sign the contract, _______ in _______ that there's a cancellation fee.
5. It doesn't _______ _______ to buy a new car when you can barely afford the rent.
6. We need to _______ a _______ about where to go on vacation.
7. The university is _______ _______ on the effects of social media on teenagers.
8. They _______ an _______ about politics at dinner. It got very heated.
9. You should _______ _______ of the free trial before you subscribe.
10. You're _______ the _______. I'm not saying we should cancel it — I'm saying we should delay it.

## Repaso

| Front | Back |
|-------|------|
| tomar una decision | **make a decision** — We need to make a decision today. (NOT "do/take a decision") |
| tener sentido | **make sense** — That doesn't make sense to me. |
| prestar atencion | **pay attention (to)** — Pay attention to what she says. |
| pillar un resfriado | **catch a cold** — I caught a cold last weekend. |
| tener en cuenta | **keep in mind** — Keep in mind that it might rain. |
