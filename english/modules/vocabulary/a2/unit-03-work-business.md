# Unit 03 — Work & Business

## Objetivo

Activar 10 palabras de entorno laboral que lees en ofertas de empleo, correos profesionales y LinkedIn, pero que no usas con fluidez cuando hablas de tu trabajo. Si trabajas o has trabajado en un contexto internacional, estas palabras son imprescindibles.

## Palabras

| English | IPA | Spanish | Example |
|---------|-----|---------|---------|
| deadline | /ˈded.laɪn/ | fecha limite, plazo | The **deadline** for the report is next Friday. We can't delay it. |
| appointment | /əˈpɔɪnt.mənt/ | cita | I have a doctor's **appointment** at 3 pm. |
| schedule | /ˈʃed.juːl/ (UK) /ˈsked.juːl/ (US) | horario, agenda, programar | What does your **schedule** look like this week? |
| budget | /ˈbʌdʒ.ɪt/ | presupuesto | We don't have enough **budget** for that project this quarter. |
| salary | /ˈsæl.ər.i/ | salario, sueldo | The **salary** they're offering is quite competitive. |
| interview | /ˈɪn.tər.vjuː/ | entrevista | I have a job **interview** tomorrow morning. I'm nervous. |
| experience | /ɪkˈspɪr.i.əns/ | experiencia | You need at least two years of **experience** to apply for this position. |
| opportunity | /ˌɒp.ərˈtjuː.nə.ti/ | oportunidad | This is a great **opportunity** to grow professionally. |
| promote | /prəˈmoʊt/ | ascender, promocionar | She was **promoted** to senior manager after only two years. |
| resign | /rɪˈzaɪn/ | dimitir, renunciar | He **resigned** because he found a better job somewhere else. |

### Trampas y notas

- **schedule**: Dos pronunciaciones validas. UK: /ˈʃed.juːl/ (empieza con "sh"). US: /ˈsked.juːl/ (empieza con "sk"). Ambas son correctas.
- **salary** vs. **wage**: "Salary" = sueldo mensual/anual (profesionales). "Wage" = paga por hora/dia (trabajo manual). "She earns a good salary" vs. "The minimum wage is $15/hour."
- **resign** /rɪˈzaɪn/: Ojo con la pronunciacion. La "g" es muda. Suena como "ri-ZAIN", no "re-sig-n".
- **experience**: Es INCONTABLE en ingles cuando hablas de experiencia laboral. NO se dice "an experience" para experiencia profesional. "I have a lot of experience" (no "many experiences"). Pero si puedes decir "It was a great experience" para una vivencia concreta.
- **appointment** vs. **meeting**: "Appointment" = cita programada (medico, dentista, banco). "Meeting" = reunion de trabajo. No dices "I have an appointment with my team."

## Ejercicios

### Fill in the blank

1. The _______ for submitting the proposal is December 31st.
2. I need to _______ a meeting with the client for next week.
3. The company _______ is tight this year. We can't hire more people.
4. What _______ do you expect for this position?
5. She has ten years of _______ in digital marketing.
6. I have a dentist's _______ at 10 am, so I'll be late.
7. He was _______ to director after five years with the company.
8. This is a unique _______ to work abroad. Don't miss it.
9. My first job _______ was terrifying. I couldn't stop shaking.
10. She _______ from her position after a disagreement with the board.

2. schedule
3. budget
4. salary
5. experience
6. appointment

### Translate to English

1. La fecha limite es manana y todavia no he terminado.
2. Tengo una entrevista de trabajo la semana que viene.
3. El presupuesto no es suficiente para contratar a otra persona.
4. Dimiti porque no habia oportunidades de ascenso.
5. Necesitas mas experiencia antes de solicitar ese puesto.

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. The deadline is tomorrow and I still haven't finished.
2. I have a job interview next week.
3. The budget isn't enough to hire another person.
4. I resigned because there were no opportunities for promotion.
5. You need more experience before applying for that position.

</details>
