# Unit 04 — Opinion & Debate

## Objetivo

Activar 10 verbos para expresar opiniones, acuerdos y desacuerdos. En espanol dominas los matices de una discusion, pero en ingles te limitas a "I think" y "yes/no". Estas palabras te permiten participar en conversaciones reales con precision.

## Palabras

| English | IPA | Spanish | Example |
|---------|-----|---------|---------|
| agree | /əˈɡriː/ | estar de acuerdo | I completely **agree** with you. That's exactly what I think. |
| disagree | /ˌdɪs.əˈɡriː/ | no estar de acuerdo | I **disagree**. I don't think that's the best approach. |
| suggest | /səˈdʒest/ | sugerir | I **suggest** we postpone the meeting until next week. |
| recommend | /ˌrek.əˈmend/ | recomendar | I **recommend** the fish. It's the best dish on the menu. |
| claim | /kleɪm/ | afirmar, asegurar | He **claims** he was at home all night, but nobody believes him. |
| argue | /ˈɑːr.ɡjuː/ | discutir, argumentar | They always **argue** about money. It's exhausting. |
| admit | /ədˈmɪt/ | admitir, reconocer | I have to **admit**, you were right and I was wrong. |
| deny | /dɪˈnaɪ/ | negar | She **denied** having anything to do with the problem. |
| blame | /bleɪm/ | culpar | Don't **blame** me! It wasn't my fault. |
| praise | /preɪz/ | elogiar, alabar | The teacher **praised** the students for their excellent work. |

### Trampas y notas

- **suggest/recommend + gerund**: En ingles NO se dice "I suggest to go." Se dice "I suggest going" o "I suggest (that) we go." Error muy comun de hispanohablantes.
- **argue**: Tiene dos significados. 1) Discutir/pelear ("They argue a lot"). 2) Argumentar/defender una idea ("She argued that the policy was unfair"). El contexto decide.
- **claim** vs. **say**: "Claim" implica que no estas seguro de si es verdad. "He claims he's innocent" (pero quiza no lo es). "He says he's tired" (neutral, le crees).
- **deny + gerund**: Se dice "He denied stealing the money" (no "denied to steal"). Igual que con "suggest" y "recommend", usa gerundio.
- **blame + for**: "She blamed him FOR the accident" (no "of" ni "about"). "Don't blame me for this."
- **praise** /preɪz/ vs. **prize** /praɪz/: "Praise" = elogiar. "Prize" = premio. Suenan parecido pero son diferentes.

## Ejercicios

### Fill in the blank

1. I _______ with your proposal. I think it's the best option.
2. I have to _______ that I made a mistake. I should have listened to you.
3. She _______ that the company should invest more in training.
4. The politician _______ any involvement in the scandal.
5. Stop _______ each other! You need to work together.
6. I _______ trying the new Italian restaurant. The pasta is amazing.
7. He _______ that he didn't know about the changes, but I think he did.
8. Don't _______ the team. The problem was poor management.
9. The manager _______ her for finishing the project ahead of schedule.
10. I'm sorry, but I have to _______. I think this plan won't work.

1. agree
2. admit
3. suggested
4. denied
5. arguing (o blaming)
6. recommend
7. claims

9. praised
10. disagree

### Translate to English

1. Sugiero que esperemos hasta manana.
2. Ella niega haber roto la ventana.
3. No me culpes a mi. Fue idea tuya.
4. Admito que me equivoque.
5. El jefe elogio al equipo por su buen trabajo.
