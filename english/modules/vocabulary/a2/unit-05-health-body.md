# Unit 05 — Health & Body

## Objetivo

Activar 10 palabras de salud que reconoces en articulos y folletos medicos pero que no puedes usar cuando necesitas explicar un problema de salud en ingles. Si viajas y te pones enfermo, estas palabras son de supervivencia.

## Palabras

| English | IPA | Spanish | Example |
|---------|-----|---------|---------|
| symptom | /ˈsɪmp.təm/ | sintoma | The main **symptoms** of the flu are fever, cough, and body aches. |
| treatment | /ˈtriːt.mənt/ | tratamiento | The doctor recommended a new **treatment** for her back pain. |
| injury | /ˈɪn.dʒər.i/ | lesion, herida | He suffered a knee **injury** while playing football. |
| fever | /ˈfiː.vər/ | fiebre | She has a high **fever**. Her temperature is 39 degrees. |
| headache | /ˈhed.eɪk/ | dolor de cabeza | I can't concentrate. I've had a **headache** all morning. |
| surgery | /ˈsɜːr.dʒər.i/ | cirugia, operacion | She needs **surgery** on her knee. The operation is next month. |
| prescription | /prɪˈskrɪp.ʃən/ | receta medica | You need a **prescription** from your doctor to buy this medicine. |
| recovery | /rɪˈkʌv.ər.i/ | recuperacion | His **recovery** after the surgery took about six weeks. |
| allergic | /əˈlɜːr.dʒɪk/ | alergico | I'm **allergic** to penicillin. Please note that in my records. |
| swollen | /ˈswoʊ.lən/ | hinchado | My ankle is **swollen**. I think I twisted it. |

### Trampas y notas

- **injury** vs. **wound**: "Injury" = lesion general (deportiva, accidente). "Wound" = herida abierta (corte, bala). "A sports injury" vs. "a bullet wound."
- **surgery**: En UK, "surgery" tambien significa "consulta del medico" ("doctor's surgery"). En US solo significa cirugia/operacion.
- **prescription** vs. **recipe**: FALSO AMIGO. "Prescription" = receta MEDICA. "Recipe" = receta de COCINA. Un error clasico es decir "the doctor gave me a recipe."
- **headache**: Se pronuncia /ˈhed.eɪk/ — "HED-eik". La "ch" suena como /k/, no como "ch" en espanol. Lo mismo pasa con "stomachache" y "toothache."
- **allergic TO**: Se dice "allergic TO something" (no "allergic of" ni "allergic from"). "I'm allergic to peanuts."
- **swollen**: Es el participio de "swell". "My face swelled up" → "My face is swollen." No se dice "My face is swelled."

## Ejercicios

### Fill in the blank

1. What are the _______ of Covid? — Fever, cough, and loss of smell.
2. The _______ for this infection is a course of antibiotics.
3. He had a serious _______ during the match and had to be carried off.
4. I have a terrible _______. Do you have any aspirin?
5. She's having _______ on her shoulder tomorrow. She'll be in the hospital for two days.
6. The doctor wrote a _______ for some painkillers.
7. His _______ was slow, but he's back to normal now.
8. Are you _______ to any medication?
9. My hand is _______. I think I might have broken a finger.
10. She's had a _______ since yesterday. Her temperature won't go down.

<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. symptoms
2. treatment
3. injury
4. headache
5. surgery
6. prescription
7. recovery
8. allergic
9. swollen
10. fever

</details>

### Translate to English

1. Soy alergico a los frutos secos.
2. El medico me dio una receta para unos antibioticos.
3. La recuperacion despues de la cirugia duro tres meses.
4. Tengo el tobillo hinchado. Creo que me lo he torcido.
5. Los sintomas empezaron hace dos dias: fiebre y dolor de cabeza.

3. The recovery after the surgery took three months.
4. My ankle is swollen. I think I've twisted it.
5. The symptoms started two days ago: fever and headache.
