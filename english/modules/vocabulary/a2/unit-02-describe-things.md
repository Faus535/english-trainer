# Unit 02 — Describe Things

## Objetivo

Activar 10 adjetivos descriptivos que lees constantemente en resenas de Amazon, descripciones de hoteles y correos de trabajo, pero que no usas al hablar. Cuando describes algo, probablemente dices "good", "nice" o "bad". Estos adjetivos te dan PRECISION.

## Palabras

| English | IPA | Spanish | Example |
|---------|-----|---------|---------|
| comfortable | /ˈkʌmf.tə.bəl/ | comodo | The hotel room was very **comfortable**. I slept like a baby. |
| available | /əˈveɪ.lə.bəl/ | disponible | Is this table **available**, or is someone sitting here? |
| reliable | /rɪˈlaɪ.ə.bəl/ | fiable, confiable | She's very **reliable**. If she says she'll do it, she will. |
| suitable | /ˈsuː.tə.bəl/ | adecuado, apropiado | This movie isn't **suitable** for children under 12. |
| affordable | /əˈfɔːr.də.bəl/ | asequible, economico | We found an **affordable** apartment near the city center. |
| convenient | /kənˈviː.ni.ənt/ | conveniente, practico | The hotel is in a very **convenient** location, right next to the station. |

| recent | /ˈriː.sənt/ | reciente | Have you seen any good movies **recently**? |

### Trampas y notas

- **comfortable**: Se pronuncia con 3 silabas, NO 4. /ˈkʌmf.tə.bəl/ — "CUMF-tuh-bul". Mucha gente dice "com-FOR-ta-ble" (4 silabas) — es incorrecto.
- **convenient** ≠ "conveniente" (en el sentido espanol de "buena idea"): En ingles, "convenient" significa PRACTICO, facil de usar/acceder. "It's convenient to live near the office" = es practico. Para "conveniente" como "buena idea", usa "advisable" o "a good idea".
- **available**: Funciona para cosas Y personas. "Is the doctor available?" = ¿Esta disponible el doctor? "Is this seat available?" = ¿Esta libre este asiento?
- **affordable** vs. **cheap**: "Affordable" = tiene buen precio, puedes permitirtelo (positivo). "Cheap" = barato (puede sonar negativo, de mala calidad).
- **suitable** vs. **appropriate**: Son muy similares. "Suitable" = adecuado para un proposito. "Appropriate" = adecuado socialmente. "Suitable clothes for hiking" vs. "appropriate language for a meeting."

## Ejercicios

### Fill in the blank

1. This chair is really _______. I could sit here all day.
2. Sorry, that date isn't _______. Can we meet next week instead?
3. She's the most _______ person on the team. She never misses a deadline.
4. Is this restaurant _______ for a business dinner?
5. We need to find an _______ hotel. We don't have a big budget.
6. Living near the metro is very _______. I don't need a car.
7. Water is _______ for life. You can't survive without it.
8. It's _______ that she's tired. She's been working 12-hour days.
9. In my _______ apartment, I didn't have a balcony.
10. A _______ study shows that remote work increases productivity.
