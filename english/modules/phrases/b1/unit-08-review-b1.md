# Unit 08 — B1 Review: Role-Play Scenarios

## Objetivo

Poner en práctica TODO lo aprendido en B1. Cinco escenarios realistas donde necesitas combinar argumentación, acuerdo/desacuerdo, idioms, lenguaje de reuniones, expresiones discursivas, emociones y negociación. Esto no es un test — es un simulacro de vida real. Hazlo en voz alta.

## Escenario 1 — Debate en una reunión de trabajo

**Situación:** Tu equipo quiere lanzar un producto el mes que viene. Tú crees que no está listo.

**Tu rol:** Argumenta tu posición, escucha al otro, negocia un compromiso.

> **Manager:** OK, let's get started. I think we should launch next month. The market is ready.
> **You:** I'd like to raise a point. The thing is, we still have bugs in the payment system. I'm afraid that's not acceptable for a launch.
> **Manager:** The bugs are minor. We can fix them after launch.
> **You:** I see where you're coming from, but my point is that customers won't come back if their first experience is broken.
> **Manager:** So what do you suggest?
> **You:** How about a compromise? What if we launch a beta version to a small group first? That works for me, and it reduces the risk.
> **Manager:** I can live with that. Moving on to marketing...

**Practica:** Haz este diálogo en voz alta. Luego invierte los roles — defiende el lanzamiento temprano.

## Escenario 2 — Negociando un trato

**Situación:** Estás negociando el precio de un servicio con un proveedor.

**Tu rol:** Pide un descuento, rechaza la primera oferta, llega a un acuerdo.

> **Supplier:** The annual license is €5,000.
> **You:** Would it be possible to get a better rate? We're a small company.
> **Supplier:** I could do €4,500.
> **You:** To be honest, that's still above our budget. The bottom line is, we can't go above €4,000.
> **Supplier:** I'm afraid that's not acceptable on our end.
> **You:** Let's meet halfway. What if we sign for two years at €4,200 per year?
> **Supplier:** Hmm. Fair enough. I can live with that.
> **You:** Great. That works for me.

**Practica:** Repite cambiando los números. Intenta negociar un alquiler, un sueldo, un plazo.

## Escenario 3 — Expresando emociones complejas

**Situación:** Un amigo te cuenta que le despidieron justo después de que le ascendieran.

**Tu rol:** Reacciona con empatía, indignación y apoyo.

> **Friend:** So... they promoted me last month and then fired me yesterday. Budget cuts, they said.
> **You:** You're kidding. I'm gutted for you. That makes my blood boil — how can they do that?
> **Friend:** I know. I'm sick and tired of corporate games.
> **You:** It breaks my heart to hear that. But honestly? It's not the end of the world. You're talented. You'll find something better.
> **Friend:** I hope so.
> **You:** For what it's worth, I think this could be a blessing in disguise. You've been wanting to change for months.

**Practica:** Cuenta una situación que te pasó a ti usando al menos 4 expresiones emocionales.

## Escenario 4 — Anécdota con idioms

**Situación:** Le cuentas a un amigo algo gracioso que te pasó.

**Tu rol:** Usa idioms y expresiones discursivas para que suene natural.

> **You:** OK, so long story short — yesterday I had to give a presentation, and I completely blanked. The boss's name? On the tip of my tongue, but it just wouldn't come out!
> **Friend:** No way!
> **You:** Yeah. I said "our esteemed... person in charge" and everyone just stared at me. It slipped my mind! I'd met him ten times!
> **Friend:** At the end of the day, it's not the end of the world.
> **You:** That's a no-brainer to say NOW. At the time, I wanted to disappear. But you know what? I'm getting the hang of public speaking. It rings a bell, actually — the same thing happened to my boss once.
> **Friend:** See? Everyone forgets names.

**Practica:** Cuenta una anécdota tuya (real o inventada) usando al menos 5 idioms de la unidad 3.

## Escenario 5 — Desacuerdo educado

**Situación:** Un compañero propone algo con lo que no estás de acuerdo.

**Tu rol:** Discrepa sin ofender, ofrece alternativas, llega a un acuerdo.

> **Colleague:** I think we should cancel the Friday meetings. They're pointless.
> **You:** That's debatable. I take your point that they can feel long, but I beg to differ about them being pointless.
> **Colleague:** Nobody pays attention. It's a waste of time.
> **You:** You've got a point about attention. But my point is, without them, nobody knows what anyone else is doing.
> **Colleague:** Fair enough. What do you suggest?
> **You:** What if we make them 15 minutes instead of an hour? In a nutshell, same meeting, less time.
> **Colleague:** I'm on the fence... but OK, let's try it.
> **You:** I couldn't agree more. Let's review in a month.

**Practica:** Piensa en algo en tu trabajo o vida que quieras cambiar. Argumenta a favor y en contra usando las frases de las unidades 1 y 2.
