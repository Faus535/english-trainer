# Unit 06 — B2 Review: Role-Play Scenarios

## Objetivo

Integrar todo lo aprendido en B2: humor y sarcasmo, vocabulario de entretenimiento, jerga corporativa, debate formal y significados ocultos. Cuatro escenarios realistas que combinan todo. Hazlos en voz alta — grábate si puedes. El objetivo no es perfección, es fluidez y naturalidad.

## Escenario 1 — Entrevista de trabajo (con jerga profesional)

**Situación:** Entrevista para un puesto en una empresa tech. El entrevistador usa mucha jerga corporativa. Tú también.

> **Interviewer:** Tell me about a time you had to pivot your strategy.
> **You:** Sure. In my last role, we launched a product that wasn't moving the needle. The evidence suggested our target market was wrong. So I did a deep dive into the data and identified a completely different customer pain point.
> **Interviewer:** What was the low-hanging fruit?
> **You:** We redesigned the onboarding flow — it was confusing. That alone increased retention by 30%. Then we doubled down on content marketing, which we'd been neglecting.
> **Interviewer:** And what did you learn?
> **You:** That it's worth noting when something isn't working, even if the team is emotionally attached to it. It would be premature to keep investing just because you've already started.
> **Interviewer:** Interesting. I'll touch base with the team and circle back to you next week.
> **You:** (thinking: "I'll bear that in mind" — hope that's not a British no!) Sounds great. I look forward to hearing from you.

**Practica:** Responde estas preguntas de entrevista usando al menos 4 expresiones corporativas:
- "What's your biggest professional achievement?"
- "How do you handle failure?"
- "Where do you see yourself in 5 years?"

## Escenario 2 — Conversación sarcástica entre amigos

**Situación:** Tu amigo/a y tú habláis de vuestra semana. Todo ha ido mal. Sarcasmo al máximo.

> **You:** So, how was your week?
> **Friend:** Oh, brilliant. My car broke down on Monday, I got a parking ticket on Tuesday, and my boss told me I need to "be more of a team player."
> **You:** That went well. How convenient that your boss said that right before bonus season.
> **Friend:** You don't say. And on top of everything, I binge-watched a show that everyone said was amazing.
> **You:** Let me guess — overrated?
> **Friend:** Completely. Five hours of my life I'm not getting back. Plot twist: there was no plot.
> **You:** Shocker. My week was great too. I locked myself out of my flat. Twice.
> **Friend:** Fancy that. You'd think after the first time...
> **You:** Tell me about it.

**Practica:** Describe tu peor semana (real o inventada) usando al menos 5 sarcastic expressions y 3 entertainment expressions. Grábate.

## Escenario 3 — Debate formal

**Situación:** Debate en un evento profesional sobre inteligencia artificial en el trabajo.

> **Moderator:** Should companies replace workers with AI?
> **You:** It could be argued that AI will inevitably change the workforce, but it would be premature to assume it means mass unemployment. The evidence suggests that AI augments human work rather than replacing it entirely.
> **Opponent:** With respect, there is a growing body of evidence that AI is already displacing jobs in manufacturing and customer service.
> **You:** While there is merit in that observation, it is worth noting that new technologies have always displaced some jobs while creating others. This raises the question of whether we're investing enough in retraining.
> **Opponent:** That's an interesting point. (genuine or British understatement? You decide.)
> **You:** One might contend that the real issue isn't AI itself, but how we prepare for it. The bottom line is — the technology isn't going away, so the question is what we do about it.

**Practica:** Elige uno de estos temas y argumenta AMBOS lados (2 minutos cada uno) usando registro formal:
- "Social media does more harm than good."
- "University education is no longer necessary."
- "Remote work should be the default."

## Escenario 4 — Decodificando British understatement

**Situación:** Tu jefe/a británico/a te da feedback. Decodifica lo que realmente quiere decir.

> **Boss:** Thanks for the presentation. That was... quite interesting.
> *(Translation: I have concerns.)*
>
> **Boss:** I'm not entirely convinced about the budget projections. They seem a bit optimistic.
> *(Translation: The budget is completely unrealistic.)*
>
> **Boss:** I wonder if perhaps we might consider looking at alternative timelines.
> *(Translation: The timeline is wrong. Change it.)*
>
> **Boss:** It's fine. I'm sure it'll work out.
> *(Translation: I'm worried but I've given up trying to convince you.)*

**Tu respuesta (mostrando que entiendes):**

> **You:** I take your point about the budget. Let me do a deep dive into the numbers and come back with something more realistic. And regarding the timeline — I hear you. I'll circle back with a revised plan by Thursday.
> **Boss:** That would be helpful. Thank you.
> *(Translation: Finally, someone who gets it.)*

**Practica:** Alguien te dice estas frases. ¿Qué quieren decir REALMENTE, y cómo respondes?
1. "That's an interesting approach, but I wonder if there's another way."
2. "I'll bear that in mind."
3. "I'm slightly concerned about the quality."

## Checklist de autoevaluación

Marca lo que puedes hacer con confianza:

- [ ] Usar sarcasmo e identificarlo en conversaciones
- [ ] Hablar de series/películas con vocabulario natural
- [ ] Usar jerga corporativa apropiadamente (y saber cuándo es hueca)
- [ ] Debatir en registro formal con expresiones como "It could be argued..."
- [ ] Decodificar British understatement ("interesting" = disagree, "not bad" = great)
- [ ] Cambiar de registro según el contexto (formal/informal/sarcástico)
- [ ] Combinar todo lo anterior en conversaciones fluidas y naturales

Si has marcado 5 o más, estás listo para C1. Si no, repasa las unidades donde flojeas — especialmente la 5 (subtle meaning), que es la más difícil.
