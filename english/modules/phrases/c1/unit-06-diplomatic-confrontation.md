# Unit 06 — Diplomacia y Confrontacion Indirecta

## Objetivo

Dominar el arte de la confrontacion indirecta en ingles profesional. En el mundo anglosajón, la franqueza directa ("You're wrong", "This is bad") se considera agresiva. Los profesionales usan un sistema sofisticado de suavizadores, redirecciones y escalaciones graduales que permiten decir cosas muy duras sin romper relaciones. Si trabajas en un entorno internacional, necesitas este vocabulario como el agua.

## Frases

| English | Pronunciation hint | Spanish | When to use it |
|---------|--------------------|---------|----------------|
| I see your point, however... | /aɪ siː jɔːr pɔɪnt haʊˈev.ər/ | Veo tu punto, sin embargo... | Desacuerdo suave. Valida antes de contradecir. |
| With all due respect... | /wɪð ɔːl djuː rɪˈspekt/ | Con todo el debido respeto... | Precede un desacuerdo fuerte. Atencion: puede sonar sarcastico si el tono es agresivo. |
| I'm afraid that won't be possible. | /aɪm əˈfreɪd ðæt wəʊnt bi ˈpɒs.ə.bəl/ | Me temo que eso no sera posible. | Rechazo firme pero cortes. "I'm afraid" suaviza mucho. |
| Unfortunately, we're unable to... | /ʌnˈfɔːr.tʃə.nət.li wɪər ʌnˈeɪ.bəl tə/ | Desafortunadamente, no podemos... | Negacion formal. La pasividad ("unable to") reduce el impacto. |
| I regret to inform you that... | /aɪ rɪˈɡret tə ɪnˈfɔːm juː ðæt/ | Lamento informarle que... | Malas noticias formales. Emails oficiales, cartas, despidos. |
| Perhaps we should focus on... | /pəˈhæps wi ʃʊd ˈfəʊ.kəs ɒn/ | Quizas deberiamos centrarnos en... | Redirigir la conversacion diplomaticamente. |
| Let's park that for now. | /lets pɑːrk ðæt fər naʊ/ | Dejemos eso de lado por ahora. | Posponer un tema conflictivo sin rechazarlo. |
| I take your point, but... | /aɪ teɪk jɔːr pɔɪnt bʌt/ | Entiendo tu argumento, pero... | Desacuerdo parcial. Mas fuerte que "I see your point." |
| I must insist that... | /aɪ mʌst ɪnˈsɪst ðæt/ | Debo insistir en que... | Escalacion seria. Usalo solo cuando la diplomacia no funciona. |
| I feel strongly that... | /aɪ fiːl ˈstrɒŋ.li ðæt/ | Siento firmemente que... | Expresar conviccion sin ser agresivo. Personal pero serio. |
| Let's take a step back. | /lets teɪk ə step bæk/ | Demos un paso atras. | De-escalacion. Invitar a ver la situacion global. |
| I think we're all on the same page here. | /aɪ θɪŋk wɪər ɔːl ɒn ðə seɪm peɪdʒ/ | Creo que todos estamos de acuerdo. | De-escalacion. Buscar puntos comunes despues de una discusion. |
| With the greatest respect... | /wɪð ðə ˈɡreɪ.tɪst rɪˈspekt/ | Con el mayor respeto... | Mas formal que "with all due respect". Misma funcion. |
| I appreciate your perspective, but... | /aɪ əˈpriː.ʃi.eɪt jɔːr pə.ˈspek.tɪv bʌt/ | Aprecio tu perspectiva, pero... | Desacuerdo diplomatico. Valida emocionalmente. |
| That's not quite how I see it. | /ðæts nɒt kwaɪt haʊ aɪ siː ɪt/ | No es exactamente como yo lo veo. | Desacuerdo suave sin decir "you're wrong". |
| I wonder if we might consider... | /aɪ ˈwʌn.dər ɪf wi maɪt kənˈsɪd.ər/ | Me pregunto si podriamos considerar... | Sugiere una alternativa sin imponer. Muy britanico. |
| Can we find a middle ground? | /kæn wi faɪnd ə ˈmɪd.əl ɡraʊnd/ | ¿Podemos encontrar un termino medio? | Invitar al compromiso. |
| I hear what you're saying. | /aɪ hɪər wɒt jɔːr ˈseɪ.ɪŋ/ | Escucho lo que dices. | Reconocer sin estar de acuerdo. Ojo: NO significa que estes de acuerdo. |
| Let's not lose sight of the bigger picture. | /lets nɒt luːz saɪt əv ðə ˈbɪɡ.ər ˈpɪk.tʃər/ | No perdamos de vista el panorama general. | Redireccion estrategica hacia los objetivos comunes. |
| I'd like to suggest an alternative approach. | /aɪd laɪk tə səˈdʒest ən ɔːlˈtɜːr.nə.tɪv əˈprəʊtʃ/ | Me gustaria sugerir un enfoque alternativo. | Proponer sin criticar la idea original. |
| This may not be the right time to discuss this. | /ðɪs meɪ nɒt bi ðə raɪt taɪm/ | Quiza este no sea el momento adecuado. | Posponer una discusion que se esta calentando. |
| I think there may be a misunderstanding. | /aɪ θɪŋk ðeər meɪ bi ə ˌmɪs.ʌn.dəˈstæn.dɪŋ/ | Creo que puede haber un malentendido. | Corregir a alguien sin decir que se equivoca. |
| Let's agree on what we do agree on. | /lets əˈɡriː ɒn wɒt wi duː əˈɡriː ɒn/ | Acordemos en lo que si estamos de acuerdo. | Encontrar terreno comun en una discusion dificil. |
| I'd prefer not to go down that road. | /aɪd prɪˈfɜːr nɒt tə ɡəʊ daʊn ðæt rəʊd/ | Preferiria no ir por ahi. | Rechazo de un tema o enfoque sin confrontar. |

## La escala de confrontacion

De mas suave a mas directo:

| Nivel | Frase | Intensidad |
|---|---|---|
| 1 - Sugerencia | I wonder if we might consider another approach. | Muy suave |
| 2 - Desacuerdo suave | That's not quite how I see it. | Suave |
| 3 - Desacuerdo claro | I see your point, however I disagree. | Medio |
| 4 - Desacuerdo firme | I appreciate your perspective, but I must disagree. | Firme |
| 5 - Insistencia | I feel strongly that we need to change course. | Fuerte |
| 6 - Escalacion | I must insist that this is addressed immediately. | Muy fuerte |
| 7 - Ultimatum | I'm afraid this is non-negotiable. | Maximo |

**Error hispanohablante:** Ir directamente al nivel 5-6 sin pasar por los niveles 1-3. En cultura anglosajona, esto se percibe como agresion.

## Diplomacia en emails

### Estructura de un email firme pero diplomatico

| Seccion | Funcion | Ejemplo |
|---|---|---|
| **Apertura** | Tono positivo | "Thank you for your email." / "I appreciate you raising this." |
| **Reconocimiento** | Validar la posicion del otro | "I understand your concern regarding the timeline." |
| **Transicion** | Suavizador + but/however | "However, having reviewed the situation..." |
| **Posicion** | Tu punto, con evidencia | "I believe the current approach is the most viable option." |
| **Alternativa** | Ofrecer solucion | "That said, I'd like to suggest a compromise." |
| **Cierre** | Tono colaborativo | "I'm confident we can find a solution that works for everyone." |

### Email modelo

> Subject: Re: Project Timeline Concerns
>
> Dear Sarah,
>
> Thank you for sharing your concerns about the revised timeline. I understand that the changes have created some uncertainty for your team.
>
> However, having reviewed the resource allocation with the project board, I'm afraid the original deadline is no longer feasible. Unfortunately, we're unable to extend it beyond March 15th due to contractual obligations.
>
> That said, I'd like to suggest an alternative approach: we could prioritize the core deliverables for the March deadline and schedule a second phase for the remaining items. I wonder if this might work for your team.
>
> I'm happy to discuss this further at your convenience. I'm confident we can find a solution that works for everyone.
>
> Best regards,
> Carlos

## Dialogos

### Dialogo 1 — Reunion tensa

> **Client:** I don't think your team has been meeting expectations.
> **You:** I appreciate your honesty, and I hear what you're saying. Could you share some specific examples so we can address them?
> **Client:** The last three deliverables were late.
> **You:** I take your point, and I understand that's frustrating. I think there may have been a misunderstanding about the agreed timelines, though. Let me share the original schedule so we can compare.
> **Client:** Well, regardless, we need things to improve.
> **You:** Absolutely. I feel strongly that we can get back on track. Let's agree on what we do agree on — the quality has been good — and focus on fixing the timing issue. Can we find a middle ground on the deadlines?

### Dialogo 2 — Desacuerdo con un colega

> **Colleague:** I think we should cancel the project entirely.
> **You:** That's not quite how I see it. I see your point about the challenges, however I think there's still value in continuing. Perhaps we should focus on the most profitable elements.
> **Colleague:** But the numbers don't lie.
> **You:** You're right that the numbers are concerning. I'd like to suggest an alternative approach: rather than cancelling, we could pivot. Let's take a step back and look at the bigger picture.
> **Colleague:** I still think we should cut our losses.
> **You:** I understand your position. Let me propose this: give us two more weeks. If the numbers don't improve, I'll support the decision to cancel. Can we find a middle ground there?

## Practica

### A. Ordena de mas suave a mas directo

Pon estas frases en orden (1 = mas suave, 5 = mas directo):

a) "I must insist that we change the supplier."
b) "I wonder if we might consider other suppliers."
c) "I appreciate your loyalty to the supplier, but I disagree with continuing."
d) "That's not quite how I see it."
e) "I'm afraid this is non-negotiable — we must change the supplier."

1. b) I wonder if we might consider... (sugerencia)
2. d) That's not quite how I see it. (desacuerdo suave)
3. c) I appreciate your loyalty, but I disagree. (desacuerdo claro)
4. a) I must insist... (escalacion)
5. e) I'm afraid this is non-negotiable. (ultimatum)

### C. Escribe un email diplomatico

Situacion: Un proveedor ha enviado un producto defectuoso por segunda vez. Escribe un email de 5-6 frases que sea firme pero diplomatico, usando al menos 4 expresiones de esta unidad. Estructura: reconocimiento → problema → consecuencia → solucion → cierre.

### D. Roleplay: Escala gradualmente

Escribe 4 frases sobre el mismo desacuerdo, escalando del nivel 1 al nivel 4:

Tema: Tu colega quiere contratar a un candidato que tu crees que no es adecuado.

1. (Sugerencia suave): ___
2. (Desacuerdo suave): ___
3. (Desacuerdo claro): ___
4. (Insistencia): ___

<details>
<summary>Respuestas sugeridas</summary>

1. I wonder if we might consider looking at a few more candidates before deciding.
2. That's not quite how I see it — I have some reservations about this particular candidate.
3. I appreciate your enthusiasm, but I don't think this candidate is the right fit for the role.
4. I feel strongly that we should not proceed with this hire. I must insist that we reopen the search.

</details>
