# Unit 03 — Rhetoric & Persuasion

## Objetivo

Aprender las técnicas retóricas que usan los mejores oradores del mundo — en TED Talks, discursos políticos, debates y presentaciones. No se trata de manipular, sino de comunicar con impacto. Estas técnicas son las mismas que usaron Churchill, Obama, Steve Jobs y cualquier speaker que te haya dejado con la boca abierta. Ahora las vas a usar tú.

## Técnicas retóricas

### 1. Rhetorical Questions (Preguntas retóricas)

Preguntas que no esperan respuesta — obligan al oyente a pensar.

| English | Pronunciation hint | Effect |
|---------|--------------------|--------|
| Do we really want to live in a world where...? | /duː wiː ˈrɪə.li wɒnt tə lɪv/ | Provoca reflexión. Pone al público de tu lado. |
| What would happen if...? | /wɒt wʊd ˈhæp.ən ɪf/ | Abre posibilidades. Invita a imaginar. |
| Isn't it time we...? | /ˈɪz.ənt ɪt taɪm wiː/ | Urgencia. Implica que ya deberíamos haber actuado. |
| How long are we going to...? | /haʊ lɒŋ ɑːr wiː ˈɡoʊ.ɪŋ tuː/ | Frustración. Presiona para el cambio. |

**Ejemplo:** "How long are we going to pretend that the current system is working? Isn't it time we tried something new?"

### 2. Tricolon (Listas de tres)

El cerebro ama los patrones de tres. Tres adjetivos, tres frases, tres ideas. Suena completo y satisfactorio.

| Technique | Example | Why it works |
|-----------|---------|-------------- |
| Three adjectives | "This project is ambitious, innovative, and necessary." | Ritmo natural. El tercero da el golpe final. |
| Three phrases | "We came, we saw, we conquered." | Memorabilidad. Escala dramática. |
| Three concepts | "Education, opportunity, and determination — that's what changes lives." | Estructurado. Fácil de seguir y recordar. |

**Ejemplo famoso:** "Government of the people, by the people, for the people." — Lincoln

### 3. Anaphora (Repetición al inicio)

Repetir la misma palabra o frase al inicio de frases consecutivas. Crea ritmo, énfasis y emoción.

| Technique | Example |
|-----------|---------|
| Anaphora | "We will fight on the beaches. We will fight on the landing grounds. We will fight in the fields." — Churchill |
| Anaphora | "I have a dream that... I have a dream that... I have a dream that..." — MLK |
| Anaphora moderna | "Every time you ignore the data, you lose money. Every time you skip testing, you lose trust. Every time you cut corners, you lose customers." |

### 4. Understatement for Emphasis (Subestimar para enfatizar)

Decir menos de lo que quieres decir, para que el oyente complete el impacto.

| What you say | What the audience feels |
|-------------|------------------------|
| "It wasn't our best day." | Fue un desastre. |
| "The results were... not insignificant." | Los resultados fueron impresionantes. |
| "I have some concerns." | Estoy muy preocupado/a. |
| "It's a bit of a challenge." | Es un problema enorme. |

## Diálogos

### Diálogo 1 — Mini TED Talk (2 minutos)

> **You:** Do we really want to live in a world where children spend more time on screens than outside? What would happen if we redesigned our cities — not for cars, not for efficiency, but for people?
>
> Imagine parks instead of parking lots. Imagine streets where children play, where neighbors talk, where communities thrive.
>
> The evidence is clear. The findings are consistent. The solution is obvious. Green spaces improve mental health, reduce crime, and build community.
>
> Every time we build another highway, we lose a neighborhood. Every time we prioritize speed over safety, we lose lives. Every time we choose concrete over trees, we lose something we can't get back.
>
> So isn't it time we made a different choice? It's ambitious, yes. It's expensive, yes. But it's necessary. The question isn't whether we can afford to do this — the question is whether we can afford not to.

### Diálogo 2 — Presentación persuasiva en el trabajo

> **You:** How long are we going to use a system that everyone knows is broken? I've talked to the team. I've looked at the data. I've heard the complaints.
>
> The current tool is slow, unreliable, and outdated. Switching isn't a luxury — it's a necessity.
>
> Some might say the timing isn't right. That it's a bit of a challenge. That there are other priorities. But the results from our pilot were... not insignificant. We reduced processing time by 60%.
>
> Every week we delay, we waste 20 hours of productivity. Every month we wait, we fall further behind competitors. Every quarter we postpone, we lose money.
>
> I'm not saying it's easy. I'm saying it's worth it.

## Práctica

### Identifica la técnica

1. "We need clarity, accountability, and results." → _______
2. "What would happen if we invested in people instead of processes?" → _______
3. "Every time we ignore feedback, we lose a customer." → _______
4. "The situation is... not ideal." → _______

<details>
<summary>Respuestas</summary>

1. Tricolon (lista de tres)
2. Rhetorical question
3. Anaphora (repetición de "Every time")
4. Understatement

</details>

### Escribe usando cada técnica

1. **Rhetorical question:** Escribe una pregunta retórica sobre un problema que te importa.
   → _______

2. **Tricolon:** Describe tu trabajo/hobby con tres adjetivos en una frase.
   → _______

3. **Anaphora:** Escribe tres frases que empiecen con la misma palabra/frase.
   → _______

4. **Understatement:** Describe algo impresionante minimizándolo.
   → _______

<details>
<summary>Ejemplos posibles</summary>

1. "How long are we going to accept a healthcare system that puts profit over patients?"
2. "Programming is creative, frustrating, and addictive."
3. "We need better tools. We need better training. We need better leadership."
4. "The project wasn't exactly a failure — we only lost a million euros." (understatement with irony)

### Prepara un mini-speech (2 minutos)

Elige un tema que te apasione y prepara un mini-discurso de 2 minutos que incluya:
- Al menos 1 pregunta retórica
- Al menos 1 tricolon
- Al menos 1 anáfora
- Al menos 1 understatement

Temas sugeridos:
- Por qué todo el mundo debería aprender a programar
- Por qué viajar es la mejor educación
- Por qué tu ciudad necesita cambiar
- Por qué la salud mental importa

Grábate. Escúchate. Repite hasta que suene natural y convincente.

## Repaso

| Front | Back |
|-------|------|
| Pregunta retórica | **"Isn't it time we...?"** — No espera respuesta. Provoca reflexión. |
| Tricolon (lista de 3) | **"Clear, concise, and compelling."** — El cerebro ama los patrones de tres. |
| Anáfora (repetición) | **"Every time we... Every time we... Every time we..."** — Crea ritmo y énfasis. |
| Understatement | **"Not insignificant"** = impresionante. Decir menos para sugerir más. |
| Efecto combinado | Usa las 4 técnicas juntas para un discurso memorable e impactante. |
