# Unit 01 — Small Talk

## Objetivo

Dominar las conversaciones cortas e informales que ocurren constantemente en la vida real: con compañeros, vecinos, en ascensores. El "small talk" es la herramienta social más importante en la cultura anglosajona. Si no lo haces, pareces frío o maleducado.

## Frases

## Diálogos

### Diálogo 1 — En la oficina un lunes

> **Tom:** Morning! How was your weekend?
> **You:** Pretty good, actually! I went to that new Italian place downtown. Have you been?
> **Tom:** No, not yet. Is it any good?
> **You:** Yeah, really good. You should try it. How about you? What did you do?
> **Tom:** Oh, same old, same old. Just stayed home and watched Netflix.
> **You:** Ha, sounds like a perfect weekend to me!

### Diálogo 2 — En el ascensor

> **Neighbor:** Morning! Lovely weather, isn't it?
> **You:** Yeah, finally! Got any plans for the weekend?
> **Neighbor:** Not much, just taking it easy. Maybe go to the park.
> **You:** Nice. Oh, I love your bag, by the way!
> **Neighbor:** Oh, thanks! I got it at that little shop on Main Street.
> **You:** I'll have to check it out. Have a good one!
> **Neighbor:** You too!

## Práctica

### Completa el diálogo

1. — How's it going?
   — _______ complain! How about you?
   — _______ good, actually.

2. — _______ any plans for the _______?
   — Not _______, just _______ it easy.

3. — I _______ your shoes!
   — Thanks, I _______ them at Zara.

2. Got / weekend / much / taking

### Traduce a inglés

1. ¿Qué tal el fin de semana?
2. Bastante bien, la verdad. Fui al cine.
3. Lo de siempre. Me quedé en casa.
4. ¡Me encanta tu camiseta! ¿Dónde la compraste?
5. ¡Qué día tan horrible! (lloviendo)
